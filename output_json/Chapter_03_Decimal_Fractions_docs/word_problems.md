# Word Problems

## Overview
These problems present a narrative involving quantities (like coins or numbers) that must be translated into algebraic equations. The central idea is to assign a variable to an unknown quantity and express all other relationships in the problem in terms of that variable.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Product of two numbers," "How many times greater," "Sum of [amount] is made up of [total] coins."
* **Given:** Relationships between two quantities (e.g., one is $1/5$ of another) or total values (e.g., total sum of money).
* **Asked:** The individual values of the quantities or the ratio between two numbers.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Total Value Equation]
$$
V_{total} = (V_1 \times Q_1) + (V_2 \times Q_2)
$$

**Variables:**
- $V_{total}$ = Total monetary value
- $V_1, V_2$ = Value of each coin type (e.g., $0.10$ for 10P)
- $Q_1, Q_2$ = Quantity of each coin type

**When to use:** When a total sum is composed of two different types of items with known individual values.

**Worked example:** For 80 coins of 10P ($0.10$) and 5P ($0.05$) totaling ₹6.25: $0.10x + 0.05(80-x) = 6.25$.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Define the variable:** Assign $x$ to one of the unknown quantities.
2. **Express the second quantity:** Use the total count or the given relationship to write the second quantity in terms of $x$.
3. **Translate to an equation:** Write the mathematical relationship (product, sum, or ratio) provided in the text.
4. **Solve for $x$:** Use algebraic manipulation (expanding brackets, isolating $x$) to find the value.
5. **Find the second quantity:** Substitute $x$ back into the expression from Step 2.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** For coin problems, use the "All-One-Type" assumption.
* **Why it works:** If all 80 coins were 5P, the total would be $80 \times 0.05 = 4.00$. The difference from the actual total ($6.25 - 4.00 = 2.25$) is caused by the 10P coins.
* **When to use:** When you have a total count and two values.
* **Example:** $2.25 / (0.10 - 0.05) = 2.25 / 0.05 = 45$ coins of 10P.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Mixing units (e.g., using 10 instead of 0.10 for 10 Paise).
    * **Why it happens:** Forgetting that the total is in Rupees.
    * **Fix:** Convert everything to the same unit (Rupees) before starting.
* **Mistake:** Forgetting to take the square root.
    * **Why it happens:** Solving for $x^2$ and stopping there.
    * **Fix:** Always check if the equation results in $x^2$ or $x$.
* **Mistake:** Miscounting decimal places.
    * **Why it happens:** Rushing the multiplication of decimals.
    * **Fix:** Multiply as whole numbers first, then count total decimal places from the factors.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** A sum of ₹6.25 is made up of 80 coins which are either 10P or 5P. How many are there of each kind?

**Solution:**
1. **Define:** Let the number of 10P coins be $x$.
2. **Express:** The number of 5P coins is $(80 - x)$.
3. **Equation:** $0.10x + 0.05(80 - x) = 6.25$.
4. **Solve:** $0.10x + 4 - 0.05x = 6.25 \Rightarrow 0.05x = 2.25 \Rightarrow x = 45$.
5. **Result:** 10P coins = 45; 5P coins = $80 - 45 = 35$.

**Answer:** 45 coins of 10P and 35 coins of 5P.

---

## Similar Patterns

**Ratio/Proportion Problems:** These are distinguished by the presence of a fixed ratio (e.g., "in the ratio 3:2") rather than a total count or product relationship.

---

## Revision Summary

* **Key formula:** $V_{total} = (V_1 \times Q_1) + (V_2 \times Q_2)$.
* **Spot it by:** Narrative involving totals, counts, or products of two unknowns.
* **First move:** Assign $x$ to one unknown and express the other as $(Total - x)$ or $(x/n)$.
* **Fastest method:** Use the "All-One-Type" assumption for coin/mixture problems.
* **Biggest trap:** Mixing Paise and Rupees units.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Algebraic Substitution** — Replacing a phrase like "the other is 1/5 of the first" with $x/5$.
* **Decimal Multiplication** — Multiplying decimals by treating them as integers and placing the decimal point based on the sum of decimal places.

### Formulas You Must Know First
* **Basic Algebra** — $a(b - c) = ab - ac$.
* **Square Roots** — $\sqrt{0.04} = 0.2$ because $0.2 \times 0.2 = 0.04$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **10P/5P** — Denominations of currency; 10P = ₹0.10, 5P = ₹0.05.
* **Product** — The result of multiplication.
