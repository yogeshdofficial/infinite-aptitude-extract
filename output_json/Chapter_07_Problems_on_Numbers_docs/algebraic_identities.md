# Algebraic Identities

## Overview
These questions involve finding an unknown number based on its relationship with its own square, fractions, or other numbers. The central idea is to translate the word problem into a mathematical equation and solve for the variable $x$.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "square of a number," "multiplied by," "lesser than," "difference," "sum of a number and its square."
* **Given:** Relationships between a number and its fractional parts or powers.
* **Asked:** The value of the unknown number ($x$).
* **Structure:** Often involves quadratic expressions ($x^2 + bx + c = 0$) or linear equations with fractions.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Quadratic Equation Form]
$$
ax^2 + bx + c = 0
$$
**Variables:**
- $x$ = the unknown number
- $a, b, c$ = constant coefficients

**When to use:** When the problem states the sum or difference of a number and its square (e.g., $x^2 + x = 182$).

**Worked example:** For $x^2 + x - 182 = 0$, factor into $(x+14)(x-13)=0$. The positive root is $x=13$.

### [Difference of Squares]
$$
x^2 - a^2 = b
$$
**Variables:**
- $x$ = the unknown number
- $a$ = the known number being squared
- $b$ = the resulting difference

**When to use:** When the problem says "the square of a number minus the square of another is [value]."

**Worked example:** $x^2 - 73^2 = 5075 \implies x^2 = 5075 + 5329 = 10404 \implies x = \sqrt{10404} = 102$.

---

## Solution Framework

**Step 1: Assign Variable** — Let the unknown number be $x$.
**Step 2: Translate to Equation** — Convert the word problem into a mathematical expression (e.g., "two-thirds of a number" becomes $\frac{2}{3}x$).
**Step 3: Simplify** — Combine like terms and clear fractions to isolate the variable or the quadratic expression.
**Step 4: Solve** — Use factoring for quadratics or basic arithmetic for linear equations.
**Step 5: Verify** — Ensure the result matches the constraints (e.g., "positive integer").

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** For $x^2 - ax = b$, if $a$ is even, the roots are often near $a/2$.
* **Why it works:** Completing the square $(x - a/2)^2 = b + (a/2)^2$ centers the search.
* **When to use:** When you have a quadratic equation $x^2 - 20x = 96$.
* **Example:** $x^2 - 20x = 96 \implies (x-10)^2 = 96 + 100 = 196$. Thus, $x-10 = 14 \implies x = 24$.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Writing $\frac{2}{3}x = 864$ instead of $\frac{2}{3}x^2 = 864$ when the problem says "multiplied by two-thirds of itself."
* **Why it happens:** Misreading "of itself" as just "of the number."
* **Fix:** Always write "of itself" as an additional factor of $x$.
* **Mistake:** Forgetting to set the quadratic equation to zero before factoring.
* **Why it happens:** Rushing to factor $x^2 + x = 182$ as $x(x+1) = 182$.
* **Fix:** Move all terms to one side: $x^2 + x - 182 = 0$.

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** Twenty times a positive integer is less than its square by 96. What is the integer?

**Solution:**
1. **Assign Variable:** Let the integer be $x$.
2. **Translate:** "Square" is $x^2$. "Twenty times" is $20x$. "Less than by 96" means $x^2 - 20x = 96$.
3. **Simplify:** $x^2 - 20x - 96 = 0$.
4. **Solve:** Find two numbers that multiply to $-96$ and add to $-20$. These are $-24$ and $+4$. So, $(x-24)(x+4) = 0$.
5. **Verify:** $x=24$ or $x=-4$. Since it must be positive, $x=24$.

**Answer:** 24

## Similar Patterns

**Ratio/Proportion Problems:** Distinguish by looking for "ratio" keywords (e.g., "in the ratio 3:2"). This pattern focuses on single-variable algebraic relationships.

---

## Revision Summary

* **Key formula:** $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$ or $x^2 - a^2 = b$.
* **Spot it by:** Phrases like "square of a number" or "multiplied by itself."
* **First move:** Assign $x$ and write the equation exactly as described.
* **Fastest method:** Factoring quadratics by looking for sum/product pairs.
* **Biggest trap:** Forgetting to include the second $x$ when the problem says "of itself."

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Quadratic Factoring** — Finding two numbers that add to $b$ and multiply to $c$ in $x^2 + bx + c$.
* **Square Roots** — Knowing common squares (e.g., $36^2 = 1296$) to solve $x^2 = k$.

### Formulas You Must Know First
$$
(a+b)(a-b) = a^2 - b^2
$$
**What it means:** The product of the sum and difference of two numbers is the difference of their squares.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Integer** — A whole number (can be positive, negative, or zero).
* **Reciprocal** — The value obtained by flipping a fraction (e.g., reciprocal of $x$ is $1/x$).
