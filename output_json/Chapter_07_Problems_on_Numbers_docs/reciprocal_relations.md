# Reciprocal Relations

## Overview
These questions involve a number and its reciprocal ($\frac{1}{x}$) linked by their sum or product. The central idea is to convert the verbal statement into a quadratic equation by multiplying through by the variable $x$ to clear the denominator.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Sum of a number and its reciprocal," "sum of their reciprocals," "reciprocal is equal to."
* **Given:** The sum or a specific ratio involving $x$ and $\frac{1}{x}$.
* **Asked:** The value of the number(s) or a function of the number (e.g., $x \times \sqrt{x}$).
* **Visual:** Look for fractions where the variable appears in the denominator.

---

## Key Formulas

### Reciprocal Sum Identity
$$
\frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} = \frac{x+y}{xy}
$$

**Variables:**
- $x, y$ = the two numbers
- $xy$ = the product of the numbers

**When to use:** When the problem provides the sum of two numbers and the sum of their reciprocals.

**Worked example:** Given $x+y=10$ and $\frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} = \frac{5}{12}$.
Substitute: $\frac{10}{xy} = \frac{5}{12} \Rightarrow 5xy = 120 \Rightarrow xy = 24$.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Define the variable:** Assign $x$ to the unknown number (or $x$ and $y$ for two numbers).
2. **Set up the equation:** Write the expression based on the problem statement (e.g., $x + \frac{1}{x} = \text{value}$).
3. **Clear the denominator:** Multiply the entire equation by $x$ (or $xy$) to eliminate fractions.
4. **Form a quadratic:** Rearrange into $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$.
5. **Solve for $x$:** Factor the quadratic or use the quadratic formula to find the value(s).

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** For $x + \frac{1}{x} = \frac{a}{b}$, the numbers are often the fractions $\frac{a}{b}$ and $\frac{b}{a}$ if $a^2+b^2$ relates to the middle term.
* **Why it works:** The equation $x + \frac{1}{x} = \frac{13}{6}$ is symmetric; if $x = \frac{2}{3}$, then $\frac{1}{x} = \frac{3}{2}$.
* **When to use it:** When the sum is a simple fraction and you need a quick check.
* **Example:** For $x + \frac{1}{x} = \frac{13}{6}$, immediately test $x = \frac{2}{3}$ and $x = \frac{3}{2}$.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Simplifying $\frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y}$ as $\frac{1}{x+y}$.
    * **Why it happens:** Confusing addition of fractions with addition of denominators.
    * **Fix:** Always use the common denominator $xy$ to get $\frac{x+y}{xy}$.
* **Mistake:** Forgetting the second root of the quadratic equation.
    * **Why it happens:** Assuming there is only one "number."
    * **Fix:** Always check if both factors $(x-p)(x-q)=0$ yield valid solutions.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** The sum of a rational number and its reciprocal is $\frac{13}{6}$. Find the number.

**Solution:**
1. **Define:** Let the number be $x$.
2. **Set up:** $x + \frac{1}{x} = \frac{13}{6}$.
3. **Clear denominator:** Multiply by $6x$: $6x^2 + 6 = 13x$.
4. **Form quadratic:** $6x^2 - 13x + 6 = 0$.
5. **Solve:** Factor $6x^2 - 9x - 4x + 6 = 0 \Rightarrow 3x(2x-3) - 2(2x-3) = 0 \Rightarrow (3x-2)(2x-3) = 0$.
   $x = \frac{2}{3}$ or $x = \frac{3}{2}$.

**Answer:** $\frac{2}{3}$ or $\frac{3}{2}$.

## Similar Patterns
* **Quadratic Puzzles:** These are distinct because they involve the specific reciprocal relationship ($x$ and $1/x$), whereas general quadratic puzzles involve arbitrary coefficients.

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} = \frac{x+y}{xy}$.
* **Spot it by:** "Sum of reciprocals" or "number and its reciprocal" phrasing.
* **First move:** Multiply by $x$ or $xy$ to clear denominators.
* **Fastest method:** Factor the resulting quadratic equation.
* **Biggest trap:** Thinking $\frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} = \frac{1}{x+y}$.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Quadratic Equation** — An equation of the form $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$.
* **Factoring** — Splitting the middle term to find roots of a quadratic.
* **Reciprocal** — The inverse of a number $x$ is $\frac{1}{x}$.

### Formulas You Must Know First
$$
(x-y)^2 = (x+y)^2 - 4xy
$$
**What it means:** Relates the difference of two numbers to their sum and product.
**Example:** If $x+y=10$ and $xy=24$, then $(x-y)^2 = 100 - 96 = 4$, so $x-y=2$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Rational Number** — A number that can be expressed as a fraction $\frac{p}{q}$.
* **Cross-multiplication** — Multiplying the numerator of one side by the denominator of the other.
