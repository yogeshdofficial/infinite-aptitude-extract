# Linear Equation Word Problems

## Overview
This pattern involves translating word-based puzzles into a single linear equation with one variable. The central idea is to represent the unknown quantity as $x$ and convert the descriptive phrases into mathematical operations.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "sum of", "exceeds", "one-half", "one-third", "number is".
* **Given:** Relationships between fractions of an unknown number and a specific constant value.
* **Asked:** The value of the unknown number.
* **Structure:** A statement comparing a combination of fractions of $x$ to another fraction of $x$ plus a constant.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Linear Equation Setup]
$$
(\text{Fraction}_1 \cdot x + \text{Fraction}_2 \cdot x) - \text{Fraction}_3 \cdot x = \text{Constant}
$$

**Variables:**
- $x$ = the unknown number
- $\text{Fraction}_n$ = the numerical coefficient (e.g., $\frac{1}{2}$)
- $\text{Constant}$ = the value the expression exceeds or equals

**When to use:** When a problem describes parts of a number relative to each other and a fixed value.

**Worked example:** 
Given: $(\frac{1}{2}x + \frac{1}{5}x) - \frac{1}{3}x = \frac{22}{3}$.
Calculation: $\frac{15x + 6x - 10x}{30} = \frac{22}{3} \implies \frac{11x}{30} = \frac{22}{3} \implies x = 20$.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Define the variable:** Assign $x$ to the unknown number mentioned in the question.
2. **Translate phrases:** Convert "one-half of a number" to $\frac{x}{2}$, "exceeds" to a minus sign ($-$), and "is" to an equals sign ($=$).
3. **Form the equation:** Combine the translated parts into a single algebraic expression.
4. **Find common denominator:** Identify the LCM of the denominators to clear the fractions.
5. **Solve for $x$:** Isolate $x$ by multiplying/dividing both sides to find the final value.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Trick:** Multiply the entire equation by the Least Common Multiple (LCM) of all denominators immediately.
* **Why it works:** It eliminates fractions early, reducing the risk of arithmetic errors during addition/subtraction.
* **When to use:** Whenever the equation involves three or more different denominators.
* **Example:** For $\frac{x}{2} + \frac{x}{5} - \frac{x}{3} = \frac{22}{3}$, multiply by $30$: $15x + 6x - 10x = 220 \implies 11x = 220 \implies x = 20$.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Mistake:** Misinterpreting mixed fractions (e.g., $1\frac{7}{3}$).
* **Why it happens:** Forgetting that $1\frac{7}{3} = 1 + \frac{7}{3} = \frac{10}{3}$.
* **Fix:** Always convert mixed fractions to improper fractions before starting the equation.
* **Mistake:** Incorrectly identifying the "exceeds" relationship.
* **Why it happens:** Confusing "A exceeds B by C" ($A - B = C$) with "B exceeds A by C" ($B - A = C$).
* **Fix:** Always write the larger quantity first in the subtraction.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** If the sum of one-half and one-fifth of a number exceeds one-third of that number by $\frac{22}{3}$, the number is?

**Solution:**
1. **Define:** Let the number be $x$.
2. **Translate:** Sum of one-half and one-fifth is $(\frac{x}{2} + \frac{x}{5})$. Exceeds one-third by $\frac{22}{3}$ means $(\frac{x}{2} + \frac{x}{5}) - \frac{x}{3} = \frac{22}{3}$.
3. **Common Denominator:** LCM of $2, 5, 3$ is $30$.
4. **Equation:** $\frac{15x + 6x - 10x}{30} = \frac{22}{3} \implies \frac{11x}{30} = \frac{22}{3}$.
5. **Solve:** $x = \frac{22}{3} \times \frac{30}{11} = 2 \times 10 = 20$.

**Answer:** $20$

## Similar Patterns
* **Algebraic Word Problems:** These are distinct because they involve a single variable $x$ and direct translation, whereas "Problems on Numbers" (Ex 4-13) often involve two variables ($x$ and $y$) or quadratic equations.

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $(\text{Sum of parts}) - \text{Part} = \text{Difference}$.
* **Spot it by:** Phrases like "one-half of a number" and "exceeds by".
* **First move:** Assign $x$ to the unknown number.
* **Fastest method:** Multiply by the LCM of denominators to clear fractions.
* **Biggest trap:** Miscalculating mixed fractions or the order of subtraction.

---

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Fractions:** Basic addition and subtraction of fractions using a common denominator.
* **Algebraic Manipulation:** Moving terms across the equals sign changes their sign (e.g., $x - 5 = 10 \implies x = 15$).

### Formulas You Must Know First
* **LCM (Least Common Multiple):** The smallest number divisible by all denominators in the equation.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Exceeds:** Indicates a difference or subtraction between two values.
* **Reciprocal:** The inverse of a number (e.g., reciprocal of $x$ is $\frac{1}{x}$).
