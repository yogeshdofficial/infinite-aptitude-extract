# Linear Equations

## Overview
These problems present a "puzzle" where relationships between unknown numbers are described using arithmetic operations. The central idea is to translate the English text into algebraic equations and solve for the variables.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "as much as," "exceeds," "times," "sum," "difference," "reciprocal," "consecutive."
* **Given:** Relationships between numbers (sum, difference, product, or ratios).
* **Asked:** The value of one or more specific numbers.
* **Scan for:** Phrases like "one-third of the one exceeds one-seventh of the other by 8" or "sum of their squares is 113."

---

## Key Formulas

### [Difference of Squares Identity]
$$
(x-y)^2 = (x+y)^2 - 4xy
$$
**Variables:**
- $x, y$ = the two unknown numbers.
**When to use:** When you are given the sum ($x+y$) and the product ($xy$) and need to find the difference ($x-y$).
**Worked example:** Given $x+y=42$ and $xy=437$. $(x-y)^2 = 42^2 - 4(437) = 1764 - 1748 = 16$. Thus, $|x-y| = \sqrt{16} = 4$.

---

## Solution Framework
1. **Assign Variables:** Define the unknown(s) as $x$ (and $y$ if two numbers are involved).
2. **Translate Text:** Convert each sentence into a mathematical equation (e.g., "7 times the number" becomes $7x$).
3. **Simplify:** Combine like terms and move variables to one side of the equation.
4. **Solve:** Use basic arithmetic or the quadratic formula to find the value of $x$.
5. **Verify:** Plug the result back into the original problem statement to ensure it satisfies all conditions.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Trick:** For "A number is as much greater than A as it is less than B," the number is the average: $\frac{A+B}{2}$.
* **Why it works:** It is the midpoint of the two values.
* **Example:** Q1: "As much greater than 36 as less than 86." $\frac{36+86}{2} = \frac{122}{2} = 61$.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Mistake:** Incorrectly translating "exceeds by."
    * **Why:** Confusing the order of subtraction.
    * **Fix:** "A exceeds B by C" means $A - B = C$.
* **Mistake:** Forgetting to distribute the negative sign.
    * **Why:** Writing $-3(184-x)$ as $-552-3x$ instead of $-552+3x$.
    * **Fix:** Always use brackets when multiplying a negative constant into a binomial.
* **Mistake:** Using $(x-y)^2 = (x+y)^2 - 2xy$.
    * **Why:** Confusing the identity with $(x+y)^2 = x^2 + 2xy + y^2$.
    * **Fix:** Remember the difference identity requires $4xy$ to cancel out the $2xy$ term.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** The sum of two numbers is 184. If one-third of the one exceeds one-seventh of the other by 8, find the smaller number.

**Solution:**
1. **Assign Variables:** Let the numbers be $x$ and $(184-x)$.
2. **Translate:** $\frac{x}{3} - \frac{184-x}{7} = 8$.
3. **Simplify:** Multiply by 21 (LCM of 3 and 7): $7x - 3(184-x) = 168$.
4. **Solve:** $7x - 552 + 3x = 168 \Rightarrow 10x = 720 \Rightarrow x = 72$.
5. **Verify:** If $x=72$, the other is $112$. $\frac{72}{3} = 24$ and $\frac{112}{7} = 16$. $24 - 16 = 8$. Correct.

**Answer:** The smaller number is 72.

## Similar Patterns
* **Ratio/Proportion Problems:** These involve multipliers (e.g., $3x, 2x, 5x$) rather than simple sums/differences. Look for the colon (:) symbol.

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $(x-y)^2 = (x+y)^2 - 4xy$.
* **Spot it by:** Phrases like "sum is," "difference is," or "exceeds by."
* **First move:** Assign $x$ to the unknown and write the equation.
* **Fastest method:** Use the average formula for "as much greater as less" problems.
* **Biggest trap:** Forgetting to distribute negative signs in subtraction.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Algebraic Expansion:** $(a-b)^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2$.
* **Linear Equations:** Solving $ax + b = c$ by isolating $x$.
* **Quadratic Factoring:** Finding two numbers that multiply to $ac$ and add to $b$ for $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$.

### Formulas You Must Know First
* **Distributive Property:** $a(b-c) = ab - ac$.
* **Quadratic Formula:** $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Reciprocal:** The inverse of a number, $1/x$.
* **Consecutive:** Numbers following each other in order (e.g., $x, x+1$).
