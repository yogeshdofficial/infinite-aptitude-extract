# Ratio And Proportion — Exam Cheat Sheet

## How To Solve Any Question In This Chapter
1. **Identify the Entity:** Determine if the question involves ages, coins, incomes, mixtures, or abstract variables.
2. **Standardize Units:** Convert all values to the same unit (e.g., paise to rupees, annual to monthly, same weight units).
3. **Define the Constant:** Assign a variable $x$ to the ratio parts (e.g., $3x, 4x$) to represent the actual quantities.
4. **Form the Equation:** Translate the "condition" (e.g., "sum is 210", "ratio becomes 3:5") into an algebraic equation.
5. **Solve for $x$:** Isolate $x$ and substitute back into the ratio parts to find the specific values requested.
6. **Sanity Check:** Verify if the result is positive, matches the ratio proportions, and satisfies the original constraints.

## Formula Bank

**[Age Problems]**
$$ \frac{\text{Age}_1 + t}{\text{Age}_2 + t} = \frac{a}{b} $$
→ *produces: Age after time $t$*
→ *use when: Future/Past age ratios given*

$$ \text{Total Sum} = \text{Average} \times \text{Count} $$
→ *produces: Total age of group*
→ *use when: Average age is provided*

**[Basic Ratio]**
$$ \frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d} \implies ad = bc $$
→ *produces: Missing variable value*
→ *use when: Comparing two ratios*

$$ \frac{1}{x} : \frac{1}{y} : \frac{1}{z} $$
→ *produces: Simplified integer ratio*
→ *use when: Coefficients are equal*

**[Coin Problems]**
$$ \text{Total Value} = \sum (\text{Number} \times \text{Value per coin}) $$
→ *produces: Total amount in currency*
→ *use when: Coin counts/ratios given*

**[Combined Ratio]**
$$ \frac{A}{C} = \frac{A}{B} \times \frac{B}{C} $$
→ *produces: Linked ratio $A:C$*
→ *use when: Chaining multiple ratios*

**[Distribution]**
$$ \text{Share}_i = \frac{\text{Ratio}_i}{\sum \text{Ratio}} \times \text{Total} $$
→ *produces: Individual portion of total*
→ *use when: Dividing a sum*

**[Income and Expenditure]**
$$ \text{Savings} = \text{Income} - \text{Expenditure} $$
→ *produces: Remaining balance*
→ *use when: Calculating savings*

**[Mixture Adjustment]**
$$ \text{Component} = \text{Total} \times \frac{\text{Ratio}_i}{\sum \text{Ratio}} $$
→ *produces: Quantity of specific ingredient*
→ *use when: Splitting a mixture*

**[Proportionality]**
$$ x = \frac{bc}{a} $$
→ *produces: Fourth proportional*
→ *use when: $a:b::c:x$*

$$ x = \sqrt{ab} $$
→ *produces: Mean proportional*
→ *use when: Finding middle term*

**[Quantity Change]**
$$ \frac{x+a}{y+a} = \frac{m}{n} $$
→ *produces: Value of $a$*
→ *use when: Adding to ratio terms*

**[Variation]**
$$ y = kx \text{ (Direct) } | y = \frac{k}{x} \text{ (Inverse)} $$
→ *produces: Proportionality constant $k$*
→ *use when: Variables change together*

## Step Sequences
- **Age:** Define $x$ → Add/Sub time → Cross-multiply → Solve $x$
- **Basic Ratio:** Equate coefficients → Simplify fractions → Find ratio
- **Coin:** Convert to value → Sum parts → Solve $x$ → Multiply
- **Combined:** Align common terms → Multiply ratios → Simplify
- **Distribution:** Sum ratio parts → Divide total → Multiply parts
- **Income/Exp:** Set $I=ax, E=by$ → $I-E=S$ → Solve $x$
- **Mixture:** Find initial quantities → Add/Sub → New ratio
- **Proportionality:** Apply formula ($b^2/a$ or $\sqrt{ab}$) → Calculate
- **Quantity Change:** Set ratio $ax, bx$ → Add/Sub → Solve $x$
- **Variation:** Set $y=kx$ → Find $k$ → Calculate new $y$

## Fastest Tricks
- **Basic Ratio:** Use Componendo & Dividendo for $\frac{P+Q}{P-Q}$ expressions.
- **Combined Ratio:** Multiply ratios directly to link ends ($A/D = A/B \times B/C \times C/D$).
- **Coin Problems:** Convert all to smallest unit (paise) before summing.
- **Variation:** $l \propto r^2$ for constant resistance; square the radius ratio.
- **Age:** Difference in ages remains constant; use this to find $x$.

---

## Trap Watch
- **Age:** Adding time to only one person → Add to both.
- **Coin:** Confusing coin count with coin value → Convert to value.
- **Combined:** Forgetting to invert ratios (e.g., $d:c$ to $c:d$) → Check order.
- **Mixture:** Adding to total volume incorrectly → Add to specific component.
- **Variation:** Forgetting to square the variable → Check proportionality power.

---

## Last-Minute Reminders
- Always check if the ratio is $A:B$ or $B:A$ before calculating.
- The sum of ratio parts is the denominator for all distribution problems.
- When adding/subtracting from a ratio, the new ratio must be set as an equation.
- Mean proportional is always the square root of the product of two numbers.
- In income problems, always distinguish between percentage of income and percentage of savings.
