# Investment Comparison

## Overview
This pattern involves comparing two different stocks to determine which provides a higher annual return on investment. The central idea is to normalize the investment amount (usually by taking the product of the market prices) to calculate and compare the actual income generated by each.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Which is better investment?", "yields", "stock at", "market value".
* **Given:** Two different stocks with their respective dividend rates and market prices.
* **Asked:** To identify which stock provides a higher return or to find the market value given a specific yield.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Return Percentage]
$$
\text{Return \%} = \frac{\text{Dividend}}{\text{Market Price}} \times 100
$$
**Variables:**
- Dividend = Annual income per share (Face Value $\times$ Dividend Rate)
- Market Price = The price at which the stock is bought

**When to use:** When asked for the percentage interest/yield obtained on a specific investment.

**Worked example:** 16% stock at 128. Dividend = 16, Market Price = 128. Return = $(16/128) \times 100 = 12.5\%$.

### [Market Value from Yield]
$$
\text{Market Value} = \frac{\text{Dividend Rate}}{\text{Yield Rate}} \times \text{Face Value}
$$
**Variables:**
- Dividend Rate = The percentage paid on Face Value
- Yield Rate = The actual return percentage on the investment
- Face Value = Usually ₹100

**When to use:** When the yield is given and you need to find the market price of the stock.

**Worked example:** 6% stock yields 8%. Market Value = $(6/8) \times 100 = 75$.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Identify Face Value:** Assume Face Value (N.V.) = ₹100 unless stated otherwise.
2. **Calculate Dividend:** Find the annual income per share using the dividend rate (e.g., 9% of ₹100 = ₹9).
3. **Normalize Investment:** If comparing two stocks, assume an investment equal to the product of their market prices.
4. **Calculate Income:** Determine the income for each stock based on the normalized investment.
5. **Compare:** The stock yielding the higher income is the better investment.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** To compare two stocks (Rate $R_1$ at Price $P_1$ vs $R_2$ at Price $P_2$), compare the values of $(R_1/P_1)$ and $(R_2/P_2)$.
* **Why it works:** Since the Face Value is constant (₹100), the income per unit of investment is simply the ratio of the dividend to the market price.
* **When to use:** When you only need to know which is better, not the exact income.
* **Example:** 7.5% at 105 vs 6.5% at 94. Compare $(7.5/105) \approx 0.0714$ and $(6.5/94) \approx 0.0691$. The first is higher.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Calculating return percentage on Face Value instead of Market Price.
* **Why it happens:** Confusing the dividend rate (fixed on Face Value) with the yield (variable on Market Price).
* **Fix:** Always divide the dividend by the Market Price to find the actual yield.
* **Mistake:** Assuming Market Value is always ₹100.
* **Why it happens:** Ignoring the "at premium" or "at discount" clauses.
* **Fix:** Market Value = Face Value $\pm$ Premium/Discount.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** Which is better investment? $7\frac{1}{2}\%$ stock at 105 or $6\frac{1}{2}\%$ stock at 94.

**Solution:**
1. **Identify Dividend:** Stock 1 pays ₹7.50; Stock 2 pays ₹6.50.
2. **Normalize Investment:** Let investment = $105 \times 94 = 9870$.
3. **Calculate Income 1:** $(7.5 / 105) \times 9870 = 705$.
4. **Calculate Income 2:** $(6.5 / 94) \times 9870 = 682.50$.
5. **Compare:** $705 > 682.50$.

**Answer:** The $7\frac{1}{2}\%$ stock at 105 is the better investment.

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\text{Yield} = (\text{Dividend} / \text{Market Price}) \times 100$.
* **Spot it by:** "Which is better investment" or "yields" in the question.
* **First move:** Calculate the annual dividend per share (usually on ₹100).
* **Fastest method:** Compare the ratios of $(\text{Dividend Rate} / \text{Market Price})$.
* **Biggest trap:** Calculating yield based on Face Value instead of Market Price.

---

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Percentage:** Understanding how to calculate $x\%$ of a value.
* **Ratios:** Comparing two fractions by cross-multiplication or decimal conversion.

### Formulas You Must Know First
* **Dividend:** $\text{Annual Income} = \text{Face Value} \times (\text{Dividend Rate} / 100)$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Face Value (N.V.):** The printed value of the share (standardized to ₹100).
* **Market Value (M.V.):** The actual price paid to buy the share in the market.
* **Dividend:** The annual profit paid to the shareholder based on Face Value.
* **Yield:** The actual percentage return on the money invested.
