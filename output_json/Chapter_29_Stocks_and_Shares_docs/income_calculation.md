# Income Calculation

## Overview
This pattern involves calculating the annual return (dividend) generated by an investment in stocks or debentures. The central idea is that **dividends are always calculated on the Face Value (Nominal Value)** of the stock, regardless of the market price paid.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Annual income," "dividend," "investing in," "stock," "debentures."
* **Given:** Face Value (FV), Dividend Rate (%), and either the total stock amount or the total investment amount (Market Price).
* **Asked:** Total annual income or the rate of interest on the investment.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Income from Stock Amount]
$$
\text{Income} = \text{Face Value} \times \frac{\text{Dividend Rate}}{100}
$$
**Variables:** FV = Total face value of shares held; Dividend Rate = % declared by the company.
**When to use:** When the total face value of the stock held is directly given.
**Worked example:** For ₹ 2500, 8% stock: $2500 \times \frac{8}{100} = 200$.

### [Income from Investment Amount]
$$
\text{Income} = \text{Investment} \times \frac{\text{Dividend Rate}}{\text{Market Price}}
$$
**Variables:** Investment = Total money spent; Market Price = Price of one share.
**When to use:** When you are given the total money invested and the market price per share.
**Worked example:** Investing ₹ 6800 in 10% stock at 136: $6800 \times \frac{10}{136} = 500$.

---

## Solution Framework
1. **Identify Face Value:** If the total face value is not given, calculate it using: $\text{Face Value} = \text{Investment} \times \frac{\text{Face Value of 1 share}}{\text{Market Price of 1 share}}$.
2. **Include Costs:** If brokerage or premium exists, add them to the market price to find the true cost per share.
3. **Apply Dividend:** Multiply the total face value by the dividend rate percentage.
4. **Calculate Rate of Interest (if asked):** Divide the total income by the total investment and multiply by 100.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Trick:** Income = $\text{Investment} \times \frac{\text{Dividend Rate}}{\text{Market Price}}$.
* **Why it works:** It combines the conversion of investment to face value and the dividend calculation into one step.
* **When to use:** When you have a single investment amount and a single stock type.
* **Example:** Investing ₹ 6800 in 10% stock at 136: $6800 \times \frac{10}{136} = 500$.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Mistake:** Calculating dividend on the Market Price. **Fix:** Always use Face Value.
* **Mistake:** Forgetting to add brokerage to the cost. **Fix:** Total cost = Market Price + Brokerage.
* **Mistake:** Confusing investment percentage with dividend percentage. **Fix:** Keep the dividend rate tied strictly to the face value.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** Find the annual income derived by investing ₹ 6800 in 10% stock at 136.

**Solution:**
1. **Identify variables:** Investment = 6800, Dividend Rate = 10%, Market Price = 136.
2. **Find Face Value:** Since 136 buys 100 face value, 6800 buys $\frac{100}{136} \times 6800 = 5000$.
3. **Calculate Income:** $10\% \text{ of } 5000 = \frac{10}{100} \times 5000 = 500$.

**Answer:** ₹ 500.

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\text{Income} = \text{Face Value} \times \frac{\text{Dividend Rate}}{100}$.
* **Spot it by:** Looking for "annual income" and "dividend rate."
* **First move:** Determine the total face value of the shares.
* **Fastest method:** Use $\text{Investment} \times \frac{\text{Dividend Rate}}{\text{Market Price}}$.
* **Biggest trap:** Calculating dividend on the market price instead of face value.

---

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Percentage:** Understanding that $x\%$ of $y$ is $\frac{x}{100} \times y$.
* **Proportion:** If $A$ gives $B$, then $C$ gives $\frac{B}{A} \times C$.

### Formulas You Must Know First
* **Total Cost:** $\text{Number of shares} \times (\text{Market Price} + \text{Brokerage})$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Face Value (FV):** The value printed on the share certificate.
* **Market Price (MP):** The price at which the share is bought/sold in the market.
* **Dividend:** The annual profit paid on the Face Value.
