# Age Relationships

## Overview
These questions define the ages of individuals using ratios, fractions, or differences relative to one another at specific points in time. The central idea is to express all ages as a function of a single variable ($x$) and solve the resulting algebraic equation.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "ratio of ages," "times as old as," "years ago/hence," "difference of ages," "product of ages."
* **Given:** Relationships between two or more people at present or at a specific time in the past/future.
* **Asked:** Present age of one person, or a future ratio of ages.
* **Time markers:** Look for "ago" (subtract) and "hence/after" (add).

---

## Key Formulas

### Age Relation Equation
$$
\text{Age}_A \pm t = k \times (\text{Age}_B \pm t)
$$
**Variables:**
- $\text{Age}_A, \text{Age}_B$ = Present ages of two people.
- $t$ = Time shift (positive for future, negative for past).
- $k$ = The multiplier (e.g., "twice" = 2, "one-half" = 0.5).

**When to use:** When a relationship is given for a time other than the present.

**Worked example:** Man is $\frac{2}{5}$ of mother's age ($x$). After 8 years, he is $\frac{1}{2}$ of her age. Equation: $\frac{2}{5}x + 8 = \frac{1}{2}(x + 8)$. Solving gives $x=40$.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Define the variable:** Assign $x$ to the person whose age is the reference point (usually the one with the simplest relationship).
2. **Express all ages in $x$:** Write every person's age in terms of $x$ based on the given ratios or fractions.
3. **Apply time shifts:** If the problem mentions "years ago" or "years hence," add or subtract that value from the expressions created in Step 2.
4. **Set up the equation:** Use the "multiplier" relationship provided in the problem to equate the expressions.
5. **Solve for $x$:** Perform algebraic simplification to find the value of $x$.
6. **Calculate the target:** Plug $x$ back into the specific expression requested by the question.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** The difference between two people's ages is **always constant**.
* **Why it works:** If A is 5 years older than B today, A will be 5 years older than B in 10 years or 10 years ago.
* **When to use:** When the problem provides an age difference and a ratio at a different time.
* **Example:** Ages differ by 16. 6 years ago, elder was 3x younger. Let younger be $y$. $y+16-6 = 3(y-6) \Rightarrow y+10 = 3y-18 \Rightarrow 2y=28 \Rightarrow y=14$.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Forgetting to add/subtract the time shift to *both* people.
    * **Why it happens:** Focusing only on the person whose age is changing.
    * **Fix:** Always write $(Age_1 \pm t)$ and $(Age_2 \pm t)$ before applying the multiplier.
* **Mistake:** Incorrectly calculating the reciprocal for fractions.
    * **Why it happens:** Confusing "A is $\frac{1}{4}$ of B" with "A is 4 times B."
    * **Fix:** If $A = \frac{1}{4}B$, then $B = 4A$.
* **Mistake:** Solving for $x$ but forgetting to answer the specific question asked.
    * **Why it happens:** Stopping at the first value found.
    * **Fix:** Re-read the final question (e.g., "What is the mother's age?") and ensure the final value corresponds to that person.

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** The ages of A and B are in the ratio 6 : 5 and the sum of their ages is 44 years. What will be the ratio of their ages after 8 years?

**Solution:**
1. **Define variable:** Let the common ratio multiplier be $x$. A's age = $6x$, B's age = $5x$.
2. **Set up equation:** Sum is 44, so $6x + 5x = 44 \Rightarrow 11x = 44$.
3. **Solve for $x$:** $x = 4$.
4. **Find present ages:** A = $6(4) = 24$, B = $5(4) = 20$.
5. **Apply time shift:** After 8 years, A = $24+8 = 32$, B = $20+8 = 28$.
6. **Calculate ratio:** $32 : 28 = 8 : 7$.

**Answer:** 8:7

## Similar Patterns
* **Partnership Problems:** These involve investment and time, whereas Age problems involve only time shifts. Distinguish by checking if the problem mentions "capital/investment" (Partnership) or "years ago/hence" (Age).

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\text{Age}_A \pm t = k \times (\text{Age}_B \pm t)$.
* **Spot it by:** Ratios, fractions, and "years ago/hence" phrases.
* **First move:** Assign $x$ to the reference person and express others in terms of $x$.
* **Fastest method:** Use the constant age difference property for difference-based problems.
* **Biggest trap:** Forgetting to add/subtract the time shift to *both* individuals.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Ratios:** Expressing parts of a whole ($a:b$ means $ax$ and $bx$).
* **Linear Equations:** Solving $ax + b = cx + d$ for $x$.
* **Quadratic Equations:** Solving $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$ using factoring (needed for product-of-ages problems).

### Formulas You Must Know First
$$
\text{Sum of parts} = \text{Total Value}
$$
**What it means:** If a total is divided in ratio $a:b$, one part is $\frac{\text{Total}}{a+b}$.
**Example:** 44 years divided in $6:5$ ratio. $6+5=11$ parts. $44/11 = 4$. Ages are $6 \times 4$ and $5 \times 4$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Hence:** Future time (add years).
* **Ago:** Past time (subtract years).
* **Ratio:** A comparison of two quantities by division.
