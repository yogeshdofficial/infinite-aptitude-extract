# Ratio Evolution

## Overview
This pattern involves problems where the ratio of two people's ages is given at two different points in time (e.g., "present" and "10 years ago"). The central idea is to represent the ages at the first time point using a variable $x$, then express the ages at the second time point by adding or subtracting the time gap to set up a ratio equation.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Ratio of ages," "years ago," "years hence/after," "will be."
* **Given:** Two distinct ratios at two different time points (e.g., "Present ratio is 9:10; 10 years ago it was 4:5").
* **Asked:** Present age, future age, or the ratio of ages at a specific time.
* **Scan for:** Two time-markers (e.g., "10 years ago" and "present" or "10 years hence").

---

## Key Formulas

### Age Ratio Equation
$$
\frac{\text{Age}_1 \pm T}{\text{Age}_2 \pm T} = \frac{R_1}{R_2}
$$

**Variables:**
- $\text{Age}_1, \text{Age}_2$ = Ages at the reference time point (e.g., $9x, 10x$)
- $T$ = Time difference between the two points
- $R_1, R_2$ = Ratio at the second time point

**When to use:** To find the value of $x$ when two ratios are provided.

**Worked example:** Tina and Rakesh are $9x$ and $10x$. 10 years ago, ratio was $4:5$.
$\frac{9x-10}{10x-10} = \frac{4}{5} \Rightarrow 45x - 50 = 40x - 40 \Rightarrow 5x = 10 \Rightarrow x = 2$.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Assign Variables:** Use the first given ratio to define ages as $ax$ and $bx$.
2. **Adjust for Time:** Add or subtract the time gap ($T$) from both $ax$ and $bx$ to reach the second time point.
3. **Set Equation:** Equate the new expressions to the second given ratio.
4. **Solve for $x$:** Cross-multiply and isolate $x$.
5. **Calculate Target:** Substitute $x$ back into the required expression (e.g., $ax + T$ or $bx$).

---

## Shortcut Tricks

**Trick:** The "Cross-Difference" Method.
**Why it works:** It eliminates the need for algebraic expansion by comparing the "units" of the ratio to the actual time gap.
**When to use:** When you have two ratios and a fixed time gap.
**Example:** Ages $9:10$ (present) and $4:5$ (10 years ago).
Difference in units: $(9-4) = 5$ units and $(10-5) = 5$ units.
5 units = 10 years $\Rightarrow$ 1 unit = 2 years. Rakesh (10 units) = $10 \times 2 = 20$.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Adding/subtracting time to only one person.
    * **Fix:** Always apply the time change to both individuals.
* **Mistake:** Confusing "ago" (subtract) with "hence/after" (add).
    * **Fix:** Check the timeline: Past = $-$, Future = $+$.
* **Mistake:** Calculating the wrong person's age.
    * **Fix:** Re-read the final question to ensure you solve for the correct variable.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** The ratio of the ages of Tina and Rakesh is 9 : 10. Ten years ago, the ratio of their ages was 4 : 5. What is the present age of Rakesh?

**Solution:**
1. **Assign Variables:** Tina = $9x$, Rakesh = $10x$.
2. **Adjust for Time:** 10 years ago, Tina = $9x - 10$, Rakesh = $10x - 10$.
3. **Set Equation:** $\frac{9x-10}{10x-10} = \frac{4}{5}$.
4. **Solve for $x$:** $5(9x-10) = 4(10x-10) \Rightarrow 45x - 50 = 40x - 40 \Rightarrow 5x = 10 \Rightarrow x = 2$.
5. **Calculate Target:** Rakesh's present age = $10x = 10(2) = 20$.

**Answer:** 20 years.

---

## Similar Patterns
**Age Difference Constant:** If the problem mentions "the difference in their ages is constant," use the fact that $(ax - bx)$ remains the same regardless of time.

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\frac{ax \pm T}{bx \pm T} = \frac{R_1}{R_2}$
* **Spot it by:** Two ratios at two different time points.
* **First move:** Define present ages as $ax$ and $bx$.
* **Fastest method:** Cross-difference of ratio units.
* **Biggest trap:** Forgetting to add/subtract time from both ages.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Ratios:** Expressing quantities as $ax$ and $bx$.
* **Linear Equations:** Solving $ax + b = cx + d$ for $x$.

### Formulas You Must Know First
* **Age at time $T$:** $\text{Present Age} \pm T$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Hence/After:** Future time.
* **Ago:** Past time.
