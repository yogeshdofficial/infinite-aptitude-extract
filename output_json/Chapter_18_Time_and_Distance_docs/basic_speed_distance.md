# Basic Speed Distance

## Overview
This pattern involves calculating one of three variables—Speed, Distance, or Time—given the other two. The central idea is the fundamental relationship $\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time}$, provided all units are converted to a consistent system before calculation.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "speed of", "distance of", "how many minutes/metres", "travels", "covers".
* **Given:** Two of the three variables (Speed, Distance, Time).
* **Asked:** The missing third variable.
* **Unit Mismatch:** Look for mixed units like "km/hr" vs "metres" or "minutes" vs "seconds".

---

## Key Formulas

### [Basic Relationship]
$$
\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time}
$$
**Variables:**
- $D$ = Total distance covered
- $S$ = Constant speed
- $T$ = Total time taken

**When to use:** When you have speed and time and need the distance.

**Worked example:** Train at $82.6$ km/hr for $15$ min ($0.25$ hr): $82.6 \times 0.25 = 20.65$ km.

### [Unit Conversion]
$$
\text{Speed (m/sec)} = \text{Speed (km/hr)} \times \frac{5}{18}
$$
**Variables:**
- $S_{ms}$ = Speed in metres per second
- $S_{kh}$ = Speed in kilometres per hour

**When to use:** When the question asks for speed in m/sec but provides it in km/hr.

**Worked example:** $20$ km/hr $\times \frac{5}{18} = \frac{50}{9}$ m/sec.

---

## Solution Framework
1. **Standardize Units:** Convert all times to hours or seconds, and all distances to km or metres.
2. **Identify Variables:** Write down the given values and the target variable.
3. **Apply Formula:** Use $D=S \times T$, $S=D/T$, or $T=D/S$.
4. **Final Conversion:** If the answer is required in a specific unit (e.g., m/sec), apply the conversion factor at the very end.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Trick:** To convert km/hr to m/sec, multiply by $5$ then divide by $18$.
* **Why it works:** $1 \text{ km} = 1000 \text{ m}$ and $1 \text{ hr} = 3600 \text{ sec}$. $1000/3600 = 5/18$.
* **When to use:** Every time you see km/hr and need m/sec.
* **Example:** $18$ km/hr $\rightarrow 18 \times (5/18) = 5$ m/sec.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Mistake:** Dividing by $60$ instead of $3600$ when converting hours to seconds.
* **Fix:** Always convert hours to minutes ($ \times 60$) then minutes to seconds ($ \times 60$).
* **Mistake:** Forgetting to convert the final distance from km to metres.
* **Fix:** Check the units of the options/question before finalizing the answer.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** A cyclist covers a distance of $750$ m in $2$ min $30$ sec. What is the speed in km/hr?

**Solution:**
1. **Standardize Units:** $2$ min $30$ sec = $150$ seconds. Distance = $750$ m.
2. **Calculate Speed (m/sec):** $S = \frac{D}{T} = \frac{750}{150} = 5$ m/sec.
3. **Convert to km/hr:** Multiply by $\frac{18}{5}$ to go from m/sec to km/hr.
4. **Final Calculation:** $5 \times \frac{18}{5} = 18$ km/hr.

**Answer:** $18$ km/hr.

---

## Similar Patterns
* **Relative Speed:** Use this when two objects are moving simultaneously. Distinguish by looking for "same direction" (subtract speeds) or "opposite direction" (add speeds).

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time}$.
* **Spot it by:** Looking for two of the three variables (S, D, T).
* **First move:** Convert all units to match (e.g., all to metres and seconds).
* **Fastest method:** Use $5/18$ for km/hr to m/sec conversion.
* **Biggest trap:** Mixing units (e.g., using km/hr with seconds).

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Fractions** — Understanding how to multiply and divide fractions.
* **Algebraic Equations** — Solving for $x$ in linear equations like $12x = 72$.

### Formulas You Must Know First
* $\text{Speed} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}}$
* $\text{Time} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}}$

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Relative Speed** — The speed of one object as observed from another moving object.
* **km/hr** — Kilometres per hour (standard unit for long distances).
* **m/sec** — Metres per second (standard unit for short distances).
