# Time, Speed, and Distance Ratios

## Overview
This pattern covers problems where distance is constant or related through movement, requiring you to relate speed, time, and distance. The central idea is that for a fixed distance, time is inversely proportional to speed, and distance is the product of speed and time.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "same distance," "return journey," "leaps," "ratio of time," "ratio of distance."
* **Given:** Speeds, time differences, or relative leap distances.
* **Asked:** Ratio of times, ratio of distances, or the actual distance covered.
* **Clue:** If you see two speeds for the same distance, think "Inverse Ratio of Time."
* **Clue:** If you see "leaps," think "Distance = (Number of leaps) $\times$ (Distance per leap)."

---

## Key Formulas

### [Inverse Time-Speed Relationship]
$$
\text{Time}_1 : \text{Time}_2 = \frac{1}{S_1} : \frac{1}{S_2} = S_2 : S_1
$$
**Variables:** $S_1, S_2$ = speeds for the same distance.
**When to use:** When the distance is constant and you need the ratio of times.
**Worked example:** $S_1 = 4, S_2 = 6$. Ratio of times = $6 : 4 = 3 : 2$.

### [Distance from Speed and Time Difference]
$$
\text{Distance} = \frac{S_1 \times S_2}{|S_1 - S_2|} \times \Delta T
$$
**Variables:** $S_1, S_2$ = speeds; $\Delta T$ = difference in time taken.
**When to use:** When the same distance is covered at two different speeds with a known time gap.
**Worked example:** $S_1 = 60, S_2 = 40, \Delta T = 2$. Distance = $\frac{60 \times 40}{20} \times 2 = 240$ km.

---

## Solution Framework
1. **Identify the constant:** Determine if distance is fixed (e.g., A to B and back).
2. **Set up the ratio:** If distance is fixed, write the speed ratio and invert it for the time ratio.
3. **Equate leaps:** If "leaps" are involved, express the distance of one entity in terms of the other ($3x = 4y$).
4. **Calculate total distance:** Use $\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time}$ for each entity.
5. **Solve for the unknown:** Use algebraic substitution or cross-multiplication to find the requested value.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Trick:** For constant distance, the ratio of times is simply the speeds swapped.
* **Why it works:** Because $T = \frac{D}{S}$, so $T_1/T_2 = (D/S_1) / (D/S_2) = S_2/S_1$.
* **Example:** Speed ratio $4:6 \rightarrow$ Time ratio $6:4 = 3:2$.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Mistake:** Assuming time ratio is the same as speed ratio.
    * **Fix:** Always invert the speed ratio for time when distance is constant.
* **Mistake:** Confusing "leaps" with "distance."
    * **Fix:** Multiply the number of leaps by the distance per leap to get total distance.
* **Mistake:** Subtracting times in the wrong order.
    * **Fix:** Always use $|S_1 - S_2|$ to ensure the time difference is positive.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** A bus covered a distance from A to B at 60 km/hr and returned at 40 km/hr, taking 2 hours more on the return. Find the distance.

**Solution:**
1. **Identify:** Distance is constant. Speeds are $60$ and $40$. Time difference $\Delta T = 2$.
2. **Formula:** Use $\text{Distance} = \frac{S_1 \times S_2}{|S_1 - S_2|} \times \Delta T$.
3. **Substitute:** $\text{Distance} = \frac{60 \times 40}{60 - 40} \times 2$.
4. **Calculate:** $\frac{2400}{20} \times 2 = 120 \times 2 = 240$.

**Answer:** 240 km.

---

## Similar Patterns
**Relative Speed Problems:** These involve two objects moving simultaneously (not necessarily the same distance). Distinguish by checking if the question asks for "meeting time" or "distance between them" rather than a simple ratio of times for a single journey.

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\text{Time} \propto \frac{1}{\text{Speed}}$ for constant distance.
* **Spot it by:** "Same distance" or "Return journey" keywords.
* **First move:** Write down the ratio of speeds.
* **Fastest method:** Use the $\frac{S_1 \times S_2}{\text{diff}} \times \Delta T$ formula for distance.
* **Biggest trap:** Forgetting to invert the speed ratio when finding time.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Ratios** — Expressing the relationship between two quantities as $a:b$.
* **Inverse Proportion** — When one value increases, the other decreases proportionally ($y = \frac{k}{x}$).
* **LCM** — Least Common Multiple, used to simplify ratios of fractions.

### Formulas You Must Know First
$$
\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time}
$$
**What it means:** The fundamental relationship between movement variables.
**Example:** $60 \text{ km/hr} \times 2 \text{ hours} = 120 \text{ km}$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Leap** — A single jump or stride taken by an animal.
* **Return Journey** — Traveling back over the same path already covered.
