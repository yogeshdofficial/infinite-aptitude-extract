# True Discount — Exam Cheat Sheet

## How To Solve Any Question In This Chapter
1. **Identify Variables:** Extract Amount ($A$), True Discount ($T.D.$), Simple Interest ($S.I.$), Rate ($R$), and Time ($T$). Convert months to years ($T = \frac{m}{12}$).
2. **Determine Relationship:** Check if the question involves comparing $S.I.$ vs $T.D.$, finding a missing variable, or comparing two financial offers.
3. **Select Pattern:** Use the Quick-Recognition Table to map the scenario to a specific pattern.
4. **Apply Formula:** Use the Formula Bank to isolate the unknown variable.
5. **Sanity Check:** Ensure $P.W. < A$ and $T.D. < S.I.$ for the same sum/time/rate.

## Formula Bank

**[Discount Proportionality]**
$$T.D. = \frac{A \times R \times T}{100 + RT}$$
→ *produces: True Discount value*
→ *use when: Calculating discount for varying time*

**[Equivalent Offers]**
$$P.W. = \frac{100 \times A}{100 + RT}$$
→ *produces: Present Worth of credit*
→ *use when: Comparing cash vs credit*

**[Interest Comparison]**
$$Sum = \frac{S.I. \times T.D.}{S.I. - T.D.}$$
→ *produces: Principal sum*
→ *use when: Both S.I. and T.D. known*

$$S.I. - T.D. = \frac{T.D. \times R \times T}{100}$$
→ *produces: Difference between interests*
→ *use when: Finding rate or difference*

**[Missing Variables]**
$$P.W. = A - T.D.$$
→ *produces: Present Worth*
→ *use when: Sum and T.D. known*

$$R = \frac{100 \times T.D.}{P.W. \times T}$$
→ *produces: Rate percent*
→ *use when: Finding interest rate*

$$T = \frac{100 \times T.D.}{P.W. \times R}$$
→ *produces: Time in years*
→ *use when: Finding time duration*

**[Present Worth]**
$$P.W. = A - T.D.$$
→ *produces: Present Worth*
→ *use when: Amount and T.D. known*

**[Rate Calculation]**
$$S.P. = C.P. \times (1 + \frac{Gain\%}{100})$$
→ *produces: Effective Selling Price*
→ *use when: Calculating gain on credit*

## Step Sequences

**Discount Proportionality:** Identify $P.W.$ → Calculate $R$ → Apply new $T$ → Solve.
**Equivalent Offers:** Calculate $P.W.$ of credit → Compare with cash → Select higher.
**Interest Comparison:** Find $Sum$ via formula → Calculate $R$ → Verify.
**Missing Variables:** Find $P.W.$ → Apply $R$ or $T$ formula → Solve.
**Present Worth:** Calculate $100+RT$ → Divide $100A$ by factor → Result.
**Rate Calculation:** Find $P.W.$ → Use $R$ formula → Solve.

---

## Fastest Tricks

* **Discount Proportionality:** $T.D.$ is interest on $P.W.$, not on the Amount.
* **Equivalent Offers:** Always compare the $P.W.$ of the credit offer to the cash offer.
* **Interest Comparison:** $Sum = \frac{S.I. \times T.D.}{S.I. - T.D.}$ is the fastest way to find the principal.
* **Rate Calculation:** $R = \frac{100 \times (S.I. - T.D.)}{T.D. \times T}$.

---

## Trap Watch

* **Discount Proportionality:** Assuming $T.D.$ is proportional to time → Use $P.W.$ denominator.
* **Equivalent Offers:** Forgetting to convert months to years → Divide months by 12.
* **Interest Comparison:** Confusing $Sum$ with $P.W.$ → $Sum = P.W. + T.D.$
* **Missing Variables:** Using $A$ instead of $P.W.$ in $R$ formula → Use $A - T.D.$
* **Present Worth:** Calculating interest on $A$ instead of $P.W.$ → Use $100+RT$ divisor.

---

## Last-Minute Reminders

True Discount is the simple interest on the Present Worth.
The Amount due is always the sum of the Present Worth and the True Discount.
Always convert time into years before plugging into any formula.
The Present Worth is always less than the Amount due.
Simple Interest is always greater than True Discount for the same sum and time.
