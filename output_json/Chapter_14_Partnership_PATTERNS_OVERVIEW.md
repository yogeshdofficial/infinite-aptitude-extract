# Partnership

## What This Chapter Is About

Partnership deals with the proportional distribution of business profits among multiple investors based on their capital contributions and the duration of their investments. The central relationship is that profit is always directly proportional to the product of the capital invested and the time for which it remains in the business.

---

## Key Terms

**Partner** — An individual who contributes capital to a joint business venture.

**Capital** — The amount of money invested by a partner into the business.

**Time Period** — The duration for which a specific amount of capital is invested.

**Simple Partnership** — A business arrangement where all partners invest their capital for the same duration.

**Compound Partnership** — A business arrangement where partners invest their capital for different durations.

**Working Partner** — A partner who receives an additional share of profit as compensation for managing daily business operations.

---

## Core Formulas

### [Profit Distribution Ratio]

$$
\text{Profit}_A : \text{Profit}_B : \text{Profit}_C = (C_1 \times T_1) : (C_2 \times T_2) : (C_3 \times T_3)
$$

*When to use:* To determine how to split total profit among partners based on their respective investments and time.

*Variables:* $C$ = Capital invested; $T$ = Time period of investment.

### [Equivalent Capital]

$$
\text{Equivalent Capital} = \text{Capital} \times \text{Time Units}
$$

*When to use:* To normalize investments when partners join or withdraw capital at different times.

*Variables:* Capital = Amount invested; Time Units = Duration of investment.

---

## How Patterns Connect

**Capital Changes** — Questions where partners add or withdraw capital mid-way through the business term.

**Equivalent Units** — Questions requiring the conversion of different time periods into a common unit for comparison.

**Missing Capital** — Questions where the profit ratio and time are known, but one partner's investment amount is unknown.

**Missing Time** — Questions where the profit ratio and capital are known, but the duration of an investment is unknown.

**Missing Total Profit** — Questions where individual shares are known, and the total profit must be calculated.

**Mixed Distribution** — Questions involving complex profit-sharing rules, such as fixed salaries plus proportional dividends.

**Phased Usage** — Questions where capital is invested in distinct, sequential stages or blocks of time.

**Profit Rate Capital** — Questions focusing on the relationship between the rate of return and the capital invested.

**Relative Investment** — Questions where capital amounts are given as fractions or multiples of one another.

**Rent Sharing** — Questions where partners pay rent for business premises proportional to their usage or investment.

**Simple Partnership** — Questions where all partners invest for the exact same duration.

**Time Investment Ratio** — Questions where the primary goal is to find the ratio of time periods given capital and profit.

**Varying Time** — Questions where partners join or leave the business at different points throughout the year.

**Working Partner** — Questions where a specific partner receives a management fee before the remaining profit is distributed.

---

## Common Traps (Chapter-Wide)

* **Time Mismatch:** Failing to convert all time periods into the same unit (e.g., mixing months and years) before calculating the product of capital and time.
* **Ignoring Management Fees:** Forgetting to subtract the working partner's salary or commission from the total profit *before* distributing the remainder according to the investment ratio.
* **Partial Year Calculations:** Miscalculating the "time" variable for partners who join mid-year; always count the months from the date of joining until the end of the financial year.
