# Chapter 14: Partnership

## Chapter Overview

Partnership is the study of distributing business profits or losses among partners based on their capital investment and the duration of that investment.

The central idea is the **"Equivalent Capital"** concept: Profit is directly proportional to the product of capital and time.

This chapter is essential for competitive exams as it tests ratio manipulation, weighted averages, and logical deduction under varying business conditions.

---

## Core Concepts

* **Simple Partnership:** All partners invest for the same duration. Profit is shared in the ratio of capital.
* **Compound Partnership:** Partners invest for different durations. Profit is shared in the ratio of (Capital × Time).
* **Working vs. Sleeping Partners:** A working partner often receives a fixed salary or a percentage of profit for management before the remaining profit is distributed.
* **Capital Adjustments:** If capital changes mid-term, calculate the "effective capital" by summing the products of capital and the time it was held.

---

## Important Formulas

* **General Profit Ratio:**
  $$\text{Profit}_A : \text{Profit}_B = (C_1 \times T_1) : (C_2 \times T_2)$$
  *Where $C$ = Capital, $T$ = Time.*

* **Individual Share:**
  $$\text{Share of } A = \left( \frac{\text{A's Ratio}}{\text{Sum of all Ratios}} \right) \times \text{Total Profit}$$

* **Working Partner Adjustment:**
  $$\text{Net Profit} = \text{Total Profit} - \text{Management Salary}$$
  *Distribute the Net Profit using the standard ratio formula.*

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Ratio Simplification:** Always simplify the ratio of investments (e.g., remove common zeros) *before* multiplying by time to save time.
* **The "Unit" Method:** If capitals are given as fractions (e.g., $1/2 : 1/3 : 1/4$), multiply by the LCM of denominators to get a clean integer ratio ($6:4:3$).
* **Time-Saving Observation:** If all partners invest for the same time, ignore time entirely. If all invest the same amount, ignore capital and share profit equally.
* **Mental Check:** The sum of individual profit shares must always equal the total profit. Use this to verify your final answers.

---

## Pattern Map

* **Fixed Capital/Time:** Simple ratio division. *Clue: "At the end of the year".*
* **Variable Time:** Multiply capital by months. *Clue: "X joined after Y months".*
* **Capital Changes:** Sum of products. *Clue: "Withdrew/Invested X amount after Y months".*
* **Salary/Commission:** Deduct fixed amount first. *Clue: "Working partner gets X% for management".*

---

## Problem Recognition Guide

* **Keywords:** "Invested," "Joined," "Withdrew," "Ratio of shares," "Annual profit."
* **Given:** Individual capitals, time periods, and total profit.
* **Found:** Individual profit shares or the total profit.
* **Structure:** Usually involves a 3-step process: (1) Find ratio, (2) Simplify, (3) Distribute total profit.

---

## Common Mistakes

1. **Ignoring Time:** Treating all investments as equal time. *Fix: Always check if everyone stayed for the full duration.*
2. **Wrong Time Units:** Mixing months and years. *Fix: Convert everything to months.*
3. **Forgetting Salary:** Distributing total profit before paying the working partner. *Fix: Subtract salary first.*
4. **Calculation Errors:** Multiplying large numbers. *Fix: Cancel zeros early.*
5. **Misinterpreting "After X months":** Confusing "invested for X months" with "invested after X months." *Fix: Read carefully; "after" means total time minus X.*

## Revision Sheet

### Most Important Formula
$$\text{Profit Ratio} = \sum (\text{Capital} \times \text{Time})$$

### Main Idea
Profit is shared based on the **weighted contribution** of money over time.

### Fast Trick
Simplify investment ratios by dividing by the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) before multiplying by time.

### Biggest Trap
Forgetting to subtract the "Working Partner's Salary" from the total profit before applying the investment ratio.
