# Clock Angles

## Overview
These questions ask for the angle between the hour and minute hands at a specific time or the total rotation of a single hand over a duration. The central idea is that both hands move at constant, distinct speeds: the minute hand moves $6^{\circ}$ per minute, and the hour hand moves $0.5^{\circ}$ per minute.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "angle between the hour hand and the minute hand," "through how many degrees will the hour hand rotate," "minute hand will move over."
* **Given:** A specific time (e.g., 3:25) or a time interval (e.g., 2 hours 20 minutes).
* **Asked:** The angle in degrees between the hands or the total degrees covered by one hand.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Angle Between Hands]
$$
\theta = |30H - 5.5M|^{\circ}
$$

**Variables:**
- $H$ = Hour (1-12)
- $M$ = Minutes (0-59)

**When to use:** To find the angle between hands at a specific time (e.g., 3:25).

**Worked example:** For 3:25, $H=3, M=25$. $\theta = |30(3) - 5.5(25)| = |90 - 137.5| = 47.5^{\circ}$.

### [Hand Rotation]
$$
\text{Angle} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time}
$$

**Variables:**
- Speed = $0.5^{\circ}/\text{min}$ (hour hand) or $6^{\circ}/\text{min}$ (minute hand)
- Time = Total duration in minutes

**When to use:** To find how many degrees a single hand moves over a duration.

**Worked example:** Minute hand in 5 minutes: $6^{\circ}/\text{min} \times 5 \text{ min} = 30^{\circ}$.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Step 1: Standardize Time:** Convert all time durations into total minutes or hours to ensure consistency.
2. **Step 2: Identify Speed:** Use $0.5^{\circ}/\text{min}$ for the hour hand or $6^{\circ}/\text{min}$ for the minute hand.
3. **Step 3: Calculate Position/Rotation:** Multiply the speed by the total time elapsed.
4. **Step 4: Find Difference:** If finding the angle between hands, subtract the smaller angle from the larger one.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** Use the formula $\theta = |30H - 5.5M|^{\circ}$ for any "angle between hands" question.
* **Why it works:** It combines the individual speeds ($30^{\circ}$ per hour and $0.5^{\circ}$ per minute) into a single expression.
* **When to use it:** Whenever you are given a specific time like "X:Y".
* **Example:** For 3:25, $30(3) = 90$ and $5.5(25) = 137.5$. Difference is $47.5^{\circ}$.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Assuming the hour hand stays on the hour mark.
    * **Why it happens:** Forgetting that the hour hand moves as the minute hand moves.
    * **Fix:** Always use the $5.5M$ component in the formula.
* **Mistake:** Using $6^{\circ}$ for the hour hand.
    * **Why it happens:** Confusing the speed of the minute hand with the hour hand.
    * **Fix:** Remember: Minute hand = $6^{\circ}/\text{min}$, Hour hand = $0.5^{\circ}/\text{min}$.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** Find the angle between the hour hand and the minute hand of a clock when the time is 3:25.

**Solution:**
1. **Step 1:** Identify $H=3$ and $M=25$.
2. **Step 2:** Calculate hour hand position: $30 \times 3 = 90^{\circ}$ (base) plus $0.5 \times 25 = 12.5^{\circ}$ (movement due to minutes) $= 102.5^{\circ}$.
3. **Step 3:** Calculate minute hand position: $6 \times 25 = 150^{\circ}$.
4. **Step 4:** Subtract: $150^{\circ} - 102.5^{\circ} = 47.5^{\circ}$.

**Answer:** $47.5^{\circ}$ (or $47\frac{1}{2}^{\circ}$)

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\theta = |30H - 5.5M|^{\circ}$
* **Spot it by:** Looking for "angle between hands" or "degrees rotated."
* **First move:** Convert time to total minutes if calculating single-hand rotation.
* **Fastest method:** Use the $30H - 5.5M$ formula for angles between hands.
* **Biggest trap:** Forgetting the hour hand moves $0.5^{\circ}$ every minute.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Circular Motion** — A full circle is $360^{\circ}$.
* **Time Conversion** — $1 \text{ hour} = 60 \text{ minutes}$.

### Formulas You Must Know First
* **Speed = Distance / Time** — Used to derive the $0.5^{\circ}/\text{min}$ and $6^{\circ}/\text{min}$ rates.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Hour Hand** — The short hand, moves $30^{\circ}$ per hour or $0.5^{\circ}$ per minute.
* **Minute Hand** — The long hand, moves $360^{\circ}$ per hour or $6^{\circ}$ per minute.
