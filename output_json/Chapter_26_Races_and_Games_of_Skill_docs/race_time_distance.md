# Race Time Distance

## Overview
These questions involve two runners competing over a fixed distance where one runner finishes ahead of the other. The core idea is that the "lead" (in distance or time) represents the difference in performance over the race course, allowing you to calculate speeds or total times using ratios.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "beats by", "start of", "km race", "winning post", "time over the course".
* **Given:** A distance lead (e.g., "beats by 28 m") or a time lead (e.g., "beats by 7 seconds").
* **Asked:** Total time taken by a runner, the distance of the race, or the speed of a runner.
* **Scenario:** Two runners (A and B) covering the same total distance.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Time-Distance Equivalence]
$$
\text{Speed}_B = \frac{\text{Distance Lead}}{\text{Time Lead}}
$$

**Variables:**
- $\text{Distance Lead}$ = distance B is behind when A finishes.
- $\text{Time Lead}$ = time difference between A and B finishing.

**When to use:** When a problem gives both a distance and time lead for the same race.

**Worked example:** A beats B by 28 m or 7 seconds. B's speed = $\frac{28}{7} = 4 \text{ m/s}$.

### [Total Time Calculation]
$$
\text{Total Time}_B = \frac{\text{Total Race Distance}}{\text{Speed}_B}
$$

**Variables:**
- $\text{Total Race Distance}$ = length of the race (e.g., 1000 m).
- $\text{Speed}_B$ = speed of the slower runner.

**When to use:** To find the total time taken by the slower runner after finding their speed.

**Worked example:** Race = 200 m, B's speed = 5 m/s. B's time = $\frac{200}{5} = 40 \text{ seconds}$.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Identify the "Gap":** Determine the distance or time difference between the two runners at the finish line.
2. **Calculate Speed:** Use the gap to find the speed of the slower runner ($\text{Speed} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}}$).
3. **Find Total Time:** Divide the total race distance by the slower runner's speed to get their total time.
4. **Adjust for Winner:** Subtract the time lead from the slower runner's total time to find the winner's time.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** $\frac{\text{Distance}_A}{\text{Distance}_B} = \frac{\text{Time}_A}{\text{Time}_B}$ (for constant speed).
* **Why it works:** Since speed is constant, the ratio of distances covered in the same time is equal to the ratio of speeds.
* **When to use:** When comparing two runners' performance over different distances or times.
* **Example:** If A runs 1000 m in 200 s and B runs 950 m in 200 s, their speeds are in ratio $1000:950$.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Adding the time lead instead of subtracting it.
    * **Fix:** Remember the winner is *faster*, so they take *less* time.
* **Mistake:** Using the winner's speed to calculate the loser's distance.
    * **Fix:** Always use the loser's speed to calculate the distance they cover in the "gap" time.
* **Mistake:** Forgetting to convert minutes to seconds.
    * **Fix:** Always convert all time units to seconds before performing calculations.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** In a 200 m race, A beats B by 35 m or 7 seconds. A’s time over the course is?

**Solution:**
1. **Identify the Gap:** B is 35 m behind when A finishes, which takes B 7 seconds to cover.
2. **Calculate Speed:** B's speed = $\frac{35 \text{ m}}{7 \text{ s}} = 5 \text{ m/s}$.
3. **Find Total Time (B):** B's total time = $\frac{200 \text{ m}}{5 \text{ m/s}} = 40 \text{ seconds}$.
4. **Adjust for Winner (A):** A's time = $40 \text{ s} - 7 \text{ s} = 33 \text{ seconds}$.

**Answer:** 33 seconds.

## Similar Patterns
* **Relative Speed Problems:** These involve two objects moving toward or away from each other; distinguish by checking if they are on a "race course" (fixed distance) or a "journey" (variable distance).

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\text{Speed} = \frac{\text{Distance Gap}}{\text{Time Gap}}$.
* **Spot it by:** "Beats by" or "Start of" keywords.
* **First move:** Calculate the speed of the slower runner using the gap.
* **Fastest method:** Use the unitary method for distance/time ratios.
* **Biggest trap:** Adding the time lead instead of subtracting it.

---

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Unitary Method** — finding the value of a single unit to calculate larger values.
* **Speed-Distance-Time** — $\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time}$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Start** — A head start given to a slower runner to make the race competitive.
* **Beats by** — The margin by which the winner finishes ahead of the loser.
