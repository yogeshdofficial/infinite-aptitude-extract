# Direct Proportion

## Overview
This pattern involves two quantities where an increase in one leads to a proportional increase in the other (e.g., more items cost more money). The central idea is that the ratio between the two quantities remains constant.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "at the same rate," "uniform," "how many," "what will be the price/weight/cost."
* **Given:** Two related values (e.g., 5.5 dozen pens = ₹1287).
* **Asked:** A new value for a different quantity (e.g., cost of 16 pens).
* **Logic:** If you have more of the first item, you must have more of the second.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Direct Proportion Ratio]
$$
\frac{A_1}{A_2} = \frac{B_1}{B_2}
$$

**Variables:**
- $A_1, A_2$ = Quantity 1 (e.g., number of pens)
- $B_1, B_2$ = Quantity 2 (e.g., total cost)

**When to use:** When you have a known pair $(A_1, B_1)$ and one part of a second pair $(A_2)$ to find the unknown $(B_2)$.

**Worked example:** 66 pens cost ₹1287. Find cost of 16 pens.
$\frac{66}{16} = \frac{1287}{x} \implies 66x = 16 \times 1287 \implies x = 312$.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Standardize Units:** Convert all quantities to the same unit (e.g., dozen to pens, kg to grams) to avoid calculation errors.
2. **Identify the Pair:** Write down the known relationship as a ratio (e.g., $A_1$ corresponds to $B_1$).
3. **Set up the Proportion:** Place the unknown as $x$ and equate the ratios: $\frac{A_1}{A_2} = \frac{B_1}{x}$.
4. **Cross-Multiply:** Multiply $A_1 \times x$ and $A_2 \times B_1$.
5. **Solve for $x$:** Divide the product of the second pair by the remaining coefficient.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** Use the "Unitary Method" (Find the value of 1 unit first).
* **Why it works:** It simplifies the ratio to a single multiplication step: $\text{Value} = (\frac{\text{Total Cost}}{\text{Total Quantity}}) \times \text{New Quantity}$.
* **When to use:** When the numbers are easy to divide.
* **Example:** Cost of 5 pens is ₹20. Cost of 8 pens? $20/5 = 4$ (price per pen). $4 \times 8 = 32$.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Forgetting to convert units (e.g., using 5.5 dozen instead of 66 pens).
    * **Fix:** Always check if units match before setting up the ratio.
* **Mistake:** Inverting the ratio (e.g., $\frac{A_1}{A_2} = \frac{x}{B_1}$).
    * **Fix:** Ensure the numerator and denominator represent the same "type" of unit on both sides.
* **Mistake:** Rounding decimals too early.
    * **Fix:** Keep fractions as they are until the final division step.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** If 11.25 m of a uniform iron rod weighs 42.75 kg, what will be the weight of 6 m of the same rod?

**Solution:**
1. **Standardize:** Units are already in meters and kg. No conversion needed.
2. **Identify Pair:** $11.25 \text{ m} \to 42.75 \text{ kg}$.
3. **Set up Proportion:** $\frac{11.25}{6} = \frac{42.75}{x}$.
4. **Cross-Multiply:** $11.25 \times x = 6 \times 42.75$.
5. **Solve:** $11.25x = 256.5 \implies x = \frac{256.5}{11.25} = 22.8$.

**Answer:** 22.8 kg.

---

## Similar Patterns

**Indirect Proportion:** Distinguish by checking the logic: if one value increases, does the other *decrease*? (e.g., more workers = fewer days). If yes, use Inverse Proportion ($A_1 \times B_1 = A_2 \times B_2$).

---

## Revision Summary

* **Key formula:** $\frac{A_1}{A_2} = \frac{B_1}{B_2}$
* **Spot it by:** "At the same rate" or "Uniform" phrasing.
* **First move:** Convert all units to be identical.
* **Fastest method:** Unitary method (find price/value of 1 unit).
* **Biggest trap:** Forgetting to convert dozens/weeks/kg to base units.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Ratio** — A comparison of two quantities by division.
* **Cross-Multiplication** — Solving $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}$ by calculating $ad = bc$.

### Formulas You Must Know First
* **Unit Price** = $\frac{\text{Total Price}}{\text{Total Quantity}}$

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Dozen** — A group of 12 items.
* **Uniform** — Having the same properties throughout; implies a constant rate.
