# Hcf And Lcm — Exam Cheat Sheet

## How To Solve Any Question In This Chapter
1. **Identify Goal:** Does the question ask for a "common divisor" (HCF) or a "common multiple" (LCM)?
2. **Check Constraints:** Are there remainders, ratios, or specific number properties (e.g., "exactly divisible")?
3. **Map to Pattern:** Use the Quick-Recognition Table to match the question type.
4. **Standardize Units:** Convert all measurements to the smallest unit (e.g., meters to cm) before calculating.
5. **Execute Formula:** Apply the specific formula from the Formula Bank.
6. **Sanity Check:** Ensure the HCF is $\le$ the smallest number and the LCM is $\ge$ the largest number.

## Formula Bank

**[Basic HCF LCM]**
$$H.C.F. = \text{Product of lowest powers of common prime factors}$$
→ *produces: The greatest common divisor*
→ *use when: Finding HCF of large numbers*

$$L.C.M. = \text{Product of highest powers of all prime factors}$$
→ *produces: The least common multiple*
→ *use when: Finding LCM of large numbers*

$$H.C.F. \text{ of fractions} = \frac{H.C.F. \text{ of numerators}}{L.C.M. \text{ of denominators}}$$
→ *produces: HCF of fractional values*
→ *use when: Dealing with fractions*

$$L.C.M. \text{ of fractions} = \frac{L.C.M. \text{ of numerators}}{H.C.F. \text{ of denominators}}$$
→ *produces: LCM of fractional values*
→ *use when: Dealing with fractions*

**[Greatest Common Measure]**
$$\text{Required Number} = H.C.F. \text{ of } (N_1 - R_1), (N_2 - R_2)$$
→ *produces: Number leaving specific remainders*
→ *use when: Remainders are provided*

$$\text{Required Number} = H.C.F. \text{ of } (b-a), (c-b), (c-a)$$
→ *produces: Number leaving same remainder*
→ *use when: Remainder is unknown/same*

**[LCM Problems]**
$$\text{Required Number} = L.C.M. + \text{Remainder}$$
→ *produces: Number with constant remainder*
→ *use when: "Leaves remainder R"*

$$\text{Required Number} = L.C.M. - \text{Difference}$$
→ *produces: Number with constant deficit*
→ *use when: "Increased by X is divisible"*

**[Number Relationships]**
$$\text{Product of two numbers} = H.C.F. \times L.C.M.$$
→ *produces: Missing number or product*
→ *use when: Two numbers are involved*

$$\text{Numbers} = \text{Ratio} \times H.C.F.$$
→ *produces: The actual values*
→ *use when: Ratio and HCF given*

## Step Sequences

**Basic HCF LCM:** Factorize → Identify common/all bases → Select min/max powers → Multiply.
**Greatest Common Measure:** Subtract remainders → Apply Euclidean algorithm → Find HCF.
**LCM Problems:** Find LCM → Add remainder or subtract deficit → Adjust for n-digits.
**Number Relationships:** Multiply ratio by HCF → Use product formula → Solve for missing variable.

---

## Fastest Tricks

**Basic HCF LCM:** Use Euclidean algorithm for large numbers to avoid prime factorization.
**Greatest Common Measure:** HCF of differences is always a factor of the numbers.
**LCM Problems:** For "same remainder", calculate $LCM(divisors) + R$.
**Number Relationships:** If ratio is $a:b$ and HCF is $x$, numbers are $ax$ and $bx$.

---

## Trap Watch

**Basic HCF LCM:** Selecting highest power for HCF → Use lowest power.
**Greatest Common Measure:** Forgetting to subtract remainders first → Subtract before HCF.
**LCM Problems:** Adding remainder when it should be subtracted → Check "increased by".
**Number Relationships:** Forgetting to multiply ratio by HCF → Multiply both terms.

---

## Last-Minute Reminders

The HCF of two co-prime numbers is always 1.
The product of two numbers is equal to the product of their HCF and LCM.
Always convert all units to the smallest unit before starting calculations.
When finding the greatest number of n-digits, find the LCM and adjust the remainder.
The HCF of a set of numbers cannot be greater than the smallest number in the set.
