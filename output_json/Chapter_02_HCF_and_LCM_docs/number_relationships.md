# Number Relationships

## Overview
These questions involve finding two unknown numbers when given their ratio, sum, product, or difference alongside their HCF or LCM. The central idea is to represent the two numbers as $Hx$ and $Hy$, where $H$ is the HCF and $x, y$ are co-prime integers.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Ratio of", "H.C.F. is", "L.C.M. is", "Sum of two numbers", "Difference of".
* **Given:** A ratio (e.g., $15:11$) or a relationship between HCF/LCM and the numbers.
* **Asked:** The actual values of the two numbers.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Numbers from Ratio and HCF]
$$
\text{Number}_1 = H \times a, \quad \text{Number}_2 = H \times b
$$
**Variables:** $H$ = HCF, $a:b$ = given ratio.
**When to use:** When the ratio and HCF are provided.
**Example:** Ratio $15:11$, HCF $13$. Numbers: $15 \times 13 = 195$ and $11 \times 13 = 143$.

### [Numbers from Ratio and LCM]
$$
\text{L.C.M.} = H \times a \times b \implies \text{L.C.M.} = x \times a \times b
$$
**Variables:** $x$ = common multiplier, $a, b$ = ratio terms.
**When to use:** When the ratio and LCM are provided.
**Example:** Ratio $3:4$, LCM $84$. $3 \times 4 \times x = 84 \implies 12x = 84 \implies x = 7$. Numbers: $21, 28$.

### [Product Property]
$$
\text{Number}_1 \times \text{Number}_2 = \text{H.C.F.} \times \text{L.C.M.}
$$
**Variables:** $\text{Number}_1, \text{Number}_2$ = the two values.
**When to use:** When three of these four values are known.
**Example:** HCF $11$, LCM $693$, Number $77$. $77 \times B = 11 \times 693 \implies B = 99$.

---

## Solution Framework

**Step 1: Define variables** — Express numbers as $Hx$ and $Hy$ (where $x, y$ are co-prime) or use the ratio multiplier $x$.
**Step 2: Set up equation** — Use the given sum, product, or LCM to create an algebraic equation.
**Step 3: Solve for $x$** — Isolate the variable to find the common multiplier.
**Step 4: Calculate numbers** — Multiply the ratio terms or factors by the value of $x$ found.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Trick:** For sum of numbers with HCF $H$, divide the sum by $H$ to get $(a+b)$.
* **Why it works:** Since $H(a+b) = \text{Sum}$, then $(a+b) = \text{Sum}/H$.
* **When to use:** When asked for the number of possible pairs.
* **Example:** Sum $462$, HCF $22$. $a+b = 462/22 = 21$. Pairs: $(1,20), (2,19), (4,17), (5,16), (8,13), (10,11)$. Total $6$.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Mistake:** Treating the ratio terms as the numbers themselves.
    * **Fix:** Always multiply ratio terms by the HCF or common multiplier $x$.
* **Mistake:** Forgetting to check for "co-prime" pairs in sum problems.
    * **Fix:** Ensure $a$ and $b$ share no common factors other than $1$.
* **Mistake:** Confusing LCM with HCF in the ratio formula.
    * **Fix:** Remember LCM includes the product of the ratio terms ($H \times a \times b$).

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** The sum of two numbers is 462 and their H.C.F. is 22. Find the number of pairs.

**Solution:**
1. **Define:** Let numbers be $22a$ and $22b$, where $a, b$ are co-prime.
2. **Equation:** $22a + 22b = 462 \implies a + b = 21$.
3. **Find Pairs:** List pairs $(a, b)$ that sum to 21 and are co-prime: $(1,20), (2,19), (4,17), (5,16), (8,13), (10,11)$.
4. **Count:** There are 6 such pairs.

**Answer:** 6 pairs.

---

## Similar Patterns
**Algebraic Word Problems:** Distinguish by checking if HCF/LCM is mentioned; if not, it is a standard linear equation problem.

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\text{Product} = \text{HCF} \times \text{LCM}$.
* **Spot it by:** Presence of HCF/LCM and ratio/sum.
* **First move:** Express numbers as $Hx$ and $Hy$.
* **Fastest method:** Divide sum by HCF to find the sum of co-prime ratio terms.
* **Biggest trap:** Forgetting that $a$ and $b$ must be co-prime.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Co-prime numbers** — Two numbers are co-prime if their HCF is 1.
* **HCF** — The largest number that divides all given numbers.
* **LCM** — The smallest number divisible by all given numbers.

### Formulas You Must Know First
$$
\text{Product of two numbers} = \text{H.C.F.} \times \text{L.C.M.}
$$
**What it means:** The product of two numbers is always equal to the product of their HCF and LCM.
**Example:** Numbers 12 and 18. HCF=6, LCM=36. $12 \times 18 = 216$; $6 \times 36 = 216$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **H.C.F.** — Highest Common Factor.
* **L.C.M.** — Least Common Multiple.
* **Ratio** — A relationship between two numbers indicating how many times one contains the other.
