# Simple Interest — Exam Cheat Sheet

## How To Solve Any Question In This Chapter
1. **Identify the Goal:** Determine if you need Principal ($P$), Rate ($R$), Time ($T$), or Amount ($A$).
2. **Categorize:** Scan for keywords (e.g., "two parts," "rate changed," "instalments") to map to a pattern.
3. **Standardize Units:** Convert all time to years (months/12, days/365) and ensure $R$ is annual.
4. **Set Up Equation:** Use $SI = \frac{PRT}{100}$ or $A = P + SI$ as your base.
5. **Execute:** Solve for the unknown; if multiple variables are missing, look for proportionality ($SI \propto P$ or $SI \propto T$).
6. **Sanity Check:** Ensure the interest is less than the amount and the rate is a realistic percentage (usually 2–20%).

## Formula Bank

**[Comparative Interest]**
$$SI = \frac{P \times R \times T}{100}$$
→ *produces: Interest for one period*
→ *use when: Comparing two different interest scenarios*

**[Find Missing Variable]**
$$A = P(1 + \frac{RT}{100})$$
→ *produces: Total amount including interest*
→ *use when: Given amount and need principal*

$$R = \frac{100 \times SI}{P \times T}$$
→ *produces: Annual interest rate*
→ *use when: Need rate from known interest*

**[Instalment Debt]**
$$D = nx + \frac{xR}{100} \times \frac{n(n-1)}{2}$$
→ *produces: Annual instalment $x$*
→ *use when: Discharging debt $D$ in $n$ years*

**[Multi-Phase Interest]**
$$Total SI = \sum \frac{P \times R_i \times T_i}{100}$$
→ *produces: Total interest over phases*
→ *use when: Rate changes over time*

**[Rate Change Impact]**
$$New SI = Old SI + \frac{P \times \Delta R \times T}{100}$$
→ *produces: New total interest*
→ *use when: Rate increases or decreases*

**[Simple Interest Calculation]**
$$SI = \frac{P \times R \times T}{100}$$
→ *produces: Interest earned*
→ *use when: Basic interest calculation*

**[Split Investment]**
$$Total Income = \frac{P_1 R_1 T}{100} + \frac{P_2 R_2 T}{100}$$
→ *produces: Total interest from two parts*
→ *use when: Principal split into two rates*

## Step Sequences

*   **Comparative Interest:** Calculate $SI\%$ for both → Find difference → Equate to value → Solve $P$.
*   **Find Missing Variable:** Identify knowns → Select formula → Isolate variable → Calculate.
*   **Instalment Debt:** Identify $D, R, n$ → Apply instalment formula → Solve for $x$.
*   **Multi-Phase Interest:** List $R_i, T_i$ per phase → Sum $SI$ components → Solve for $P$.
*   **Rate Change Impact:** Find $Old SI$ → Calculate $\Delta SI$ using $\Delta R$ → Add to $Old A$.
*   **Simple Interest Calculation:** Convert $T$ to years → Apply $SI$ formula → Calculate.
*   **Split Investment:** Assume $P_1 = x, P_2 = Total-x$ → Form interest equation → Solve $x$.

---

## Fastest Tricks

*   **Comparative Interest:** Use difference in $SI\%$ to find $P$ instantly.
*   **Find Missing Variable:** For doubling, $R = \frac{100}{T}$ or $T = \frac{100}{R}$.
*   **Multi-Phase Interest:** Use weighted average of rates over total time.
*   **Rate Change Impact:** $\Delta SI = P \times \Delta R \times T$ (ignore old $R$).
*   **Split Investment:** Use Alligation method for faster ratio calculation.

---

## Trap Watch

*   **Comparative Interest:** Mixing subtraction order → Always subtract smaller from larger.
*   **Find Missing Variable:** Confusing $A$ with $SI$ → $A = P + SI$.
*   **Instalment Debt:** Forgetting final instalment earns no interest → Use $n(n-1)/2$.
*   **Multi-Phase Interest:** Miscalculating time for final phase → Check total $T$ sum.
*   **Simple Interest Calculation:** Using days without 365 denominator → Always divide by 365.
*   **Split Investment:** Forgetting to subtract $P_1$ from total → Use $P-x$.

---

## Last-Minute Reminders

Simple interest is always calculated on the original principal, never on accumulated interest.
Always convert months to years by dividing by 12 before plugging into the formula.
When a sum "becomes" a value, that value is the Amount ($P + SI$).
If the rate is given as a mixed fraction, convert to an improper fraction immediately.
For date-to-date calculations, exclude the starting day but include the ending day.
