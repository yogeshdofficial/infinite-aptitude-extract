# Partnership — Exam Cheat Sheet

## How To Solve Any Question In This Chapter
1. **Identify Roles:** Check if there is a "working partner" (salary/commission) or if all are "sleeping partners."
2. **Identify Time:** Check if all investments are for the same duration. If not, use the "Effective Capital" method ($\text{Capital} \times \text{Time}$).
3. **Identify Changes:** Scan for "withdrew," "invested more," or "joined/left" to determine if capital changes mid-term.
4. **Map Pattern:** Match the scenario to the Quick-Recognition Table below.
5. **Set Up Ratio:** Calculate the total effective capital for each partner: $\sum (\text{Capital} \times \text{Time})$.
6. **Distribute Profit:** Use the ratio to divide the total profit: $\text{Share} = \frac{\text{Individual Ratio}}{\text{Sum of Ratios}} \times \text{Total Profit}$.
7. **Sanity Check:** Ensure the sum of individual shares equals the total profit and ratios are in simplest integer form.

## Quick-Recognition Table

| Pattern | Trigger Words | Given | Find |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Capital Changes | withdrew, added, increases | Capital, Time, Changes | Share of profit |
| Complex Dist. | rent, charity, interest | Total profit, conditions | Individual share |
| Missing Var. | joined after, x months | Profit ratio, capital | Time or capital |
| Simple Part. | started, invested, ratio | Capitals, total profit | Individual share |
| Uncategorized | capital ratio, profit ratio | Both ratios | Time ratio |
| Varying Time | joined, left, months | Capitals, different times | Profit share |
| Working Part. | salary, managing, commission | Total profit, % fee | Individual share |

## Formula Bank

**[Capital Changes]**
$$ \text{Profit ratio} \propto \sum (\text{Capital} \times \text{Time}) $$
→ *produces: relative profit shares*
→ *use when: capital amounts change mid-term*

**[Complex Distribution]**
$$ \text{Share} = \text{Fixed Amount} + \left( \frac{\text{Ratio Part}}{\text{Total Ratio}} \times \text{Remaining Profit} \right) $$
→ *produces: final payout per partner*
→ *use when: profit is split by mixed rules*

**[Missing Variable]**
$$ \text{Time} = \frac{\text{Profit Ratio}}{\text{Capital Ratio}} $$
→ *produces: missing time duration*
→ *use when: profit and capital are known*

**[Simple Partnership]**
$$ \text{Share}_i = \frac{C_i}{\sum C_j} \times \text{Total Profit} $$
→ *produces: individual profit share*
→ *use when: time is equal for all*

**[Uncategorized]**
$$ \text{Time Ratio} = \frac{\text{Profit Ratio}}{\text{Capital Ratio}} $$
→ *produces: ratio of time periods*
→ *use when: profit and capital ratios given*

**[Varying Time]**
$$ \text{Share}_i = \frac{C_i \times T_i}{\sum (C_j \times T_j)} \times \text{Total Profit} $$
→ *produces: individual profit share*
→ *use when: partners invest for different times*

**[Working Partner]**
$$ \text{Share} = \text{Management Fee} + \left( \frac{\text{Capital Ratio}}{\text{Total Ratio}} \times \text{Balance} \right) $$
→ *produces: total partner income*
→ *use when: partner receives salary + share*

## Step Sequences

* **Capital Changes:** Calculate effective capital → Simplify ratio → Apply share formula.
* **Complex Dist.:** Deduct fixed fees → Distribute remainder by ratio → Add fixed back.
* **Missing Var.:** Set up ratio equation → Solve for variable → Verify against total.
* **Simple Part.:** Simplify capital ratio → Sum parts → Calculate individual shares.
* **Uncategorized:** Divide profit ratio by capital ratio → Simplify to integers.
* **Varying Time:** Multiply capital by months → Sum effective units → Calculate shares.
* **Working Part.:** Calculate salary → Subtract from total → Distribute balance by ratio.

---

## Fastest Tricks

* **Capital Changes:** Divide all capital amounts by a common factor before multiplying by time.
* **Missing Var.:** If profits are equal, time ratio is the inverse of capital ratio.
* **Simple Part.:** If investments are fractions, multiply by LCM of denominators to get integers.
* **Varying Time:** Convert all units (cows/horses) to one base unit before calculating effective capital.
* **Working Part.:** Calculate the working partner's total share as (Salary % + (100-Salary)% $\times$ Capital Share).

---

## Trap Watch

* **Capital Changes:** Ignoring remaining months → Use $(12 - \text{months passed})$.
* **Missing Var.:** Confusing $x$ months with $(12-x)$ months → Check start/end dates.
* **Simple Part.:** Forgetting to simplify ratios → Divide by HCF first.
* **Varying Time:** Using only initial capital → Multiply by time duration.
* **Working Part:** Applying salary to total profit → Apply to balance only.

---

## Last-Minute Reminders

1. Always simplify the final profit ratio to the smallest possible integer form before calculating shares.
2. If a partner joins mid-year, their time is the number of months remaining until the year-end.
3. When calculating effective capital, ensure all time units are in the same scale (e.g., all in months).
4. The sum of all individual profit shares must always equal the total profit given in the question.
5. In working partner questions, always subtract the management fee from the total profit before distributing the remainder.
