# Average-Ratio and Average-Speed Patterns

## Overview
These questions involve finding unknown values (ages, prices, or speeds) when given an average and a specific relationship (ratio or travel condition). The central idea is to convert the "Average" into a "Total" to create an algebraic equation that can be solved for the unknown variable.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Average age," "Average price," "Average speed," "Ratio of," "Returns back."
* **Given:** An average value and a ratio between components, or two speeds over the same distance.
* **Asked:** The value of a specific component (e.g., "son's age," "cheapest item price") or the overall average speed.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Sum of Observations]
$$
\text{Sum} = \text{Average} \times \text{Number of items}
$$
**Variables:**
- $\text{Sum}$ = Total value of all items
- $\text{Average}$ = The mean value provided
- $\text{Number of items}$ = Count of entities (e.g., 2 people, 3 items)

**When to use:** When you have a ratio of parts and need to find the actual values.

**Worked example:** Average age of 2 people is 40. $\text{Sum} = 40 \times 2 = 80$.

### [Average Speed for Equal Distances]
$$
\text{Average Speed} = \frac{2xy}{x+y}
$$
**Variables:**
- $x$ = Speed during the first leg
- $y$ = Speed during the return leg

**When to use:** When an object travels the same distance at two different speeds.

**Worked example:** $x=84, y=56$. $\text{Average Speed} = \frac{2 \times 84 \times 56}{84+56} = \frac{9408}{140} = 67.2$ km/hr.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Convert Average to Total:** Multiply the average by the number of items to get the sum.
2. **Assign Variables:** Use the given ratio to express the items as $ax, bx, cx...$
3. **Form Equation:** Set the sum of the variables equal to the total calculated in Step 1.
4. **Solve for $x$:** Isolate $x$ by dividing the total by the sum of the ratio parts.
5. **Calculate Target:** Multiply $x$ by the specific ratio part requested in the question.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** For Average Speed, if speeds are $x$ and $y$, the average is always between $x$ and $y$.
* **Why it works:** The harmonic mean $\frac{2xy}{x+y}$ is mathematically bounded by the two input speeds.
* **When to use:** Use this to eliminate impossible options in multiple-choice questions.
* **Example:** With speeds 84 and 56, the answer must be between 56 and 84.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Using the average directly in the ratio (e.g., $11+5 = 40$).
    * **Why it happens:** Forgetting that the average is not the sum.
    * **Fix:** Always multiply the average by the count first.
* **Mistake:** Calculating the arithmetic mean of speeds $\frac{x+y}{2}$.
    * **Why it happens:** Confusing simple average with average speed over equal distances.
    * **Fix:** Use the harmonic mean formula $\frac{2xy}{x+y}$.
* **Mistake:** Assuming data is sufficient when it is not.
    * **Why it happens:** Trying to force a calculation when variables are missing (e.g., Q140).
    * **Fix:** Check if the number of independent equations matches the number of variables.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** The average price of three items of furniture is ₹ 15000. If their prices are in the ratio 3 : 5 : 7, the price of the cheapest item is?

**Solution:**
1. **Convert Average to Total:** $15000 \times 3 = 45000$.
2. **Assign Variables:** Prices are $3x, 5x, 7x$.
3. **Form Equation:** $3x + 5x + 7x = 45000$.
4. **Solve for $x$:** $15x = 45000 \Rightarrow x = 3000$.
5. **Calculate Target:** Cheapest is $3x = 3 \times 3000 = 9000$.

**Answer:** ₹ 9000.

---

## Similar Patterns
* **Weighted Average:** Distinguished by having different quantities for each group (e.g., 5 shirts at one price, 4 trousers at another); use the total expenditure method instead of simple ratio.

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\text{Sum} = \text{Average} \times \text{Count}$ or $\text{Avg Speed} = \frac{2xy}{x+y}$.
* **Spot it by:** Look for "Average" paired with "Ratio" or "Two speeds."
* **First move:** Convert the average into a total sum immediately.
* **Fastest method:** Use the harmonic mean formula for speed questions.
* **Biggest trap:** Calculating the simple average of speeds instead of the harmonic mean.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Ratio:** A way to compare quantities; if ratio is $a:b$, values are $ax$ and $bx$.
* **Algebraic Equations:** Solving for $x$ in $ax + bx = \text{Total}$.

### Formulas You Must Know First
* **Sum of observations:** $\text{Sum} = \text{Average} \times \text{Count}$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Average:** The central value of a set of numbers.
* **Ratio:** The quantitative relation between two or more amounts.
