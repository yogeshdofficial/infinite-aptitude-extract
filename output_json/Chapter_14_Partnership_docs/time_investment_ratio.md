# Time Investment Ratio

## Overview
These questions involve partners who invest different amounts of capital for different durations. The central principle is that the profit share is always proportional to the product of capital and time ($P \propto C \times T$).

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Ratio of time," "durations of their investments," "partnered for X months."
* **Given:** Capital ratios and Profit ratios (or Time ratios).
* **Asked:** The missing ratio (Time, Capital, or Profit).
* **Structure:** Three entities (A, B, C) with varying inputs.

---

## Important Formulas

### [Profit Distribution Principle]
$$
\text{Profit}_A : \text{Profit}_B : \text{Profit}_C = (C_1 \times T_1) : (C_2 \times T_2) : (C_3 \times T_3)
$$
*Meaning:* Profit share is the product of Capital ($C$) and Time ($T$).
*Use when:* You need to find any one variable given the other two.

---

## Solution Framework
1. Write the ratio of profits as $P_1 : P_2 : P_3$.
2. Set up the equation: $P_1 : P_2 : P_3 = (C_1 \times T_1) : (C_2 \times T_2) : (C_3 \times T_3)$.
3. Isolate the unknown variable (e.g., $T_1 = \frac{P_1}{C_1}$).
4. Simplify the resulting ratio by finding the LCM of the denominators.
5. Convert the fraction ratio into the simplest integer ratio.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Inverse Ratio Shortcut:** If profits are equal, the ratio of time is simply the inverse of the ratio of capitals ($T \propto \frac{1}{C}$).
* **Why:** Since $C \times T = \text{constant}$, $T$ must be the reciprocal of $C$ to maintain equality.
* **LCM Clearing:** When dealing with ratios of fractions like $\frac{a}{b} : \frac{c}{d} : \frac{e}{f}$, multiply all terms by the LCM of $(b, d, f)$ to clear denominators instantly.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Confusing Ratios:** Mixing up the order of partners when calculating $T = \frac{P}{C}$.
    * *Reason:* Careless mapping of $P_n$ to $C_n$.
    * *Fix:* Write the names (A, B, C) above the ratios to ensure alignment.
* **Forgetting to Simplify:** Leaving the answer as a fraction ratio.
    * *Reason:* Stopping after the division step.
    * *Fix:* Always multiply by the LCM to get the final integer ratio.
* **Ignoring Units:** Mixing months and years.
    * *Reason:* Assuming all time inputs are in the same unit.
    * *Fix:* Convert all time periods to the same unit (e.g., all months) before starting.

---

## Similar Patterns
This is distinct from "Simple Partnership" where time is constant. If time is constant, ignore $T$ and equate Profit ratio directly to Capital ratio.

---

## Revision Summary
**Key formula:** $\text{Profit} \propto \text{Capital} \times \text{Time}$.
**Spot it by:** Different capitals and different time durations given in the problem.
**Fastest method:** Use $T = \frac{P}{C}$ and clear denominators using the LCM.
**Biggest trap:** Failing to convert all time periods into the same unit (months).
