# Simple Partnership

## Overview

* These questions involve multiple partners investing capital into a business for the same duration.
* The central idea is that the profit distribution ratio is identical to the investment ratio.

---

## Recognition Clues

* **Keywords:** "Started a business," "invested," "share of profit," "ratio of their profits."
* **Given:** Individual investment amounts (or fractions of total capital) and total annual profit.
* **Asked:** Individual share of profit, or a specific percentage of a partner's share.
* **Timeframe:** All partners invest for the same duration (e.g., "annual profit," "at the end of 2 years").

---

## Important Formulas

### [Profit Distribution Ratio]

$$
\text{Share}_A : \text{Share}_B : \text{Share}_C = \text{Investment}_A : \text{Investment}_B : \text{Investment}_C
$$

*Meaning:* Profit is divided in direct proportion to the capital contributed.
*Use when:* All partners invest for the same time period.

### [Individual Share Calculation]

$$
\text{Partner's Share} = \text{Total Profit} \times \left( \frac{\text{Partner's Ratio Part}}{\text{Sum of Ratio Parts}} \right)
$$

*Meaning:* The specific portion of the total profit allocated to one partner.
*Use when:* You have the total profit and the simplified investment ratio.

---

## Solution Framework

1. Write down the investment amounts for all partners.
2. Simplify the investments into the smallest possible integer ratio by canceling common factors (zeros, decimals).
3. Calculate the sum of the ratio parts.
4. Divide the total profit by the sum of parts to find the value of "one part."
5. Multiply the value of "one part" by the individual partner's ratio part to get their share.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Zero Cancellation:** Immediately strike out common trailing zeros from all investment amounts before calculating the ratio.
* **Fractional Investment:** If investments are given as fractions (e.g., $\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{3}$), multiply by the LCM of denominators to convert them into whole numbers immediately.
* **Ratio Scaling:** If the total profit is a multiple of the sum of ratio parts, use mental estimation to eliminate options before calculating.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Ignoring Time:** Applying simple partnership logic when partners join at different times; always check if the time period is identical for everyone.
* **Ratio Inversion:** Calculating the ratio as $1/A : 1/B$ instead of $A : B$; remember profit is directly proportional to investment.
* **Calculation Errors:** Forgetting to simplify the ratio fully before calculating shares; always reduce the ratio to the lowest terms first.

---

## Similar Patterns

* **Compound Partnership:** Confused when partners join at different times; distinguish by checking if the problem mentions "after X months" or "joined later."
* **Distinction:** In Simple Partnership, time is constant; in Compound Partnership, you must multiply each investment by its respective time duration before finding the ratio.

---

## Revision Summary

**Key formula:** $\text{Profit Ratio} = \text{Investment Ratio}$.

**Spot it by:** Identical time periods for all partners in the business.

**Fastest method:** Simplify investment amounts to the smallest integer ratio first.

**Biggest trap:** Forgetting to simplify the ratio, leading to complex multiplication.
