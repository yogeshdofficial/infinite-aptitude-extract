# Simple Partnership

## Overview
This pattern involves business partners who invest their capital for the same duration of time. The central idea is that the profit is distributed in the exact same ratio as the capital invested by each partner.

---

## Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Ratio** — A way to compare two or more quantities of the same kind by division, expressed as $a:b:c$.
* **Simplification of Ratios** — Dividing all terms of a ratio by their Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) to reach the smallest whole-number form.
* **Proportional Division** — Distributing a total amount based on the parts of a ratio, where each share is $\text{Total} \times \frac{\text{Individual Part}}{\text{Sum of Parts}}$.

### Formulas You Must Know First
$$
\text{Share} = \frac{\text{Total Profit} \times \text{Individual Ratio}}{\text{Sum of Ratios}}
$$
**What it means:** This formula calculates the specific portion of the total profit belonging to one partner based on their relative contribution.
**Example:** If total profit is ₹ 100 and the ratio is $2:3$, the sum is $5$. Partner A's share is $100 \times \frac{2}{5} = 40$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Capital/Investment** — The amount of money a partner puts into the business.
* **Profit** — The total gain earned by the business at the end of the period.
* **Share** — The portion of the total profit allocated to an individual partner.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Started a business," "investing," "share of each," "profit earned."
* **Given:** Individual investment amounts and the total profit.
* **Time:** The duration is either identical for all (e.g., "annual profit," "at the end of 2 years") or not mentioned at all.
* **Asked:** The specific monetary share of one or more partners.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Profit Sharing Ratio]
$$
\text{Profit Ratio} = \text{Investment}_A : \text{Investment}_B : \text{Investment}_C
$$
**Variables:**
- $\text{Investment}_n$ = The capital amount contributed by partner $n$.

**When to use:** When all partners invest for the same duration.

**Worked example:** If A invests 120,000 and B invests 150,000, the ratio is $120,000 : 150,000 = 4 : 5$.

---

## Solution Framework
1. **Extract Investments:** Write down the capital invested by each partner.
2. **Form the Ratio:** Create a ratio of these investments (e.g., $A:B:C$).
3. **Simplify:** Divide all terms by their common factors to get the simplest integer ratio.
4. **Sum the Parts:** Add the terms of the simplified ratio together.
5. **Calculate Value of One Part:** Divide the total profit by the sum of the ratio parts.
6. **Find Individual Shares:** Multiply each partner's ratio part by the value of one part.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Trick:** Cancel common zeros in investment amounts immediately.
* **Why it works:** Ratios are scale-invariant; dividing all terms by 1,000 or 10,000 does not change the relationship between them.
* **When to use it:** Whenever investment amounts end in multiple zeros (e.g., 120,000 and 150,000).

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Mistake:** Forgetting to simplify the ratio.
    * **Why it happens:** Rushing to calculate shares using large numbers.
    * **Fix:** Always divide by the GCD before summing the parts.
* **Mistake:** Calculating the share of the wrong partner.
    * **Why it happens:** Mislabeling the ratio parts.
    * **Fix:** Explicitly write "A:B:C" above the numbers to maintain order.
* **Mistake:** Using the wrong total profit.
    * **Why it happens:** Confusing the total profit with an individual investment.
    * **Fix:** Clearly circle the "Total Profit" value in the question before starting.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)
**Question:** A, B and C started a business by investing ₹ 120000, ₹ 135000 and ₹ 150000 respectively. Find the share of each, out of an annual profit of ₹ 56700.

**Solution:**
1. **Extract:** A=120000, B=135000, C=150000.
2. **Ratio:** $120000 : 135000 : 150000$.
3. **Simplify:** Divide by 15000: $8 : 9 : 10$.
4. **Sum:** $8 + 9 + 10 = 27$.
5. **Value of one part:** $\frac{56700}{27} = 2100$.
6. **Individual Shares:**
   - A: $8 \times 2100 = 16800$
   - B: $9 \times 2100 = 18900$
   - C: $10 \times 2100 = 21000$

**Answer:** A's share is ₹ 16800, B's share is ₹ 18900, C's share is ₹ 21000.

---

## Similar Patterns
**Compound Partnership:** Distinguish this by checking if the question mentions different time periods for investments (e.g., "A joined after 3 months"). If times differ, you must multiply capital by time before forming the ratio.

---

## Revision Summary
**Key formula:** $\text{Share} = \text{Total Profit} \times \frac{\text{Individual Ratio}}{\text{Sum of Ratios}}$.
**Spot it by:** Identical investment durations for all partners.
**First move:** Simplify the investment amounts into the smallest ratio.
**Fastest method:** Cancel common zeros in investments before calculating.
**Biggest trap:** Failing to simplify the ratio before summing the parts.
