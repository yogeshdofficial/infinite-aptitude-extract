# Pattern Name: Basic Profit Distribution by Capital Ratio

## 1. Pattern Overview
This pattern provides a systematic method to distribute total business profits among partners based on the proportional weight of their individual capital contributions and the duration of those investments. It is essential for accurately calculating equitable financial returns in any joint business venture.

## 2. Key Formulas
**Simple Partnership Ratio:**
$$\text{Profit Ratio} = \text{Investment}_A : \text{Investment}_B : \text{Investment}_C$$

**Compound Partnership Ratio (Equivalent Capital):**
$$\text{Profit Ratio} = (\text{Capital}_A \times \text{Time}_A) : (\text{Capital}_B \times \text{Time}_B) : (\text{Capital}_C \times \text{Time}_C)$$

**Individual Share Calculation:**
$$\text{Partner's Share} = \left( \frac{\text{Partner's Ratio Part}}{\text{Sum of all Ratio Parts}} \right) \times \text{Total Profit}$$

## 3. When to Use This Pattern
*   **Keywords:** "Started a business," "invested," "joined after X months," "annual profit," "share of each."
*   **Recognition Clues:** The problem provides multiple investment amounts and asks for the division of a single total profit figure.
*   **Given:** Individual capital amounts and the duration of investment for each partner.
*   **To Find:** The specific monetary value of each partner's profit share.

## 4. Core Concept & Theory
The fundamental principle of partnership is that **profit is directly proportional to the product of capital and time.** 
*   If time is equal for all, profit is distributed solely by capital ratio (Simple Partnership).
*   If time varies, we must calculate the "Equivalent Capital" (Capital × Time) to normalize the contributions to a single unit of time (Compound Partnership).

## 5. Step-by-Step Solution Method
1.  **Identify Time Periods:** Determine how many months each partner's money was actually in the business.
2.  **Calculate Equivalent Capital:** Multiply each partner's investment by their respective time period.
3.  **Simplify the Ratio:** Divide the resulting products by their greatest common divisor to get the simplest integer ratio.
4.  **Sum the Ratio Parts:** Add all parts of the ratio together to find the denominator.
5.  **Distribute Profit:** Multiply the total profit by the fraction representing each partner's share (Partner's Part / Total Parts).

## 6. Worked Examples

**Example 1: Simple Partnership**
*Given:* A, B, and C invest ₹120,000, ₹135,000, and ₹150,000 respectively for one year. Total profit is ₹56,700.
*Solution:*
1. Ratio = 120,000 : 135,000 : 150,000 = 8 : 9 : 10.
2. Sum of parts = 8 + 9 + 10 = 27.
3. A's share = (8/27) × 56,700 = ₹16,800.
4. B's share = (9/27) × 56,700 = ₹18,900.
5. C's share = (10/27) × 56,700 = ₹21,000.

**Example 2: Compound Partnership**
*Given:* Alfred (₹45,000 for 12 months), Peter (₹60,000 for 9 months), Ronald (₹90,000 for 3 months). Total profit ₹16,500.
*Solution:*
1. Equivalent Capitals: (45k × 12) : (60k × 9) : (90k × 3) = 540k : 540k : 270k.
2. Simplify: 2 : 2 : 1.
3. Sum of parts = 5.
4. Alfred's share = (2/5) × 16,500 = ₹6,600.
5. Peter's share = (2/5) × 16,500 = ₹6,600.
6. Ronald's share = (1/5) × 16,500 = ₹3,300.

## 7. Recognition Clues & Keywords
*   **"At the end of the year":** Signals that you must calculate the time duration for each partner (e.g., if someone joins late, their time is < 12 months).
*   **"Invested respectively":** Indicates the order of partners matches the order of amounts.
*   **Do not confuse with:** Simple interest problems; partnership is about *sharing* a pool of money, not earning interest on a principal.

## 8. Common Mistakes
1.  **Ignoring Time:** Students often use only the capital amounts even when partners join at different times. Always check the "time" variable.
2.  **Calculation Errors in Simplification:** Failing to reduce the ratio to its simplest form makes the final multiplication unnecessarily difficult.
3.  **Incorrect Time Calculation:** Miscounting the months (e.g., "After 3 months" means the partner was in for 9 months, not 3). Always calculate: $12 - \text{months joined late}$.
4.  **Summing Errors:** Forgetting to add all parts of the ratio before calculating the fraction.

## 9. Practice Tips
*   **Normalize Units:** Always ensure all capital amounts are in the same currency/unit (e.g., all in thousands) before creating the ratio.
*   **Verification:** After calculating individual shares, add them up. The sum must equal the total profit provided in the problem.
*   **Related Patterns:** Study "Partnership with Working Partner Salary" (where one partner gets a fixed fee before the remaining profit is split).
