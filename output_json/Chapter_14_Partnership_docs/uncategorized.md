# Partnership: Finding Time Ratio

## Overview
This pattern involves finding the duration for which partners invested their capital when the capital ratios and profit ratios are already known. The central idea is that profit is directly proportional to the product of capital and time ($P \propto C \times T$).

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Capital contribution," "share profits," "ratio of time," "business term."
* **Given:** Ratio of capital investments and ratio of profit shares.
* **Asked:** The ratio of the time periods for which the capitals were invested.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Time Ratio Formula]

$$
\text{Time Ratio} = \frac{\text{Profit Ratio}}{\text{Capital Ratio}}
$$

**Variables:**
- $\text{Profit Ratio}$ = The relative shares of profit received by partners.
- $\text{Capital Ratio}$ = The relative amounts of money invested by partners.

**When to use:** When you are given the capital and profit ratios and need to find the time ratio.

**Worked example:** Given Capital Ratio $21:18:14$ and Profit Ratio $15:8:9$.
Time Ratio = $\frac{15}{21} : \frac{8}{18} : \frac{9}{14} = \frac{5}{7} : \frac{4}{9} : \frac{9}{14}$.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Set up the ratio:** Write the time ratio as $\frac{P_1}{C_1} : \frac{P_2}{C_2} : \frac{P_3}{C_3}$.
2. **Simplify fractions:** Reduce each individual fraction to its simplest form to make calculations easier.
3. **Find the LCM:** Identify the Least Common Multiple of all denominators in the simplified fractions.
4. **Clear denominators:** Multiply each term by the LCM to convert the ratio into whole numbers.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** Cross-multiply or use the "Cover-up" method if only two partners exist.
* **Why it works:** It bypasses the LCM step by directly equating the cross-products.
* **When to use it:** Only when there are exactly two partners.
* **Example:** If $P_1:P_2 = 2:3$ and $C_1:C_2 = 4:5$, then $T_1:T_2 = \frac{2}{4} : \frac{3}{5} = \frac{1}{2} : \frac{3}{5} = 5:6$.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Calculating $\frac{\text{Capital}}{\text{Profit}}$ instead of $\frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{Capital}}$.
    * **Why it happens:** Confusing the relationship between variables.
    * **Fix:** Remember $P = C \times T$, so $T = \frac{P}{C}$.
* **Mistake:** Forgetting to simplify the fractions before finding the LCM.
    * **Why it happens:** Trying to find the LCM of large, unsimplified numbers.
    * **Fix:** Always reduce $\frac{15}{21}$ to $\frac{5}{7}$ first.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** A, B and C enter into a partnership. Their capital contribution is in the ratio $21 : 18 : 14$. At the end of the business term they share profits in the ratio $15 : 8 : 9$. Find the ratio of time for which they invest their capitals.

**Solution:**
1. **Set up:** Time ratio = $\frac{15}{21} : \frac{8}{18} : \frac{9}{14}$.
2. **Simplify:** $\frac{5}{7} : \frac{4}{9} : \frac{9}{14}$.
3. **LCM:** The denominators are $7, 9, 14$. The LCM is $126$.
4. **Clear:** 
   - $A: \frac{5}{7} \times 126 = 5 \times 18 = 90$
   - $B: \frac{4}{9} \times 126 = 4 \times 14 = 56$
   - $C: \frac{9}{14} \times 126 = 9 \times 9 = 81$

**Answer:** The ratio of time is $90 : 56 : 81$.

---

## Similar Patterns

**General Partnership:** If the question asks for profit share given capital and time, use $P = C \times T$ directly; this pattern is the inverse (finding $T$ given $P$ and $C$).

---

## Revision Summary

* **Key formula:** $\text{Time} = \frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{Capital}}$.
* **Spot it by:** Given ratios of Capital and Profit, asked for Time.
* **First move:** Write the ratio as fractions $\frac{P}{C}$.
* **Fastest method:** Simplify fractions first, then multiply by the LCM.
* **Biggest trap:** Inverting the ratio (writing $\frac{C}{P}$ instead of $\frac{P}{C}$).

---

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Ratios** — A way to compare quantities using division.
* **LCM (Least Common Multiple)** — The smallest positive integer that is divisible by each of the numbers in a set.

### Formulas You Must Know First
$$
\text{Profit} = \text{Capital} \times \text{Time}
$$
**What it means:** The total profit earned is the product of the investment amount and the duration it was invested.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Capital** — The amount of money invested by a partner.
* **Profit Share** — The portion of the total profit allocated to a partner.
* **Business Term** — The total duration for which the business operated.
