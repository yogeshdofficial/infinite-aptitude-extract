# Equivalent Units (Partnership)

## Overview
* This pattern deals with distributing profits among partners based on their financial contribution and the duration of their investment.
* It covers problems where partners invest different amounts for either the same or varying time periods.
* The central idea is to convert all investments into **"Equivalent Capital"** (Capital × Time) to create a common basis for profit distribution.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Started a business," "Joined after X months," "Left after Y months," "Annual profit," "Share of profit."
* **Structure:** Multiple investors, varying capital amounts, varying time durations.
* **Given:** Individual capital amounts and time periods (or start/end dates).
* **To Find:** Individual share of total profit.

---

## Important Formulas

### Effective Capital (Equivalent Unit)
$$E = C \times T$$
*   **Meaning:** The total value of an investment over a specific period.
*   **Use:** Always calculate this first for every partner.

### Profit Ratio
$$\frac{P_1}{P_2} = \frac{C_1 \times T_1}{C_2 \times T_2}$$
*   **Meaning:** Profit is directly proportional to the product of capital and time.
*   **Use:** To find the ratio in which the total profit is divided.

### Individual Share
$$\text{Partner's Share} = \left( \frac{\text{Partner's Ratio}}{\text{Sum of Ratios}} \right) \times \text{Total Profit}$$
*   **Use:** Final step to convert ratio into actual currency.

---

## Solution Framework
1.  **Standardize Time:** Convert all time periods into the same unit (e.g., months).
2.  **Calculate Effective Capital:** Multiply each partner's capital by their respective time duration ($C \times T$).
3.  **Simplify Ratio:** Reduce the resulting values to their simplest integer ratio.
4.  **Sum the Parts:** Add the ratio components together.
5.  **Distribute:** Multiply the total profit by the fraction of each partner's ratio.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Zero Cancellation:** Before multiplying, cancel common zeros in capital amounts (e.g., $120,000 : 135,000 \rightarrow 120 : 135$).
* **Ratio Simplification:** Simplify the $C \times T$ products immediately to keep numbers small.
* **Time Symmetry:** If all partners invest for the same time, ignore time entirely; profit ratio = capital ratio.
* **Fractional Parts:** If the total ratio sum is 10, calculating shares is just a matter of moving the decimal point in the total profit.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Ignoring Time:** Assuming profit is shared only by capital ratio when time periods differ.
* **Miscalculating Time:** Using the "time passed" instead of "time invested" (e.g., if someone joins after 3 months, their time is $12 - 3 = 9$ months).
* **Unit Mismatch:** Mixing years and months in the same calculation.
* **Calculation Errors:** Failing to simplify the ratio before distributing the profit.
* **Summation Error:** Forgetting to add all parts of the ratio when calculating the denominator.

---

## Similar Patterns
* **Work-Time Problems:** Similar logic ($M_1D_1 = M_2D_2$), but focuses on output rather than profit.
* **Mixture & Alligation:** Uses ratios to combine quantities, whereas Partnership uses ratios to split a total.

## Revision Summary

### Key Formula
$$\text{Profit Ratio} = (C_1T_1) : (C_2T_2) : (C_3T_3)$$

### Recognition Signal
"Different investments + Different time durations = Equivalent Units."

### Main Trick
Cancel common zeros in capital amounts *before* multiplying by time.

### Common Trap
Using the "joining time" (e.g., 3 months) instead of the "active time" (e.g., $12 - 3 = 9$ months).
