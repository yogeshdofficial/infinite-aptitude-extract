# Ratio of Investment

## Overview

This pattern involves finding the ratio of capital invested when the profit distribution and time periods are already known. The central idea is the fundamental relationship: $\text{Profit} = \text{Investment} \times \text{Time}$.

---

## Recognition Clues

* **Keywords:** "Ratio of their investments," "shared the profit in the ratio," "partnered for [x] months."
* **Given:** Profit ratio and the time duration for each partner.
* **Asked:** The ratio of the initial investments (capitals).
* **Scan for:** A set of three or more ratios involving time and profit.

---

## Important Formulas

### [Investment Ratio Formula]

$$
\text{Investment} = \frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{Time}}
$$

*Meaning:* The capital invested is directly proportional to the profit divided by the time period.

*Use when:* You are given the final profit distribution and the time each partner stayed in the business, and you need to find the ratio of their original contributions.

---

## Solution Framework

1. Assign variables (e.g., $x, y, z$) to the unknown investments.
2. Set up the equation: $(\text{Investment}_1 \times \text{Time}_1) : (\text{Investment}_2 \times \text{Time}_2) : \dots = \text{Profit Ratio}$.
3. Express each investment in terms of one variable (e.g., $x$) using the given profit ratios.
4. Simplify the resulting ratio of fractions by finding the Least Common Multiple (LCM) of the denominators.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **The Inverse Ratio Shortcut:** If profits are equal, the investment ratio is simply the inverse of the time ratio ($I_1:I_2 = T_2:T_1$).
* **Why it works:** Since $I \times T = \text{Constant}$, $I$ must be inversely proportional to $T$.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Confusing Profit and Investment:** Students often multiply the profit ratio by time instead of dividing.
    * *Reason:* Misremembering the formula $P = I \times T$ as $I = P \times T$.
    * *Fix:* Always write $I \times T = P$ first, then isolate $I$.
* **Incorrect Simplification:** Failing to convert the final ratio of fractions into a whole number ratio.
    * *Reason:* Forgetting to multiply by the LCM of the denominators.
    * *Fix:* Multiply every term by the LCM of the denominators to clear fractions immediately.

---

## Similar Patterns

This is the inverse of the standard "Profit Sharing" pattern. In Profit Sharing, you are given Investment and Time to find Profit; here, you are given Profit and Time to find Investment.

---

## Revision Summary

**Key formula:** $\text{Investment} = \frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{Time}}$.

**Spot it by:** Looking for given profit ratios and time durations.

**Fastest method:** Set up $I \times T = P$ and solve for $I$ as a fraction of the first partner.

**Biggest trap:** Multiplying profit by time instead of dividing by it.
