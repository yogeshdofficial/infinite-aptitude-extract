# Ratio of Time

## Overview
These questions involve partners who contribute different capitals for different durations to earn a profit. The central idea is that the ratio of profit is always proportional to the product of capital and time ($P \propto C \times T$).

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Ratio of capital," "ratio of profit," "ratio of time," "duration of investment."
* **Given:** Capital ratios and profit ratios are provided.
* **Asked:** The ratio of the time periods for which the capitals were invested.
* **Visual Scan:** Look for three sets of ratios (Capital, Time, Profit) where two are given and one is missing.

---

## Important Formulas

### [Profit-Capital-Time Relationship]

$$
\text{Ratio of Profits} = (\text{Capital}_1 \times \text{Time}_1) : (\text{Capital}_2 \times \text{Time}_2) : (\text{Capital}_3 \times \text{Time}_3)
$$

*Meaning:* Profit share is the product of the investment amount and the duration it stays in the business.

*Use when:* You need to find the missing ratio of time by rearranging the equation: $\text{Time} = \frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{Capital}}$.

---

## Solution Framework
1. Write the profit ratio as a fraction of the capital ratio for each partner.
2. Simplify the resulting fractions (e.g., $\frac{P_1}{C_1} : \frac{P_2}{C_2} : \frac{P_3}{C_3}$).
3. Find the Least Common Multiple (LCM) of the denominators.
4. Multiply each fraction by the LCM to convert the ratio into whole numbers.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **The Reciprocal Trick:** If profits are equal, the ratio of time is simply the inverse ratio of the capitals ($T \propto \frac{1}{C}$).
* **Why it works:** Since $C_1T_1 = C_2T_2 = C_3T_3 = k$, then $T_1 = \frac{k}{C_1}$, $T_2 = \frac{k}{C_2}$, etc.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Inverting the Ratio:** Dividing Capital by Profit instead of Profit by Capital.
    * *Reason:* Confusing the variables in the formula $T = \frac{P}{C}$.
    * *Fix:* Always write $C \times T = P$ first, then isolate $T$.
* **Forgetting to Simplify:** Leaving the ratio in fractional form.
    * *Reason:* Stopping after the division step.
    * *Fix:* Always multiply by the LCM of denominators to get the final integer ratio.

---

## Similar Patterns
This is distinct from "Simple Partnership" where time is constant. If time is not mentioned, assume $T=1$ for all, but if time is provided as a variable, you must use the $C \times T$ framework.

---

## Revision Summary
**Key formula:** $\text{Time} = \frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{Capital}}$.

**Spot it by:** Given capital and profit ratios, asked for time ratio.

**Fastest method:** Divide profit ratio by capital ratio and multiply by LCM.

**Biggest trap:** Dividing Capital by Profit instead of Profit by Capital.
