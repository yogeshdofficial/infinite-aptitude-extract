# Capital Changes (Partnership)

## Overview
* **What it is:** A pattern involving the distribution of profits among partners based on their capital contributions and the duration of their investment.
* **Core Concept:** Profit is directly proportional to the product of **Capital** and **Time**.
* **Scope:** Covers simple partnerships (same time) and compound partnerships (varying time periods).

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Invested," "Joined after X months," "Withdrew," "Annual profit," "Ratio of shares."
* **Structure:** Multiple parties, different amounts, different time durations.
* **Given:** Individual capitals and time periods (or start/end dates).
* **To Find:** Individual profit shares, total profit, or required capital/time to achieve a specific ratio.

---

## Important Formulas

### Effective Capital (Investment Weight)
$$E = C \times T$$
* **Meaning:** The "weighted" value of money invested.
* **Use:** Always calculate this first for every partner.

### Profit Ratio
$$\frac{P_1}{P_2} = \frac{C_1 \times T_1}{C_2 \times T_2}$$
* **Meaning:** Profit distribution follows the ratio of effective capitals.
* **Use:** When comparing two or more partners.

### Individual Share
$$\text{Partner's Share} = \left( \frac{\text{Partner's Effective Capital}}{\text{Total Effective Capital}} \right) \times \text{Total Profit}$$
* **Use:** To find the exact monetary value of a share.

---

## Solution Framework
1. **Standardize Time:** Convert all time periods into the same unit (e.g., months).
2. **Calculate Effective Capital:** Multiply each partner's capital by their respective time duration ($C \times T$).
3. **Simplify Ratio:** Reduce the resulting values to their simplest integer ratio.
4. **Sum the Parts:** Add the ratio components to get the "Total Parts."
5. **Distribute:** Divide the total profit by "Total Parts" and multiply by each partner's ratio component.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Ratio Simplification:** Before multiplying, cancel common zeros or common factors in the capitals to save time.
* **The "12-Month" Rule:** If the business runs for a year, use the number of months invested as $T$. If someone joins after $x$ months, their $T = 12 - x$.
* **Constant Profit:** If profits are equal, the ratio of capitals is inversely proportional to the ratio of time ($C_1/C_2 = T_2/T_1$).
* **Mental Math:** If the ratio is $2:3:5$, the total parts are $10$. Simply move the decimal of the total profit to find the value of "1 part."

---

## Common Mistakes
1. **Ignoring Time:** Treating all investments as equal regardless of duration.
2. **Wrong Time Calculation:** Using the "time passed" instead of "time invested" (e.g., using 3 months instead of 9 months).
3. **Calculation Errors:** Forgetting to simplify the ratio before distributing profit.
4. **Unit Mismatch:** Mixing years and months in the same calculation.
5. **Partial Withdrawals:** Failing to split a partner's investment into two periods if they change their capital mid-year.

---

## Similar Patterns
* **Work & Wages:** Similar logic ($W = E \times T$), but uses efficiency instead of capital.
* **Mixture & Alligation:** Used when mixing different concentrations; distinguish by looking for "profit sharing" vs "percentage composition."

## Revision Summary

### Key Formula
$$\text{Profit Ratio} = (C_1T_1) : (C_2T_2) : (C_3T_3)$$

### Recognition Signal
"Multiple investors + varying time periods = Weighted Ratio."

### Main Trick
Simplify the $C \times T$ values by dividing by the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) before calculating shares.

### Common Trap
Using the time *elapsed* instead of the time *invested*. Always count months from the date of entry to the end of the term.
