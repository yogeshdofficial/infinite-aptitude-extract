# Working Partner Salary (Partnership)

## Overview
* **What it is:** A partnership model where profit is distributed based on the "Effective Capital" (Investment × Time).
* **Problem Types:** 
    1. **Simple:** Equal time periods (Profit ratio = Investment ratio).
    2. **Compound:** Different time periods (Profit ratio = Investment × Time ratio).
* **Central Idea:** Profit is always proportional to the product of capital and the duration it remained in the business.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Invested," "Joined after X months," "Left after Y months," "Annual profit," "Ratio of shares."
* **Given:** Individual investments and specific time durations for each partner.
* **To Find:** Individual profit shares or total profit distribution.
* **Structure:** Look for "After X months" — this is the trigger to calculate the remaining time for that partner.

---

## Important Formulas

### Effective Capital (EC)
$$EC = \text{Capital} \times \text{Time}$$
*Use this to normalize investments made for different durations.*

### Profit Ratio
$$\frac{P_1}{P_2} = \frac{C_1 \times T_1}{C_2 \times T_2}$$
*Use to find the ratio of profit distribution between two partners.*

### Individual Share
$$\text{Partner's Share} = \left( \frac{\text{Partner's EC}}{\text{Total EC}} \right) \times \text{Total Profit}$$
*Use to calculate the actual monetary value of a share.*

---

## Solution Framework
1. **Normalize Time:** Convert all time periods into the same unit (e.g., months).
2. **Calculate EC:** Multiply each partner's capital by the number of months it was invested.
3. **Simplify Ratio:** Reduce the EC values to their simplest integer ratio.
4. **Sum the Parts:** Add the ratio components to get the "Total Parts."
5. **Distribute:** Multiply the Total Profit by the fraction of each partner's ratio.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Zero Cancellation:** Before multiplying, cancel common zeros in the investment amounts (e.g., $120,000 : 150,000 \rightarrow 12 : 15$).
* **Time Symmetry:** If all partners invest for the same time, ignore time entirely; profit ratio = investment ratio.
* **Fractional Parts:** If the ratio is $2:3:5$, the total parts are $10$. Partner A gets $2/10$ (or $1/5$) of the profit.
* **Mental Check:** Ensure the sum of individual shares equals the total profit.

---

## Common Mistakes
1. **Ignoring Time:** Treating compound partnership as simple (forgetting to multiply by months).
2. **Wrong Time Calculation:** Using the "time joined" instead of "time invested" (e.g., if joined after 3 months, time = $12 - 3 = 9$ months).
3. **Ratio Inversion:** Mixing up the order of partners when assigning ratios.
4. **Calculation Errors:** Failing to simplify the ratio before distributing the profit.
5. **Unit Mismatch:** Mixing years and months (always convert to months).

---

## Similar Patterns
* **Work & Wages:** Similar to partnership, but based on efficiency/time rather than capital.
* **Mixture & Alligation:** Uses ratios to combine quantities; distinguish by looking for "investments" vs "ingredients."
