# Working Partner

## Overview
This pattern involves a partnership where one partner receives a fixed salary or percentage of the total profit for management duties before the remainder is distributed. The central idea is to isolate the "management fee" first, then distribute the remaining profit based on the capital-time ratio.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Working partner," "managing the business," "salary," "percentage of profit for management."
* **Given:** Total profit, individual capital investments, time periods, and the working partner's management percentage/fee.
* **Asked:** Total profit, or the individual share of a specific partner.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Total Share Formula]
$$
\text{Total Share} = \text{Management Fee} + \text{Proportional Share of Balance}
$$

**Variables:**
- **Management Fee:** The fixed amount or percentage of total profit given to the working partner.
- **Proportional Share:** The portion of the remaining profit based on the ratio of (Capital $\times$ Time).

**When to use:** To calculate the final payout for any partner.

**Worked example:** In Q31, Anu gets 12.5% of ₹ 880 (₹ 110) as a fee. Remaining profit is ₹ 770. Anu's proportional share is $\frac{5}{11} \times 770 = 350$. Total = $110 + 350 = 460$.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Step 1: Calculate Management Fee** — Find the fixed amount the working partner receives from the total profit.
2. **Step 2: Find Balance Profit** — Subtract the management fee from the total profit to get the amount to be shared.
3. **Step 3: Determine Investment Ratio** — Calculate the ratio of (Capital $\times$ Time) for all partners.
4. **Step 4: Distribute Balance** — Divide the balance profit among partners according to their investment ratio.
5. **Step 5: Finalize Shares** — Add the management fee back to the working partner's proportional share.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** Work with the "Balance Profit" ratio directly.
* **Why it works:** It avoids calculating large numbers for the total profit until the final step.
* **When to use:** When the question asks for the total profit given a difference between partners' shares.
* **Example:** In Q6, instead of calculating full shares, express everything in terms of $x$ (total profit) early: $A = 0.3x + \frac{5}{14}(0.7x)$.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Forgetting to add the management fee to the working partner's share of the balance.
* **Why it happens:** Treating the management fee as a separate entity rather than part of the partner's total income.
* **Fix:** Always write "Total Share = Fee + Proportional Share" at the start of your calculation.
* **Mistake:** Dividing the *total* profit by the investment ratio instead of the *balance* profit.
* **Why it happens:** Rushing through the steps and ignoring the "balance" condition.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** Anu is a working partner and Bimla is a sleeping partner. Anu puts in ₹ 5000 and Bimla puts in ₹ 6000. Anu receives 12.5% of the profit for managing the business and the rest is divided in proportion to their capitals. What does each get out of a profit of ₹ 880?

**Solution:**
1. **Management Fee:** Anu's fee = $12.5\% \text{ of } 880 = 0.125 \times 880 = 110$.
2. **Balance Profit:** $880 - 110 = 770$.
3. **Investment Ratio:** $5000 : 6000 = 5 : 6$. Total parts = 11.
4. **Distribute Balance:** 
   - Anu's share = $\frac{5}{11} \times 770 = 350$.
   - Bimla's share = $\frac{6}{11} \times 770 = 420$.
5. **Finalize:** 
   - Anu's total = $110 + 350 = 460$.
   - Bimla's total = $420$.

**Answer:** Anu gets ₹ 460, Bimla gets ₹ 420.

## Similar Patterns
* **Standard Partnership:** If there is no mention of a "working partner" or "management fee," simply distribute the total profit by the capital-time ratio.

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\text{Total Share} = \text{Fee} + \text{Proportional Share}$.
* **Spot it by:** Look for "working partner" or "management fee" in the text.
* **First move:** Subtract the management fee from the total profit immediately.
* **Fastest method:** Calculate the balance profit first, then distribute.
* **Biggest trap:** Forgetting to add the management fee to the working partner's final total.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Ratio Distribution:** Dividing a total amount $T$ into parts $a:b$ means the shares are $\frac{a}{a+b} \times T$ and $\frac{b}{a+b} \times T$.
* **Compound Partnership:** Profit is shared in the ratio of $(\text{Capital} \times \text{Time})$.

### Formulas You Must Know First
$$
\text{Share} = \frac{\text{Individual Ratio}}{\text{Sum of Ratios}} \times \text{Total Amount}
$$
**What it means:** This is the fundamental rule for splitting any profit based on investment ratios.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Working Partner:** A partner who receives extra compensation for managing the business.
* **Sleeping Partner:** A partner who only invests capital and does not manage the business.
* **Balance Profit:** The profit remaining after all fixed management fees have been paid out.
