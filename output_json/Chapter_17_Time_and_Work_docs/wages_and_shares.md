# Wages and Shares

## Overview
These questions involve distributing a total payment among workers based on their individual contribution to a job. The central idea is that **wages are directly proportional to the amount of work done**, which is determined by the efficiency (daily work rate) of each person.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "wages," "share," "paid," "earn," "piece of work," "together."
* **Given:** Time taken by individuals to complete a task, or the fraction of work done by pairs.
* **Asked:** The specific monetary share of one person or their daily earnings.
* **Pattern:** If you see a total amount and multiple people working, you are dealing with a ratio of work distribution.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Work-Share Proportion]
$$
\text{Share of A} = \frac{\text{Work done by A}}{\text{Total Work}} \times \text{Total Payment}
$$

**Variables:**
- $\text{Work done by A}$ = The fraction of the total task completed by A.
- $\text{Total Payment}$ = The total amount of money given for the job.

**When to use:** When the fraction of work done by each person is known or can be calculated.

**Worked example:** If A does $\frac{15}{23}$ of a work worth ₹ 529, then A's share = $\frac{15}{23} \times 529 = 345$.

### [Ratio of Wages]
$$
\text{A's Share} : \text{B's Share} = \text{A's Rate} : \text{B's Rate}
$$

**Variables:**
- $\text{Rate}$ = The amount of work done in one day (i.e., $\frac{1}{\text{Time taken}}$).

**When to use:** When you know the time taken by each person to complete the work.

**Worked example:** A takes 8 days, B takes 12 days. Rates are $\frac{1}{8} : \frac{1}{12}$. Multiply by 24 to get $3:2$. If total is ₹ 200, B's share = $\frac{2}{3+2} \times 200 = 80$.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Step 1: Find individual rates** — Calculate each person's daily work rate as $\frac{1}{\text{Time}}$.
2. **Step 2: Determine the ratio** — Express the work rates as a simplified ratio (e.g., $A:B:C$).
3. **Step 3: Calculate total parts** — Add the components of the ratio together to find the "total parts" of the work.
4. **Step 4: Distribute the payment** — Multiply the total payment by the fraction $\frac{\text{Individual Ratio}}{\text{Total Parts}}$.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** To find the ratio of two people, invert their time ratio.
* **Why it works:** Since $\text{Work} = \text{Rate} \times \text{Time}$, if work is constant, $\text{Rate} \propto \frac{1}{\text{Time}}$.
* **When to use:** When you are given only the time taken by two people.
* **Example:** A takes 3 days, B takes 2 days. Time ratio $3:2 \rightarrow$ Wage ratio $2:3$.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Using the ratio of time (e.g., 8:12) instead of the ratio of work (3:2).
* **Why it happens:** Forgetting that faster workers (less time) deserve more money.
* **Fix:** Always invert the time ratio to get the work/wage ratio.
* **Mistake:** Calculating the share of the wrong person.
* **Fix:** Double-check the question to see if it asks for A, B, or C's share.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** A can do a piece of work in 8 days and B can do the same in 12 days. They get ₹ 200 as combined wages. What is B's share?

**Solution:**
1. **Rates:** A's rate = $\frac{1}{8}$, B's rate = $\frac{1}{12}$.
2. **Ratio:** $\frac{1}{8} : \frac{1}{12}$. Multiply by 24 (LCM) $\rightarrow 3:2$.
3. **Total Parts:** $3 + 2 = 5$.
4. **B's Share:** B's ratio part is 2. Share = $\frac{2}{5} \times 200 = 80$.

**Answer:** ₹ 80.

---

## Similar Patterns
**Time and Work (Days/Hours):** These are identical in logic; the only difference is that "Wages" questions add a final step of multiplying the work fraction by a monetary value.

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\text{Share} = \frac{\text{Individual Ratio}}{\text{Total Ratio}} \times \text{Total Amount}$.
* **Spot it by:** Looking for "wages," "share," or "earnings" in a work problem.
* **First move:** Convert time taken into daily work rates (fractions).
* **Fastest method:** Invert time ratios to get wage ratios immediately.
* **Biggest trap:** Using the time ratio directly instead of the inverse work ratio.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Ratios** — Comparing quantities by division; $a:b$ is the same as $\frac{a}{b}$.
* **Fractions** — Adding and simplifying fractions like $\frac{1}{6} + \frac{1}{12}$.
* **LCM** — Finding the Least Common Multiple to simplify ratios of fractions.

### Formulas You Must Know First
$$
\text{Work} = \text{Rate} \times \text{Time}
$$
**What it means:** The total work done is the product of how fast you work and how long you work.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Efficiency** — The amount of work done in one unit of time (e.g., 1 day).
* **Share** — The portion of the total payment assigned to an individual.
