# Volume and Surface Area

## Overview
These questions involve calculating the physical properties (volume or surface area) of 3D objects like cubes and cylinders. The central idea is to identify the object's dimensions, ensure all units are consistent (e.g., mm to cm), and apply the correct geometric formula.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Total surface area," "Volume," "Weight," "Density," "Square base," "Right circular cylinder."
* **Given:** Dimensions (radius, height, side length), weight, or density.
* **Asked:** Find a missing dimension ($x$), the total surface area, or the volume.
* **Unit Check:** Look for mixed units (e.g., mm and cm) in the same question.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Volume of a Plate]
$$
\text{Volume} = \text{Area of base} \times \text{Height}
$$
**Variables:**
- $\text{Area of base}$ = $x^2$ (for square base)
- $\text{Height}$ = thickness of the plate

**When to use:** When given weight and density to find a missing dimension.

**Worked example:** Plate with side $x$ cm, thickness $1$ mm ($0.1$ cm). Volume $= x^2 \times 0.1$.

### [Total Surface Area of a Cylinder]
$$
\text{TSA} = 2\pi r(h+r)
$$
**Variables:**
- $r$ = radius of the base
- $h$ = height of the cylinder

**When to use:** When asked for the total surface area of a cylinder.

**Worked example:** $r=7, h=20$. $\text{TSA} = 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 7 \times (20+7) = 44 \times 27 = 1188$.

### [Cube Properties]
$$
\text{TSA} = 6a^2, \quad \text{Volume} = a^3
$$
**Variables:**
- $a$ = edge length of the cube

**When to use:** When given surface area to find volume, or vice versa.

**Worked example:** $\text{TSA} = 864$. $6a^2 = 864 \implies a^2 = 144 \implies a = 12$. $\text{Volume} = 12^3 = 1728$.

---

## Solution Framework
1. **Standardize Units:** Convert all dimensions to the same unit (e.g., mm to cm) before calculating.
2. **Identify the Object:** Determine if it is a cube, cylinder, or plate to select the correct formula.
3. **Set up the Equation:** Plug known values into the relevant formula (Volume or Surface Area).
4. **Solve for Unknown:** Use algebraic manipulation to isolate the variable ($x$ or $a$).
5. **Final Calculation:** Perform the final power or root operation to get the result.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Trick:** For cube problems, if $\text{TSA} = 6a^2$, always divide by $6$ first to get $a^2$.
* **Why it works:** It isolates the squared dimension immediately, simplifying the square root step.
* **When to use:** Any question asking for volume given surface area of a cube.
* **Example:** $864 / 6 = 144$; $\sqrt{144} = 12$.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Mistake:** Mixing units (e.g., using mm and cm together).
    * **Fix:** Convert everything to the unit requested in the answer (usually cm).
* **Mistake:** Using Lateral Surface Area ($2\pi rh$) instead of Total Surface Area ($2\pi rh + 2\pi r^2$).
    * **Fix:** Always add the area of the two circular bases for "Total Surface Area."
* **Mistake:** Confusing $6a^2$ (Area) with $a^3$ (Volume).
    * **Fix:** Remember: Area is 2D ($a^2$), Volume is 3D ($a^3$).

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** If the total surface area of a cube is 864 square cm, find the volume of the cube.

**Solution:**
1. **Identify:** Object is a cube. Formula: $\text{TSA} = 6a^2$.
2. **Equation:** $6a^2 = 864$.
3. **Solve for $a$:** $a^2 = 864 / 6 = 144$. So, $a = \sqrt{144} = 12$ cm.
4. **Calculate Volume:** $\text{Volume} = a^3 = 12^3 = 1728$ cm$^3$.

**Answer:** $1728$ cm$^3$.

---

## Similar Patterns
* **2D Geometry:** Distinguish by checking if the question asks for "Area" (2D) or "Volume/Capacity" (3D).

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\text{TSA}_{\text{cylinder}} = 2\pi r(h+r)$; $\text{TSA}_{\text{cube}} = 6a^2$.
* **Spot it by:** Keywords like "Volume," "Surface Area," or "Density."
* **First move:** Convert all units to match (e.g., mm to cm).
* **Fastest method:** Divide surface area by 6 for cubes before rooting.
* **Biggest trap:** Forgetting to add the two circular bases in cylinder surface area.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Density** — The ratio of mass to volume ($\text{Density} = \frac{\text{Mass}}{\text{Volume}}$).
* **Square Root** — Finding a number that, when multiplied by itself, equals the given value.

### Formulas You Must Know First
* **Weight/Mass** = $\text{Volume} \times \text{Density}$.
* **Area of a square** = $\text{side}^2$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Right circular cylinder** — A cylinder with circular bases perpendicular to the height.
* **Brass** — The material of the plate (irrelevant to geometry, but defines density).
