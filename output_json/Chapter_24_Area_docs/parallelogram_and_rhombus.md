# Parallelogram and Rhombus

## Overview
These questions involve calculating the area or dimensions of quadrilaterals (parallelograms, rhombi, or trapeziums). The central idea is to relate the given geometric properties (sides, diagonals, or height) to the specific area formula for that shape.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Parallelogram," "Rhombus," "Trapezium," "Parallel sides," "Diagonal," "Altitude," "Perpendicular distance."
* **Given:** Side lengths, diagonal lengths, height (altitude), or area.
* **Asked:** Missing side, missing diagonal, or total cost (Area $\times$ Rate).

---

## Key Formulas

### [Area of Parallelogram]
$$
\text{Area} = \text{base} \times \text{height}
$$
**Variables:**
- $\text{base}$ = length of one side
- $\text{height}$ = perpendicular distance to the opposite side

**When to use:** When you have a base and its corresponding altitude.
**Worked example:** Base = 150m, Height = 80m. Area = $150 \times 80 = 12000$ m$^2$.

### [Area of Rhombus (Diagonals)]
$$
\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times d_1 \times d_2
$$
**Variables:**
- $d_1, d_2$ = lengths of the two diagonals

**When to use:** When diagonal lengths are known or can be calculated.
**Worked example:** $d_1 = 24$cm, $d_2 = 32$cm. Area = $\frac{1}{2} \times 24 \times 32 = 384$ cm$^2$.

### [Area of Trapezium]
$$
\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times (a + b) \times h
$$
**Variables:**
- $a, b$ = lengths of parallel sides
- $h$ = perpendicular distance between them

**When to use:** When parallel sides and the height are provided.

---

## Solution Framework
1. **Identify the shape:** Determine if it is a parallelogram, rhombus, or trapezium.
2. **List knowns:** Write down the given values (e.g., $d_1$, base, height).
3. **Apply Pythagorean Theorem (if needed):** For a rhombus, use $side^2 = (\frac{d_1}{2})^2 + (\frac{d_2}{2})^2$ to find a missing diagonal.
4. **Set up the Area Equation:** Use the relevant formula to link the knowns to the unknown.
5. **Solve for the variable:** Perform the algebraic steps to isolate the missing dimension.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Trick:** In a rhombus, the side and half-diagonals always form a right-angled triangle.
* **Why it works:** Diagonals of a rhombus bisect each other at $90^\circ$.
* **When to use:** When you have the side length and one diagonal, and need the other.
* **Example:** Side 20, $d_1=24$. Half-diagonal = 12. $x = \sqrt{20^2 - 12^2} = 16$. Full $d_2 = 32$.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Mistake:** Using the full diagonal in the Pythagorean theorem. **Fix:** Always divide the diagonal by 2 first.
* **Mistake:** Confusing altitude with a side length. **Fix:** Altitude must be perpendicular to the base.
* **Mistake:** Forgetting the $\frac{1}{2}$ in the rhombus/trapezium area formula. **Fix:** Double-check the formula before calculating.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** Find the area of a rhombus one side of which measures 20 cm and one diagonal 24 cm.

**Solution:**
1. **Identify:** It is a rhombus.
2. **Knowns:** Side = 20, $d_1 = 24$.
3. **Pythagorean Step:** Half of $d_1$ is 12. Let half of $d_2$ be $x$. $x^2 + 12^2 = 20^2 \Rightarrow x^2 = 400 - 144 = 256 \Rightarrow x = 16$.
4. **Find $d_2$:** $d_2 = 2 \times 16 = 32$.
5. **Area:** $\frac{1}{2} \times 24 \times 32 = 384$ cm$^2$.

**Answer:** 384 cm$^2$.

---

## Similar Patterns
* **Rectangle/Square:** These are special parallelograms where the height is simply the adjacent side; use $L \times B$ instead of $Base \times Height$.

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** Area = $Base \times Height$ (Parallelogram) or $\frac{1}{2} d_1 d_2$ (Rhombus).
* **Spot it by:** Shape name and given dimensions (diagonals vs. sides).
* **First move:** Check if you need the Pythagorean theorem to find a missing diagonal.
* **Fastest method:** Use the $3-4-5$ triangle ratio for rhombus diagonals if applicable.
* **Biggest trap:** Using the full diagonal length in the Pythagorean theorem.

## Appendix: Prerequisites
### Concepts You Must Know
* **Pythagorean Theorem:** $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$ for right-angled triangles.
* **Algebraic Solving:** Solving linear equations like $a+b=50$ and $a-b=4$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Diagonal:** A line segment connecting two non-adjacent vertices.
* **Altitude:** The perpendicular height of a shape.
