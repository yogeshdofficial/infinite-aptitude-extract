# Basic Arithmetic

## Overview
These questions require evaluating numerical expressions involving addition, subtraction, multiplication, and algebraic identities. The central idea is to simplify complex expressions by grouping terms, using the distributive property, or applying binomial squares to avoid manual calculation errors.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Simplify," "Evaluate," "What value will replace the question mark," or "is equal to."
* **Given:** Long strings of numbers with mixed signs, or products of large numbers.
* **Asked:** The final numerical result or the value of an unknown variable ($x$ or $?$).
* **Structure:** Look for repeated numbers (suggests factoring) or numbers near round figures (suggests $(a \pm b)^2$ or $(a \pm b)(a \mp b)$).

---

## Key Formulas

### Distributive Property
$$a \times b \pm a \times c = a \times (b \pm c)$$
**Variables:** $a$ = common factor, $b, c$ = terms to be added or subtracted.
**When to use:** When the same number is multiplied by two different numbers and then added or subtracted.
**Worked example:** $986 \times 137 + 986 \times 863 \Rightarrow 986 \times (137 + 863) = 986 \times 1000 = 986000$.

### Binomial Squares
$$(a + b)^2 = a^2 + b^2 + 2ab$$
$$(a - b)^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab$$
**Variables:** $a$ = nearest round number, $b$ = difference from round number.
**When to use:** Squaring large numbers that are close to multiples of 10, 100, or 1000.
**Worked example:** $1605^2 = (1600 + 5)^2 = 1600^2 + 5^2 + 2(1600)(5) = 2560000 + 25 + 16000 = 2576025$.

---

## Solution Framework
1. **Group Terms:** Combine all positive numbers together and all negative numbers together to simplify addition/subtraction.
2. **Apply BODMAS:** Always solve brackets first, then multiplication, then addition/subtraction.
3. **Factorize:** If you see a common number repeated in multiplication, use the distributive property to factor it out.
4. **Use Round Numbers:** For multiplication or squares, express numbers as $(1000 \pm x)$ to simplify the math.
5. **Isolate Variable:** If solving for $?$, move all known constants to one side by changing their signs.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Trick:** Multiply by $999...$ using $(10^n - 1)$.
* **Why it works:** $a \times (10000 - 1) = 10000a - a$, which is just appending zeros and subtracting the original number.
* **When to use:** When multiplying by $9, 99, 999, 9999$.
* **Example:** $5793405 \times 9999 = 57934050000 - 5793405 = 57928256595$.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Mistake:** Forgetting the $2ab$ term in $(a+b)^2$.
    * **Why it happens:** Rushing the expansion.
    * **Fix:** Always write out $a^2 + b^2 + 2ab$ before calculating.
* **Mistake:** Sign errors when moving terms across the equals sign.
    * **Why it happens:** Forgetting to flip the sign (e.g., $+x$ becomes $-x$).
    * **Fix:** Explicitly write the sign change in your scratchpad.
* **Mistake:** Multiplying before adding in expressions like $6 \times 3(3-1)$.
    * **Why it happens:** Ignoring the bracket priority.
    * **Fix:** Solve the bracket $(3-1)$ first, then multiply.

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** $5566 - 7788 + 9988 = ? + 4444$

**Solution:**
1. **Group Terms:** Move $4444$ to the left side: $5566 - 7788 + 9988 - 4444 = x$.
2. **Combine Positives:** $5566 + 9988 = 15554$.
3. **Combine Negatives:** $-7788 - 4444 = -12232$.
4. **Final Calculation:** $15554 - 12232 = 3322$.

**Answer:** $3322$

## Similar Patterns
* **Algebraic Simplification:** If the question contains variables like $a^3 + b^3$, it is an algebraic identity problem, not basic arithmetic.

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $a(b \pm c) = ab \pm ac$ and $(a \pm b)^2 = a^2 + b^2 \pm 2ab$.
* **Spot it by:** Large numbers, repeated factors, or numbers near round values.
* **First move:** Group all positive terms and all negative terms separately.
* **Fastest method:** Use distributive property to factor out common numbers.
* **Biggest trap:** Forgetting the $2ab$ term in squares or sign errors during transposition.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **BODMAS** — The order of operations: Brackets, Orders, Division, Multiplication, Addition, Subtraction.
* **Distributive Property** — $a(b+c) = ab + ac$.
* **Transposition** — Moving a term to the other side of an equation changes its sign ($+$ becomes $-$, $-$ becomes $+$).

### Formulas You Must Know First
* **No prerequisite formulas** — all formulas needed are introduced above.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Numeral** — A symbol or group of symbols representing a number.
* **Face Value** — The digit itself, regardless of its position.
