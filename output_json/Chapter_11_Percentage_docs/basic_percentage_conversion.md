# Basic Percentage Conversion

## Overview
This pattern involves converting between percentages, fractions, decimals, and ratios. The central idea is that "percent" means "per hundred," allowing you to move between formats by multiplying or dividing by 100.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Express as a fraction," "Express as a decimal," "Express as a rate percent," "What percent of."
* **Given:** A number (fraction, decimal, or ratio) or a percentage value.
* **Asked:** The equivalent value in a different format (e.g., percentage to fraction).

---

## Key Formulas

### [Percentage to Fraction/Decimal]
$$
\text{Value} = \frac{x}{100}
$$
**Variables:** $x$ = the percentage value.
**When to use:** When you need to convert a percentage (e.g., 56%) into a fraction or decimal.
**Worked example:** For 56%, $x = 56$. So, $\frac{56}{100} = \frac{14}{25}$ or $0.56$.

### [Fraction/Decimal/Ratio to Percentage]
$$
\text{Percentage} = \text{Value} \times 100\%
$$
**Variables:** $\text{Value}$ = the given fraction, decimal, or ratio.
**When to use:** When you need to convert a non-percentage number into a percentage.
**Worked example:** For $\frac{3}{4}$, $\frac{3}{4} \times 100\% = 75\%$.

---

## Solution Framework

**Step 1: Identify the target format.** Determine if you are converting *to* a percentage (multiply by 100) or *from* a percentage (divide by 100).
**Step 2: Set up the operation.** Write the fraction or decimal clearly.
**Step 3: Execute the conversion.** Perform the multiplication or division.
**Step 4: Simplify.** If the result is a fraction, reduce it to its lowest terms.
**Step 5: Adjust decimals.** If converting to a decimal, ensure the decimal point is moved exactly two places.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** To convert a decimal to a percentage, move the decimal point two places to the right.
* **Why it works:** Multiplying by 100 is mathematically identical to shifting the decimal point two places right.
* **When to use:** When the input is a decimal like 0.004.
* **Example:** $0.004 \times 100 = 0.4\%$.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Moving the decimal point only one place.
    * **Why it happens:** Confusing "percent" (per 100) with "per 10."
    * **Fix:** Always remember "percent" = "per 100" (two zeros = two places).
* **Mistake:** Forgetting to simplify the final fraction.
    * **Why it happens:** Stopping after the initial division.
    * **Fix:** Always check if the numerator and denominator share a common factor.
* **Mistake:** Inverting ratios (e.g., 5:4 as 4/5).
    * **Why it happens:** Misreading the order of the ratio.
    * **Fix:** The first term is always the numerator.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** Express $0.6\%$ as a fraction.

**Solution:**
1. **Identify target:** Converting *from* a percentage to a fraction.
2. **Set up:** Use the formula $\frac{x}{100}$. Here, $x = 0.6$.
3. **Execute:** $\frac{0.6}{100}$.
4. **Simplify:** Multiply numerator and denominator by 10 to remove the decimal: $\frac{6}{1000}$.
5. **Reduce:** Divide both by 2: $\frac{3}{500}$.

**Answer:** $\frac{3}{500}$

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $x\% = \frac{x}{100}$ or $\text{Value} \times 100\%$.
* **Spot it by:** Looking for "Express as..." or "What percent...".
* **First move:** Determine if you are multiplying or dividing by 100.
* **Fastest method:** Shift decimal points two places (right for %, left for decimal).
* **Biggest trap:** Forgetting to simplify fractions or misplacing the decimal point.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Fraction simplification** — Dividing the numerator and denominator by their greatest common divisor.
* **Decimal place value** — Understanding that dividing by 100 shifts the decimal point two places to the left.

### Formulas You Must Know First
No prerequisite formulas — all formulas needed are introduced above.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Percent** — A ratio or fraction expressed as parts per 100.
* **Ratio** — A relationship between two numbers, written as $a:b$ or $\frac{a}{b}$.
