# Find Percentage

## Overview
This pattern involves calculating what portion one value represents of another. The central idea is to express the relationship as a fraction of "Part over Whole" and convert it to a percentage by multiplying by 100.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "What percent of X is Y", "Find the percentage", "Pass percentage".
* **Given:** Two values (a part and a whole) or a set of values to be summed.
* **Asked:** The percentage equivalent of the part relative to the whole.
* **Units:** Often involves different units (e.g., litres vs ml, minutes vs hours) that require conversion before calculation.

---

## Key Formulas

### Percentage Calculation
$$
\text{Percentage} = \left( \frac{\text{Part}}{\text{Whole}} \times 100 \right) \%
$$

**Variables:**
- **Part:** The specific value being measured.
- **Whole:** The total reference value (the "of" value).

**When to use:** When asked to find the percentage of one quantity relative to another.

**Worked example:** "2 is what percent of 50?" $\rightarrow$ Part = 2, Whole = 50. $\frac{2}{50} \times 100 = 4\%$.

### Percentage Error
$$
\text{Percentage Error} = \left( \frac{\text{Absolute Error}}{\text{Original Value}} \times 100 \right) \%
$$

**Variables:**
- **Absolute Error:** The difference between the measured/rounded value and the original value.
- **Original Value:** The true or starting value.

**When to use:** When asked for the percentage error of a rounded or estimated number.

**Worked example:** Original = 81.472, Rounded = 81.5. Error = 0.028. $\frac{0.028}{81.472} \times 100 \approx 0.034\%$.

---

## Solution Framework

**Step 1: Identify the "Whole"** — Locate the value following the word "of" or the total sum; this is your denominator.
**Step 2: Standardize Units** — Ensure both the part and the whole are in the same units (e.g., convert all to ml, seconds, or quintals).
**Step 3: Set up the Fraction** — Place the "Part" in the numerator and the "Whole" in the denominator.
**Step 4: Multiply by 100** — Perform the division and multiply by 100 to get the percentage value.
**Step 5: Simplify/Round** — Reduce the fraction or round the decimal as required by the question options.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Trick:** Use unit-less ratios.
* **Why it works:** If you convert both numbers to the same base unit, the units cancel out, leaving a pure number.
* **When to use:** Whenever units differ (e.g., Q5(iv): 2 tonnes vs 40 quintals).
* **Example:** 2 tonnes = 20 quintals. $\frac{40}{20} \times 100 = 200\%$.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Mistake:** Swapping the numerator and denominator. **Fix:** Always put the value after "of" in the denominator.
* **Mistake:** Forgetting to convert units. **Fix:** Convert the smaller unit to the larger or vice versa before dividing.
* **Mistake:** Using the wrong "Whole" in multi-part questions. **Fix:** Sum all parts and all wholes separately before dividing.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** What percent of 6.5 litres is 130 ml?

**Solution:**
1. **Identify:** Part = 130 ml, Whole = 6.5 litres.
2. **Standardize:** Convert 6.5 litres to ml. $6.5 \times 1000 = 6500$ ml.
3. **Fraction:** $\frac{130}{6500}$.
4. **Calculate:** $\frac{130}{6500} \times 100 = \frac{130}{65} = 2\%$.

**Answer:** 2%

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\frac{\text{Part}}{\text{Whole}} \times 100\%$.
* **Spot it by:** Looking for "What percent of X is Y".
* **First move:** Convert both values to the same unit.
* **Fastest method:** Simplify the fraction before multiplying by 100.
* **Biggest trap:** Dividing by the wrong number (the part instead of the whole).

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Unit Conversion** — Knowing that $1 \text{ litre} = 1000 \text{ ml}$, $1 \text{ minute} = 60 \text{ seconds}$, and $1 \text{ metric tonne} = 10 \text{ quintals}$.
* **Significant Digits** — Understanding how to round numbers to a specific precision.

### Formulas You Must Know First
* **Fraction to Percentage** — $\text{Fraction} \times 100 = \text{Percentage}$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Part** — The subset or specific value being compared.
* **Whole** — The total quantity or the reference value.
* **Absolute Error** — The magnitude of the difference between two values.
