# Percentage Calculation

## Overview
This pattern involves calculating a specific percentage of a given number or comparing multiple percentage-based values. The central idea is to convert the percentage into a fraction or decimal and multiply it by the base value.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "of", "what percent of", "evaluate", "greatest", "add up to".
* **Given:** A percentage (e.g., $12\%$, $16\frac{2}{3}\%$) and a base number.
* **Asked:** The numerical value of that percentage or a comparison between different formats (fractions, decimals, percentages).

---

## Key Formulas

### Percentage Value
$$
\text{Value} = \frac{\text{Percentage}}{100} \times \text{Total}
$$

**Variables:**
- $\text{Percentage}$ = the rate given (e.g., 12)
- $\text{Total}$ = the base number (e.g., 5000)

**When to use:** When asked to find "X% of Y".

**Worked example:** $12\%$ of $5000$: $\frac{12}{100} \times 5000 = 12 \times 50 = 600$.

---

## Solution Framework

**Step 1: Convert percentage to fraction/decimal.** Divide the percentage value by 100 to remove the $\%$ sign.
**Step 2: Simplify the fraction.** Reduce the fraction (e.g., $16\frac{2}{3}\% = \frac{50}{300} = \frac{1}{6}$) to make multiplication easier.
**Step 3: Multiply by the base.** Multiply the simplified fraction by the given total.
**Step 4: Perform arithmetic.** Complete the multiplication or division to reach the final value.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** Use standard fraction equivalents for common percentages.
* **Why it works:** It eliminates the need for long division or multiplication by 100.
* **When to use it:** When percentages like $16\frac{2}{3}\% (\frac{1}{6})$, $33\frac{1}{3}\% (\frac{1}{3})$, or $25\% (\frac{1}{4})$ appear.
* **Example:** $16\frac{2}{3}\%$ of $600 \rightarrow \frac{1}{6} \times 600 = 100$.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Multiplying by the percentage number directly (e.g., $12 \times 5000$) instead of dividing by 100.
* **Why it happens:** Forgetting the definition of "percent" (per hundred).
* **Fix:** Always write the percentage as $\frac{P}{100}$ before starting calculations.
* **Mistake:** Miscalculating mixed fractions (e.g., $16\frac{2}{3}\%$ as $\frac{16}{3}$).
* **Why it happens:** Incorrect conversion of mixed numbers to improper fractions.
* **Fix:** Convert $a\frac{b}{c}$ to $\frac{(a \times c) + b}{c}$ first.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** Evaluate: $16\frac{2}{3}\%$ of $600$ gm – $33\frac{1}{3}\%$ of $180$ gm.

**Solution:**
1. **Convert percentages:** $16\frac{2}{3}\% = \frac{50}{3} \times \frac{1}{100} = \frac{1}{6}$. $33\frac{1}{3}\% = \frac{100}{3} \times \frac{1}{100} = \frac{1}{3}$.
2. **Multiply by base:** $\frac{1}{6} \times 600 = 100$ and $\frac{1}{3} \times 180 = 60$.
3. **Subtract:** $100 - 60 = 40$.

**Answer:** $40$ gm.

## Similar Patterns
**Ratio/Fraction Comparison:** If asked to compare fractions and percentages, convert all values to decimals first to avoid confusion.

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\text{Value} = \frac{\text{Percentage}}{100} \times \text{Total}$.
* **Spot it by:** Looking for the "%" symbol and the word "of".
* **First move:** Convert the percentage to a fraction or decimal.
* **Fastest method:** Memorize common fraction equivalents for percentages.
* **Biggest trap:** Forgetting to divide the percentage by 100.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Fractions** — A way to represent a part of a whole, written as $\frac{\text{numerator}}{\text{denominator}}$.
* **Decimals** — A base-10 representation of fractions (e.g., $0.5 = \frac{1}{2}$).
* **BODMAS** — The order of operations: Brackets, Orders, Division, Multiplication, Addition, Subtraction.

### Formulas You Must Know First
$$
\text{Mixed Fraction to Improper} = \frac{(\text{Whole} \times \text{Denominator}) + \text{Numerator}}{\text{Denominator}}
$$
**What it means:** Converting $16\frac{2}{3}$ to $\frac{50}{3}$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Percent (%)** — A ratio expressed as a fraction of 100.
* **Base Value** — The total quantity from which a percentage is calculated.
