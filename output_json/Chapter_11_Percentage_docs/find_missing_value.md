# Find Missing Value

## Overview
This pattern involves finding an unknown quantity when a percentage relationship is provided. The central idea is to translate the word problem into a simple algebraic equation where the unknown is represented by $x$.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "What percent of...", "What is...", "Find the missing figure", "inclusive of tax".
* **Given:** A percentage value and a corresponding numerical value (or a relationship between two percentages).
* **Asked:** The total value (whole), the missing percentage, or a specific portion of the total.
* **Structure:** Look for phrases like "$P\%$ of $x = \text{Value}$" or "$x + P\%$ of $x = \text{Total}$".

---

## Key Formulas

### Basic Percentage Equation
$$
\frac{P}{100} \times x = V
$$
**Variables:** $P$ = percentage rate, $x$ = unknown number, $V$ = given value.
**When to use:** When a specific percentage of an unknown number is given.
**Example:** $9\%$ of $x = 63 \implies \frac{9}{100} \times x = 63 \implies x = 700$.

### Inclusive Value (Tax/Increase)
$$
x \times (1 + \frac{R}{100}) = T
$$
**Variables:** $x$ = original price, $R$ = tax/increase rate, $T$ = total price.
**When to use:** When the given value includes an added percentage (e.g., sales tax).
**Example:** Price with $9\%$ tax is $13407 \implies x(1.09) = 13407 \implies x = 12300$.

---

## Solution Framework
1. **Define the unknown:** Assign $x$ to the value you need to find.
2. **Translate to equation:** Convert the word statement into a mathematical equation using the formulas above.
3. **Isolate $x$:** Move all constants to one side by multiplying/dividing.
4. **Solve:** Perform the final arithmetic to find the value of $x$.
5. **Final check:** If the question asks for a secondary value (e.g., monthly income from annual), perform the final conversion.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Trick:** For "Difference between $A\%$ and $B\%$ is $V$", use $(A-B)\%$ of $x = V$.
* **Why it works:** Distributive property of multiplication over subtraction.
* **When to use:** When comparing two percentages of the same base number.
* **Example:** $31\%$ of $x - 13\%$ of $x = 576 \implies 18\%$ of $x = 576 \implies x = 3200$.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Mistake:** Calculating the percentage of the *total* instead of the *base*.
    * **Fix:** Always identify the "whole" ($x$) and set the equation as $\frac{P}{100} \times x$.
* **Mistake:** Forgetting to divide by 12 when converting annual to monthly income.
    * **Fix:** Re-read the question to ensure the units match the final requirement.
* **Mistake:** Adding tax by calculating $9\%$ of the *total* price.
    * **Fix:** Use $x(1 + \frac{R}{100})$ to ensure the tax is based on the original price.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** The price of a TV set inclusive of sales tax of 9% is ₹ 13407. Find its marked price.

**Solution:**
1. **Define:** Let the marked price be $x$.
2. **Translate:** The price inclusive of tax is $x + 9\%$ of $x = 13407$.
3. **Simplify:** $x(1 + 0.09) = 13407 \implies 1.09x = 13407$.
4. **Solve:** $x = \frac{13407}{1.09} = 12300$.

**Answer:** ₹ 12300.

## Similar Patterns
* **Profit and Loss:** Distinguished by the presence of "Cost Price" and "Selling Price" keywords rather than just "percentage of a number."

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\frac{P}{100} \times x = V$.
* **Spot it by:** Looking for "percentage of" or "inclusive of" phrases.
* **First move:** Assign $x$ to the unknown and write the equation.
* **Fastest method:** Use the difference of percentages for comparison problems.
* **Biggest trap:** Calculating percentage on the final total instead of the base.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Percentage:** A fraction with a denominator of 100.
* **Algebraic Isolation:** Moving terms across the equals sign by performing the inverse operation.

### Formulas You Must Know First
$$
\text{Fraction} = \frac{\text{Part}}{\text{Whole}}
$$
**What it means:** A percentage is simply a fraction scaled by 100.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Marked Price (M.P.):** The original price before tax is added.
* **Inclusive:** The final value after a percentage (like tax) has been added to the base.
