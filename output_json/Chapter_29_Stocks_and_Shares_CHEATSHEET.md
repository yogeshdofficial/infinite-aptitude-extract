# Stocks And Shares — Exam Cheat Sheet

## How To Solve Any Question In This Chapter
1. **Identify Goal:** Determine if you need Income, Investment, Market Value, or Comparison.
2. **Extract Data:** Note Face Value (FV), Market Price (MP), Dividend Rate, and Brokerage.
3. **Normalize:** Always convert MP to include/exclude brokerage (Buy: +; Sell: -).
4. **Select Pattern:** Match the question type to the Quick-Recognition Table below.
5. **Apply Formula:** Use the specific formula for the target variable.
6. **Sanity Check:** Ensure dividend is calculated on FV, not MP; ensure return is on Investment.

## Formula Bank

**[Income Calculation]**
$$\text{Income} = \text{Face Value} \times \frac{\text{Dividend Rate}}{100}$$
→ *produces: Annual dividend income*
→ *use when: FV and dividend rate known*

$$\text{Rate of Interest} = \frac{\text{Income}}{\text{Investment}} \times 100$$
→ *produces: Actual return percentage*
→ *use when: Comparing yield on investment*

**[Investment Comparison]**
$$\text{Return Percentage} = \frac{\text{Dividend Rate}}{\text{Market Price}} \times 100$$
→ *produces: Yield percentage*
→ *use when: Comparing two different stocks*

$$\text{Market Value} = \frac{\text{Dividend Rate}}{\text{Yield Rate}} \times \text{Face Value}$$
→ *produces: Required market price*
→ *use when: Target yield is given*

**[Mixed Investment]**
$$\text{Income} = \text{Investment} \times \frac{\text{Dividend Rate}}{\text{Market Price}}$$
→ *produces: Income from one part*
→ *use when: Splitting total investment*

**[Stock Reinvestment]**
$$\text{Net Sale Price} = \text{Market Price} - \text{Service Charge}$$
→ *produces: Actual cash received*
→ *use when: Selling existing stock*

$$\text{Net Purchase Price} = \text{Market Price} + \text{Service Charge}$$
→ *produces: Actual cost paid*
→ *use when: Buying new stock*

**[Stock Valuation]**
$$\text{Total Cost} = \text{Face Value} \times \frac{\text{Market Price} \pm \text{Brokerage}}{100}$$
→ *produces: Total cash required*
→ *use when: Buying/selling shares*

## Step Sequences

**Income Calculation:** Identify FV → Apply Dividend Rate → Adjust for Brokerage → Result.
**Investment Comparison:** Calculate Yield % for A → Calculate Yield % for B → Compare → Result.
**Mixed Investment:** Set variables → Form income equation → Solve for X → Result.
**Stock Reinvestment:** Calculate old income → Calculate proceeds → Reinvest proceeds → Compare incomes.
**Stock Valuation:** Adjust MP for brokerage → Multiply by quantity → Result.

---

## Fastest Tricks

* **Income Calculation:** Income is always Dividend Rate % of Face Value.
* **Investment Comparison:** Ratio of investments is inverse of ratio of yields.
* **Stock Valuation:** Cost per share = (FV $\pm$ Discount/Premium) + Brokerage.
* **Mixed Investment:** Use weighted average income method for rapid solving.

---

## Trap Watch

* **Income Calculation:** Dividend on MP → Use Face Value.
* **Investment Comparison:** Yield on FV → Use Market Price.
* **Mixed Investment:** Ignoring brokerage → Include in cost.
* **Stock Reinvestment:** Forgetting service charge → Subtract from sale.
* **Stock Valuation:** Subtracting brokerage when buying → Add brokerage.

---

## Last-Minute Reminders

Always calculate dividends based on the Face Value, never the Market Price.
Market Price is the price at which shares are bought or sold in the market.
Brokerage is added to the cost when buying and subtracted from proceeds when selling.
A "discount" reduces the Market Price, while a "premium" increases it.
The return on investment is always calculated relative to the total money invested.
