# Relative Speed: Opposite Direction

## Overview
This pattern involves two objects (trains, or a train and a person) moving toward each other. The central idea is that their **relative speed is the sum of their individual speeds**, which effectively reduces the time taken to cross each other.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "opposite direction," "towards each other," "running against," "passing each other."
* **Given:** Lengths of one or two objects, speeds of both objects.
* **Asked:** Time taken to cross, or the speed/length of one object.

---

## Key Formulas

### Relative Speed (Opposite)
$$
S_{rel} = S_1 + S_2
$$
**Variables:** $S_{rel}$ = combined speed, $S_1$ = speed of object 1, $S_2$ = speed of object 2.
**When to use:** Whenever two objects move toward each other.
**Example:** Train at 60 kmph and train at 48 kmph: $S_{rel} = 60 + 48 = 108$ kmph.

### Time to Cross
$$
T = \frac{D_{total}}{S_{rel}}
$$
**Variables:** $T$ = time, $D_{total}$ = sum of lengths of both objects, $S_{rel}$ = relative speed.
**When to use:** To find the duration of the crossing.
**Example:** Lengths 240m and 270m, $S_{rel} = 30$ m/s: $T = \frac{240+270}{30} = \frac{510}{30} = 17$ seconds.

---

## Solution Framework
1. **Sum the speeds:** Add the two speeds to get $S_{rel}$ (ensure both are in the same units).
2. **Convert units:** If speeds are in kmph, multiply by $\frac{5}{18}$ to get m/s.
3. **Sum the distances:** Add the lengths of both objects (if one is a man/pole, its length is 0).
4. **Calculate time:** Divide total distance by $S_{rel}$ to get the time in seconds.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Trick:** Use the ratio $18:5$ to jump between kmph and m/s.
* **Why it works:** $1 \text{ kmph} = \frac{1000 \text{ m}}{3600 \text{ s}} = \frac{5}{18} \text{ m/s}$.
* **When to use:** Every time you see kmph and need to calculate time in seconds.
* **Example:** $108 \text{ kmph} \times \frac{5}{18} = 6 \times 5 = 30 \text{ m/s}$.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Mistake:** Subtracting speeds instead of adding. **Fix:** Remember "Opposite = Add, Same = Subtract."
* **Mistake:** Forgetting to convert kmph to m/s. **Fix:** Always check if time is in seconds; if yes, speed must be in m/s.
* **Mistake:** Using only one train's length. **Fix:** Always add the lengths of both moving objects.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** Two trains 240m and 270m long are running towards each other at 60 kmph and 48 kmph. How much time will they take to cross each other?

**Solution:**
1. **Sum speeds:** $60 + 48 = 108$ kmph.
2. **Convert units:** $108 \times \frac{5}{18} = 30$ m/s.
3. **Sum distances:** $240 + 270 = 510$ m.
4. **Calculate time:** $T = \frac{510}{30} = 17$ seconds.

**Answer:** 17 seconds.

---

## Similar Patterns
**Relative Speed: Same Direction:** Distinguish by the keyword "same direction" or "overtaking," where you **subtract** the speeds ($S_1 - S_2$) instead of adding them.

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $S_{rel} = S_1 + S_2$ and $T = \frac{D}{S}$.
* **Spot it by:** "Opposite direction" or "towards each other."
* **First move:** Add the speeds together.
* **Fastest method:** Convert kmph to m/s using $\frac{5}{18}$ immediately.
* **Biggest trap:** Forgetting to add the lengths of both trains.

---

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Relative Speed** — The speed of one object as observed from another moving object.
* **Unit Conversion** — Converting kmph to m/s by multiplying by $\frac{5}{18}$.

### Formulas You Must Know First
$$
\text{Speed} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}}
$$
**What it means:** The fundamental relationship between motion variables.
**Example:** If a train covers 100m in 5s, its speed is $20$ m/s.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Relative Speed** — The effective speed when two objects are in motion.
* **Crossing Time** — The duration from the moment the fronts meet until the rears clear each other.
