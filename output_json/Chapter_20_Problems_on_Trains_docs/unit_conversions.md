# Unit Conversions

## Overview

This pattern involves converting speed values between kilometers per hour (km/hr) and meters per second (m/sec). The central idea is to use a constant conversion factor derived from the ratio of units to ensure the speed remains consistent across different measurement systems.

---

## Recognition Clues

* **Keywords:** "speed of", "kmph", "metres per second", "m/s", "same as".
* **Given:** A speed value in either km/hr or m/sec.
* **Asked:** The equivalent speed in the other unit.
* **Visual cue:** Look for the units attached to the number (e.g., "108 kmph").

---

## Key Formulas

### [Conversion: km/hr to m/sec]

$$
\text{Speed (m/sec)} = \text{Speed (km/hr)} \times \frac{5}{18}
$$

**Variables:**
- $\text{Speed (km/hr)}$ = The given speed in kilometers per hour.
- $\text{Speed (m/sec)}$ = The target speed in meters per second.

**When to use:** When the question provides speed in km/hr and asks for m/sec.

**Worked example:** Given 108 km/hr.
Calculation: $108 \times \frac{5}{18} = 6 \times 5 = 30$ m/sec.

### [Conversion: m/sec to km/hr]

$$
\text{Speed (km/hr)} = \text{Speed (m/sec)} \times \frac{18}{5}
$$

**Variables:**
- $\text{Speed (m/sec)}$ = The given speed in meters per second.
- $\text{Speed (km/hr)}$ = The target speed in kilometers per hour.

**When to use:** When the question provides speed in m/sec and asks for km/hr.

**Worked example:** Given 14 m/sec.
Calculation: $14 \times \frac{18}{5} = \frac{252}{5} = 50.4$ km/hr.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Identify the units:** Check if the given speed is in km/hr or m/sec.
2. **Select the factor:** Use $\frac{5}{18}$ to go to smaller units (m/sec) or $\frac{18}{5}$ to go to larger units (km/hr).
3. **Multiply:** Multiply the given value by the selected fraction.
4. **Simplify:** Perform the arithmetic to get the final result.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** Remember "Big to Small, multiply by Small fraction ($\frac{5}{18}$); Small to Big, multiply by Big fraction ($\frac{18}{5}$)."
* **Why it works:** km/hr is a larger unit than m/sec; multiplying by a fraction less than 1 ($\frac{5}{18}$) reduces the value, while multiplying by a fraction greater than 1 ($\frac{18}{5}$) increases it.
* **When to use it:** Every time you need to convert units to avoid memorizing which way the fraction goes.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Using the wrong fraction (e.g., $\frac{18}{5}$ when converting to m/sec).
    * **Why it happens:** Confusing the numerator and denominator.
    * **Fix:** Use the "Big to Small" logic trick above.
* **Mistake:** Arithmetic errors when multiplying by 3.6 (the decimal form of $\frac{18}{5}$).
    * **Why it happens:** Trying to do decimal multiplication under pressure.
    * **Fix:** Keep the fraction as $\frac{18}{5}$ or $\frac{5}{18}$ until the final step to simplify division first.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** A train moves with a speed of 108 kmph. Its speed in metres per second is?

**Solution:**
1. **Identify:** Given speed is 108 km/hr (larger unit).
2. **Select:** Need m/sec (smaller unit), so use $\frac{5}{18}$.
3. **Multiply:** $108 \times \frac{5}{18}$.
4. **Simplify:** $108 \div 18 = 6$. Then $6 \times 5 = 30$.

**Answer:** 30 m/sec.

## Similar Patterns

**Relative Speed Calculations:** These involve adding or subtracting speeds (e.g., $u+v$ or $u-v$). Distinguish by checking if the question asks for a *conversion* of a single speed or the *resultant* speed of two moving objects.

---

## Revision Summary

* **Key formula:** $\text{km/hr} \times \frac{5}{18} = \text{m/sec}$ and $\text{m/sec} \times \frac{18}{5} = \text{km/hr}$.
* **Spot it by:** Looking for speed units (kmph vs m/s).
* **First move:** Determine if you are moving to a smaller or larger unit.
* **Fastest method:** Use the "Big to Small" logic to pick the fraction.
* **Biggest trap:** Flipping the fraction $\frac{5}{18}$ and $\frac{18}{5}$.
