# Rate Calculation

## Overview
These questions involve finding the interest rate when the True Discount (T.D.) on a sum is equated to Simple Interest (S.I.). The central idea is that the True Discount on a sum is equivalent to the Simple Interest calculated on the Present Worth (P.W.) of that sum.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "True discount," "due ... hence," "sum due," "interest on ... is the same as the true discount."
* **Given:** Either the T.D. and time, or two different sums where the S.I. of one equals the T.D. of the other.
* **Asked:** The rate of interest ($R\%$).
* **Time constraint:** If you see "True discount" and "Simple interest" in the same sentence, you are in this pattern.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Present Worth from T.D.]
$$
P.W. = \frac{100 \times T.D.}{R \times T}
$$
**Variables:** $P.W.$ = Present Worth, $T.D.$ = True Discount, $R$ = Rate, $T$ = Time.
**When to use:** When you are given the T.D. and need to find the principal amount (P.W.) to calculate the rate.
**Worked example:** $T.D. = 168, R = 14, T = 2$. $P.W. = \frac{100 \times 168}{14 \times 2} = 600$.

### [Rate Calculation]
$$
R = \frac{100 \times S.I.}{P \times T}
$$
**Variables:** $S.I.$ = Simple Interest, $P$ = Principal (or P.W.), $T$ = Time.
**When to use:** Once you have identified the interest amount and the principal.
**Worked example:** $S.I. = 210, P = 750, T = 2$. $R = \frac{100 \times 210}{750 \times 2} = 14\%$.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Identify P.W.:** If given the "Sum due" and "T.D.", calculate $P.W. = \text{Sum} - T.D.$
2. **Equate Interest:** Treat the $T.D.$ as the $S.I.$ earned on the $P.W.$ for the given time.
3. **Apply Rate Formula:** Use $R = \frac{100 \times T.D.}{P.W. \times T}$ to find the rate.
4. **Verify Units:** Ensure time ($T$) is in years (e.g., 9 months = $\frac{9}{12}$ or $0.75$ years).

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Trick:** $R = \frac{100 \times T.D.}{P.W. \times T}$
* **Why it works:** It combines the definition of T.D. (interest on P.W.) directly into the standard S.I. rate formula.
* **When to use:** Always, as it bypasses intermediate steps.
* **Example:** For Q6, $T.D. = 960 - 750 = 210$. $R = \frac{100 \times 210}{750 \times 2} = 14\%$.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Mistake:** Using the "Sum due" as the Principal ($P$) in the interest formula.
    * **Why it happens:** Confusing the total amount with the present worth.
    * **Fix:** Always use $P.W. = \text{Amount} - T.D.$
* **Mistake:** Forgetting to convert months to years.
    * **Why it happens:** Overlooking the "per annum" requirement.
    * **Fix:** Always divide months by 12.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** The interest on ₹ 750 for 2 years is the same as the true discount on ₹ 960 due 2 years hence. If the rate of interest is the same in both cases, it is?

**Solution:**
1. **Identify P.W.:** The T.D. on ₹ 960 is the difference between the sum and its P.W. Since the S.I. on ₹ 750 equals the T.D. on ₹ 960, the P.W. of ₹ 960 is ₹ 750.
2. **Calculate T.D.:** $T.D. = 960 - 750 = 210$.
3. **Apply Formula:** $R = \frac{100 \times 210}{750 \times 2}$.
4. **Solve:** $R = \frac{21000}{1500} = 14\%$.

**Answer:** 14%

## Similar Patterns
**Simple Interest:** Distinguished by the absence of "True Discount" or "Due hence" terminology; uses total principal instead of P.W.

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $R = \frac{100 \times T.D.}{P.W. \times T}$
* **Spot it by:** "True discount" and "Sum due" keywords.
* **First move:** Calculate $P.W. = \text{Sum} - T.D.$
* **Fastest method:** Direct substitution into the rate formula.
* **Biggest trap:** Using the "Sum due" as the principal.

---

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Simple Interest ($S.I.$):** Interest calculated only on the principal amount, $S.I. = \frac{P \times R \times T}{100}$.
* **True Discount ($T.D.$):** The interest on the Present Worth.

### Formulas You Must Know First
$$
\text{Amount} = P.W. + T.D.
$$
**What it means:** The total sum due is the original value plus the interest earned on that value.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Present Worth ($P.W.$):** The value of a sum today that would grow to the "Sum due" at a given rate and time.
* **Sum Due:** The total amount to be paid at a future date.
