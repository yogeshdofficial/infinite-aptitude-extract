# Find SP from CP

## Overview
These questions provide the Cost Price (CP) and a percentage of profit or loss, asking for the final Selling Price (SP). The central idea is to treat the CP as 100% and calculate the SP as a direct percentage of that base.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Find S.P.", "at what price should he sell", "profit of X%", "loss of Y%".
* **Given:** A base cost price (sometimes including overheads/transportation) and a percentage gain or loss.
* **Asked:** The final selling price (SP).
* **Scan for:** Additional costs like "transportation", "installation", or "overhead expenses" which must be added to the initial purchase price first.

---

## Important Formulas

### Selling Price with Profit
$$
\text{S.P.} = \text{C.P.} \times \left( \frac{100 + \text{Gain}\%}{100} \right)
$$
*Meaning:* The SP is the CP increased by the profit percentage.
*Use when:* The problem states a percentage gain on the total cost.

### Selling Price with Loss
$$
\text{S.P.} = \text{C.P.} \times \left( \frac{100 - \text{Loss}\%}{100} \right)
$$
*Meaning:* The SP is the CP decreased by the loss percentage.
*Use when:* The problem states a percentage loss on the total cost.

---

## Solution Framework
1. **Calculate Total CP:** Sum the purchase price and all additional overheads (transportation, installation, etc.).
2. **Identify Percentage:** Note the gain (+) or loss (-) percentage.
3. **Convert to Multiplier:** Express the percentage as $(100 \pm \text{rate}) / 100$.
4. **Calculate SP:** Multiply the Total CP by the multiplier.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Percentage Multiplier:** Instead of calculating the profit amount and adding it, multiply the CP directly by $1.XX$ (for profit) or $0.XX$ (for loss).
* **Why it works:** It combines two steps (finding the profit/loss value and adding/subtracting it) into one single multiplication operation.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Ignoring Overheads:** Forgetting to add transportation or installation costs to the initial purchase price.
    * *Reason:* Focusing only on the first number provided in the question.
    * *Fix:* Always read the full question to ensure all "spent" amounts are included in the Total CP.
* **Miscalculating Percentages:** Applying the profit percentage to the wrong base or miscalculating the decimal.
    * *Reason:* Rushing the arithmetic.
    * *Fix:* Always write out the fraction $\frac{100 \pm P}{100}$ before calculating.

---

## Similar Patterns
This pattern is distinct; it is the inverse of "Find CP from SP." If the question gives SP and asks for CP, you must divide by the percentage multiplier instead of multiplying.

---

## Revision Summary
**Key formula:** $\text{S.P.} = \text{Total C.P.} \times \frac{100 \pm \%}{100}$.
**Spot it by:** Looking for "Find S.P." and a given percentage gain/loss.
**Fastest method:** Use a single decimal multiplier (e.g., $1.15$ for $15\%$ profit).
**Biggest trap:** Forgetting to add overhead expenses (transport/installation) to the initial cost.
