# Miscellaneous Profit Loss

## Overview
These questions involve multi-step business scenarios where you must track total costs and total revenues across multiple items or conditions. The central idea is to aggregate all individual costs and revenues into a single "Total C.P." and "Total S.P." before calculating the final profit or loss percentage.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Total outlay," "unsold articles," "two flats/items sold at same price," "overhead costs," "transportation/installation."
* **Given:** Multiple items with different cost prices, different profit/loss percentages per item, or additional expenses (overheads).
* **Asked:** Overall profit/loss amount or percentage for the entire transaction.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Total Cost Price (with Overheads)]
$$
\text{Total C.P.} = \text{Purchase Price} + \text{Overheads}
$$
**Variables:**
- $\text{Purchase Price}$ = base cost of the item
- $\text{Overheads}$ = additional costs like transportation, installation, or repairs

**When to use:** When a question mentions extra expenses incurred after buying an item.

**Worked example:** TV cost ₹ 12,500, transport ₹ 300, installation ₹ 800. $\text{Total C.P.} = 12500 + 300 + 800 = 13600$.

### [Equal Gain/Loss Percentage]
$$
\text{Loss}\% = \left(\frac{x}{10}\right)^2
$$
**Variables:**
- $x$ = the common percentage of gain and loss

**When to use:** When two items are sold at the same selling price, one at $x\%$ gain and one at $x\%$ loss.

**Worked example:** Two flats sold at same price, 16% gain and 16% loss. $\text{Loss}\% = (\frac{16}{10})^2 = 1.6^2 = 2.56\%$.

---

## Solution Framework

**Step 1: Calculate Total C.P.** — Sum all purchase prices and overheads to find the actual investment.
**Step 2: Calculate Individual S.P.** — Determine the selling price for each item or group using the given profit/loss percentage.
**Step 3: Calculate Total S.P.** — Sum the selling prices of all items to find the total revenue.
**Step 4: Find Net Profit/Loss** — Subtract Total C.P. from Total S.P. (Positive = Profit, Negative = Loss).
**Step 5: Calculate Percentage** — Use $\frac{\text{Net Profit/Loss}}{\text{Total C.P.}} \times 100$ if the percentage is requested.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** For "Equal Gain/Loss" problems, the result is *always* a loss.
* **Why it works:** The loss on the higher-priced cost base outweighs the gain on the lower-priced cost base.
* **When to use it:** When the question states two items are sold at the same price with identical gain/loss percentages.
* **Example:** If $x=10\%$, loss is $(\frac{10}{10})^2 = 1\%$.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Calculating profit/loss on the cost of a subset of items (e.g., only the 600 sold) instead of the total outlay (all 800).
* **Why it happens:** Forgetting that the cost of unsold items is part of the total investment.
* **Fix:** Always use the total quantity purchased to determine the denominator for profit percentage.
* **Mistake:** Assuming no gain/loss when percentages are equal.
* **Why it happens:** Intuition suggests they cancel out.
* **Fix:** Apply the $(\frac{x}{10})^2$ formula immediately.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** A manufacturer makes 800 articles at ₹ 1.50 each. He fixes the S.P. such that if 600 are sold, he makes 30% profit on his total outlay. He sells 620 articles. Find actual profit %.

**Solution:**
1. **Total C.P.:** $800 \times 1.50 = 1200$.
2. **Target S.P. for 600:** $1200 \times 1.30 = 1560$.
3. **S.P. per article:** $1560 / 600 = 2.60$.
4. **Total S.P. for 620:** $620 \times 2.60 = 1612$.
5. **Profit:** $1612 - 1200 = 412$.
6. **Profit %:** $(412 / 1200) \times 100 = 34 \frac{1}{3}\%$.

**Answer:** $34 \frac{1}{3}\%$

## Similar Patterns
**Standard Profit/Loss:** Distinguish by the presence of "overheads" or "multiple items/conditions" which require aggregating totals first.

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\text{Loss}\% = (\frac{x}{10})^2$ for equal gain/loss.
* **Spot it by:** Multiple items or overhead costs mentioned.
* **First move:** Calculate the total investment (Total C.P.).
* **Fastest method:** Aggregate all revenues and costs before calculating net change.
* **Biggest trap:** Ignoring the cost of unsold items in the total outlay.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Percentage:** Understanding that $x\%$ of $Y$ is $\frac{x}{100} \times Y$.
* **Overhead Costs:** Expenses added to the base cost to determine the true cost price.

### Formulas You Must Know First
$$
\text{S.P.} = \text{C.P.} \times (1 \pm \frac{r}{100})
$$
**What it means:** Selling price is the cost price adjusted by the profit ($+$) or loss ($-$) percentage.
**Example:** C.P. 100, 20% profit $\rightarrow$ S.P. $= 100 \times 1.20 = 120$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Outlay:** The total amount of money spent (Total C.P.).
* **Overhead:** Extra costs like shipping or installation.
