# Dishonest Dealer

## Overview
This pattern involves a seller who uses false weights or scales to deceive customers while claiming to sell at cost price or a specific profit/loss. The central idea is that the dealer's profit is generated by the difference between the weight they *claim* to sell and the weight they *actually* provide.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "dishonest dealer," "false weight," "uses a weight of X instead of Y," "professes to sell at cost price."
* **Given:** The weight actually used (e.g., 960g) vs. the true weight (1000g), or a percentage of weight reduction.
* **Asked:** The actual gain or loss percentage.
* **Twist:** Sometimes the dealer also adjusts the price (e.g., "mixes water" or "increases price by X%").

---

## Key Formulas

### [Gain Percentage for False Weights]
$$
\text{Gain}\% = \left( \frac{\text{Error}}{\text{Actual Weight Provided}} \times 100 \right)
$$

**Variables:**
- $\text{Error}$ = $\text{True Weight} - \text{Actual Weight Provided}$
- $\text{Actual Weight Provided}$ = The weight the dealer actually gives the customer.

**When to use:** When the dealer claims to sell at cost price but uses a lighter weight.

**Worked example:** Dealer uses 960g for 1000g. Error = $1000 - 960 = 40$. Gain% = $\frac{40}{960} \times 100 = 4 \frac{1}{6}\%$.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Step 1: Identify the "Actual" vs "Claimed" weight.** Determine exactly how much product is given versus how much is charged.
2. **Step 2: Calculate the Error.** Subtract the actual weight from the true weight (usually 1000g or 100cm).
3. **Step 3: Determine the Cost Basis.** Remember that profit is calculated on the cost of the goods *actually provided* (the false weight).
4. **Step 4: Apply the Gain Formula.** Divide the error by the actual weight provided and multiply by 100.
5. **Step 5: Adjust for Price Changes (if applicable).** If the dealer also changes the price or adds impurities, calculate the total S.P. and total C.P. separately before finding the profit.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** If selling at cost price, $\text{Gain}\% = \frac{\text{Error}}{\text{False Weight}} \times 100$.
* **Why it works:** The dealer effectively "buys" the false weight amount and "sells" it for the price of the true weight.
* **When to use it:** Only when the dealer claims to sell at "cost price."
* **Example:** 90cm scale used for 100cm. Error = 10. Gain% = $\frac{10}{90} \times 100 = 11 \frac{1}{9}\%$.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Dividing the error by 1000 (the true weight).
    * **Why it happens:** Confusing the "claimed" amount with the "actual" cost basis.
    * **Fix:** Always divide by the weight the dealer *actually* gave away.
* **Mistake:** Ignoring the weight of added impurities (like water).
    * **Why it happens:** Treating the mixture as pure milk.
    * **Fix:** Add the weight of the impurity to the total quantity before calculating the new S.P.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** A dishonest dealer uses a scale of 90 cm instead of a metre scale and claims to sell at cost price. His profit is?

**Solution:**
1. **Identify:** True weight = 100 cm. Actual weight provided = 90 cm.
2. **Error:** $100 - 90 = 10$ cm.
3. **Cost Basis:** The dealer only spent money to provide 90 cm of cloth.
4. **Formula:** $\text{Gain}\% = \frac{\text{Error}}{\text{Actual Weight}} \times 100 = \frac{10}{90} \times 100$.
5. **Calculation:** $\frac{1}{9} \times 100 = 11 \frac{1}{9}\%$.

**Answer:** $11 \frac{1}{9}\%$

---

## Similar Patterns
**Profit and Loss (Standard):** Distinguish by the absence of "false weights" or "scales." If the question mentions weights or scales, it is a Dishonest Dealer problem.

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\text{Gain}\% = (\text{Error} / \text{Actual Weight}) \times 100$.
* **Spot it by:** Looking for "false weight," "scale," or "cheating" keywords.
* **First move:** Calculate the actual weight provided vs. the claimed weight.
* **Fastest method:** Use the error-to-false-weight ratio for "sell at cost" problems.
* **Biggest trap:** Dividing the error by the true weight (1000g) instead of the false weight.

---

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Cost Price (C.P.)** — The amount spent to acquire the goods.
* **Selling Price (S.P.)** — The amount received from the customer.
* **Profit %** — $\frac{S.P. - C.P.}{C.P.} \times 100$.

### Formulas You Must Know First
* **Profit = S.P. - C.P.**
* **S.P. = C.P. $\times (1 + \text{Profit}\%)$**

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **True Weight** — The standard weight (e.g., 1000g or 100cm).
* **False Weight** — The actual amount provided by the dealer.
