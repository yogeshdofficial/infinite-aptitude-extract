# Profit Loss Percent

## Overview
These questions involve calculating the percentage gain or loss based on the relationship between Cost Price (C.P.) and Selling Price (S.P.). The central idea is that all percentage gains or losses are calculated strictly relative to the **Cost Price**.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Purchased for", "Sold for", "Gain/Profit %", "Loss %".
* **Given:** C.P. and S.P. (or Gain/Loss amount).
* **Asked:** Percentage profit or percentage loss.
* **Scan for:** "In terms of percentage profit, which is the best transaction?" (requires comparing multiple ratios).

---

## Important Formulas

### [Percentage Gain/Loss]
$$
\text{Gain/Loss \%} = \left( \frac{\text{Gain or Loss}}{\text{C.P.}} \times 100 \right) \%
$$
*Meaning:* The ratio of the absolute profit/loss to the original investment.
*Use when:* You have both C.P. and S.P. and need the percentage.

### [S.P. from C.P. and Gain %]
$$
\text{S.P.} = \text{C.P.} \times \left( \frac{100 + \text{Gain \%}}{100} \right)
$$
*Meaning:* Expressing S.P. as a percentage of C.P.
*Use when:* You need to find the selling price given the cost and profit percentage.

---

## Solution Framework
1. Identify C.P. and S.P. from the problem statement.
2. Calculate the absolute difference: $\text{Profit} = \text{S.P.} - \text{C.P.}$ or $\text{Loss} = \text{C.P.} - \text{S.P.}$
3. If only S.P. and Gain amount are given, find $\text{C.P.} = \text{S.P.} - \text{Gain}$.
4. Divide the result by C.P.
5. Multiply by 100 to convert to percentage.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Fraction Comparison:** To compare transactions, convert them to fractions $\frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{C.P.}}$ and compare values.
* **Mental Approximation:** If numbers are complex (e.g., 2090.42 and 2602.58), round to the nearest hundred or thousand to estimate the percentage quickly before calculating the exact value.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Denominator Error:** Using S.P. instead of C.P. as the denominator.
    * *Reason:* Habit of calculating percentage based on the "total" or "final" value.
    * *Fix:* Always write "C.P." in the denominator before starting the calculation.
* **Missing C.P.:** Assuming the given value is C.P. when it is actually S.P.
    * *Reason:* Misreading "By selling an article for ₹ 100".
    * *Fix:* Explicitly label "C.P. =" and "S.P. =" on your scratchpad before calculating.

---

## Similar Patterns
This pattern is distinct, but often confused with "False Weight" problems. Remember: Profit/Loss Percent is about price differences, while False Weight is about mass/quantity differences.

---

## Revision Summary
**Key formula:** $\text{Profit/Loss \%} = (\frac{\text{Difference}}{\text{C.P.}}) \times 100$.
**Spot it by:** Looking for "Purchased/Sold" and "Percentage" in the same question.
**Fastest method:** Simplify the fraction $\frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{C.P.}}$ before multiplying by 100.
**Biggest trap:** Using S.P. as the base for the percentage calculation.
