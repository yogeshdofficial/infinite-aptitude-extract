# Data Interpretation: P&L (Railway/Transport)

## Overview
These questions involve calculating total revenue, total costs, and profit margins across multiple categories (classes) of a transport system. The central idea is to treat each class as an independent unit: $\text{Profit} = (\text{Fare} \times \text{Occupancy}) - (\text{Running Cost})$.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Full occupancy," "running cost," "profit margin," "fare collected."
* **Given:** Number of bogies, capacity per bogie, fare per passenger, and running cost per bogie.
* **Asked:** Total profit, highest profit margin, or percentage share of profit.
* **Structure:** A table or list providing distinct variables for different categories (e.g., AC-1, AC-2, 3-tier).

---

## Important Formulas

### [Total Revenue]
$$
\text{Revenue} = \text{Number of Bogies} \times \text{Capacity} \times \text{Fare}
$$
*Meaning:* Total income generated by a specific class.
*Use when:* Calculating the gross inflow for a specific category or the entire train.

### [Profit Margin]
$$
\text{Profit} = \text{Total Revenue} - \text{Total Running Cost}
$$
*Meaning:* Net gain after subtracting operational expenses.
*Use when:* Comparing the profitability of different classes or calculating total system profit.

### [Percentage Profit Share]
$$
\text{Percentage} = \left( \frac{\text{Profit of Class}}{\text{Total Profit}} \right) \times 100
$$
*Meaning:* The relative contribution of a specific class to the total profit.
*Use when:* Asked for the "percentage of total profit" coming from a specific segment.

---

## Solution Framework
1. Calculate **Total Revenue** for each class (Bogies $\times$ Capacity $\times$ Fare).
2. Calculate **Total Running Cost** for each class (Bogies $\times$ Cost per bogie).
3. Subtract Cost from Revenue to find **Profit per class**.
4. Sum individual profits to find **Total Profit** (if required).
5. Perform final division/percentage calculation based on the specific question.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Unit Elimination:** When comparing profit margins, ignore the number of bogies if the question asks for "highest profit margin" and the number of bogies is identical across classes.
* **Approximation:** In DI questions with large numbers, round off to the nearest hundred or thousand to simplify division, as options are usually spread out.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Cost Allocation:** Forgetting to multiply the running cost by the number of bogies for each specific class.
* **Denominator Error:** Calculating profit percentage as a fraction of Revenue instead of Cost (or vice versa) when the question specifies the base.
* **Partial Sums:** Calculating profit for only one class when the question asks for the total profit of the entire train.

---

## Similar Patterns
This is distinct from standard P&L problems because it involves **multi-variable aggregation** (summing multiple classes) rather than a single item transaction.

---

## Revision Summary
**Key formula:** $\text{Profit} = (\text{Revenue}) - (\text{Running Cost})$.
**Spot it by:** Multiple categories (classes) with individual capacities and costs.
**Fastest method:** Calculate profit per class first, then sum them up.
**Biggest trap:** Forgetting to multiply the running cost by the number of bogies.
