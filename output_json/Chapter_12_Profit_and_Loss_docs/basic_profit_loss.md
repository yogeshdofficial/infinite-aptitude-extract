# Basic Profit/Loss

## Overview
These questions involve calculating percentage gain or loss based on the relationship between Cost Price (C.P.) and Selling Price (S.P.). The central idea is that all profit or loss percentages must be calculated relative to the C.P., not the S.P.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "purchased for," "sold for," "gain percent," "loss percent," "at the rate of."
* **Given:** C.P. and S.P. (either as totals or per-unit rates).
* **Asked:** Profit % or Loss %; sometimes S.P. given a target profit %.
* **Hidden C.P.:** Look for "repairs," "transport," or "overhead" costs added to the initial purchase price.

---

## Important Formulas

### [Profit/Loss Percentage]
$$
\text{Profit \%} = \left( \frac{\text{S.P.} - \text{C.P.}}{\text{C.P.}} \times 100 \right) \%
$$
*Meaning:* Profit divided by C.P. multiplied by 100.
*Use when:* You have both C.P. and S.P. and need the percentage.

### [Selling Price from Profit %]
$$
\text{S.P.} = \text{C.P.} \times \left( 1 + \frac{\text{Profit \%}}{100} \right)
$$
*Meaning:* S.P. is a percentage of C.P. (e.g., 8% profit means S.P. is 108% of C.P.).
*Use when:* You have C.P. and a target profit percentage.

---

## Solution Framework
1. **Standardize:** Ensure C.P. and S.P. are for the same quantity (e.g., per 1 kg or per 1 item).
2. **Include Overheads:** Add any repair or extra costs to the initial purchase price to get the *Total C.P.*
3. **Calculate Difference:** Find the absolute Profit (S.P. - C.P.) or Loss (C.P. - S.P.).
4. **Apply Formula:** Divide the result by the *Total C.P.* and multiply by 100.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Percentage Comparison:** To find the "best transaction," compare the ratio $\frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{C.P.}}$ directly; the largest fraction wins.
* **Mental Approximation:** If C.P. is 2000 and Profit is 500, think $\frac{500}{2000} = \frac{1}{4} = 25\%$. Avoid long division when numbers are multiples.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Denominator Error:** Using S.P. instead of C.P. as the denominator.
    * *Reason:* Habitual focus on the final sale price.
    * *Fix:* Always write "C.P. =" at the start of your scratchpad.
* **Ignoring Overheads:** Forgetting to add repair/extra costs to the C.P.
    * *Reason:* Overlooking secondary costs mentioned in the problem.
    * *Fix:* Read the question twice specifically looking for "spent on," "repairs," or "transport."
* **Unit Mismatch:** Calculating profit on 1 item but using the total C.P. for 10 items.
    * *Reason:* Rushing the calculation without checking quantity consistency.
    * *Fix:* Explicitly write "C.P. of 1 unit" vs "C.P. of total" before calculating.

---

## Similar Patterns
This pattern is distinct; however, it is often confused with "False Weight" problems. If the question mentions "weights" or "scales," it is not a basic profit/loss problem and requires the specific false-weight formulas.

---

## Revision Summary
**Key formula:** $\text{Profit \%} = (\text{Profit} / \text{C.P.}) \times 100$.
**Spot it by:** "Purchased for" vs "Sold for" values.
**Fastest method:** Simplify the ratio $\frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{C.P.}}$ before multiplying by 100.
**Biggest trap:** Using S.P. as the denominator instead of C.P.
