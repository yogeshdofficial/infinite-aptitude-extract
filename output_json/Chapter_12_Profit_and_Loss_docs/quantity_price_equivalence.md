# Quantity-Price Equivalence

## Overview
These questions involve comparing the cost price (CP) and selling price (SP) when the number of items bought does not match the number of items sold. The central idea is to **normalize the quantity** (make the number of items equal) to directly compare the total CP and total SP.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "SP of X items is equal to CP of Y items", "bought at A for B, sold at C for D".
* **Given:** Two different sets of quantities and their respective prices.
* **Asked:** Profit or loss percentage, or the number of items to be sold for a specific price to achieve a target profit.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Profit/Loss Percent]
$$
\text{Profit\%} = \left( \frac{\text{SP} - \text{CP}}{\text{CP}} \times 100 \right) \% \quad \text{or} \quad \text{Loss\%} = \left( \frac{\text{CP} - \text{SP}}{\text{CP}} \times 100 \right) \%
$$
**Variables:** CP = Total Cost Price, SP = Total Selling Price.
**When to use:** When you have calculated the total CP and SP for an equal number of items.
**Example:** If CP of 30 items is ₹30 and SP is ₹25, Loss% = $\frac{30-25}{30} \times 100 = 16\frac{2}{3}\%$.

### [Unitary Method for Quantity]
$$
\text{Quantity} = \frac{\text{Total Price}}{\text{Price per unit}}
$$
**Variables:** Total Price = Amount spent/received, Price per unit = Cost/Selling price of one item.
**When to use:** When you need to find how many items can be bought/sold for a specific amount after accounting for profit/loss.

---

## Solution Framework

**Step 1: Equalize the quantity** — Find the LCM of the item counts to create a common base for comparison.
**Step 2: Calculate total CP and SP** — Determine the total cost and total revenue for that common quantity.
**Step 3: Find Profit or Loss** — Subtract the smaller value from the larger one to find the absolute gain or loss.
**Step 4: Calculate Percentage** — Divide the gain/loss by the **Total CP** and multiply by 100.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** If SP of $X$ items = CP of $Y$ items, then Profit/Loss % = $\frac{Y-X}{X} \times 100$.
* **Why it works:** It mathematically simplifies the process of assuming CP of 1 item = ₹1 and comparing the totals.
* **When to use:** Only for questions phrased exactly as "SP of X = CP of Y".
* **Example:** Q11: SP of 30 = CP of 25. $\frac{25-30}{30} \times 100 = -16.66\%$ (Negative indicates loss).

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Calculating percentage based on SP instead of CP.
    * **Fix:** Always divide by the Cost Price (the amount invested).
* **Mistake:** Mixing up the quantity and the price.
    * **Fix:** Clearly label your columns as "Quantity" and "Price" before calculating.
* **Mistake:** Applying profit % to the quantity instead of the price.
    * **Fix:** Profit % always modifies the monetary value (Price).

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** A vendor bought bananas at 6 for ₹10 and sold them at 4 for ₹6. Find his gain or loss percent.

**Solution:**
1. **Equalize:** LCM of 6 and 4 is 12.
2. **Calculate CP:** For 12 bananas, CP = $\frac{10}{6} \times 12 = ₹20$.
3. **Calculate SP:** For 12 bananas, SP = $\frac{6}{4} \times 12 = ₹18$.
4. **Find Loss:** Since CP (20) > SP (18), Loss = $20 - 18 = ₹2$.
5. **Percentage:** Loss% = $\frac{2}{20} \times 100 = 10\%$.

**Answer:** 10% Loss.

## Similar Patterns
**False Weight Problems:** Distinguished by the mention of "false weights" or "grams" instead of just item counts; these require specific weight-based formulas (see Appendix XIII-XV).

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\text{Profit/Loss\%} = \frac{\text{Difference}}{\text{CP}} \times 100$.
* **Spot it by:** Comparing quantities of items bought vs. sold.
* **First move:** Find the LCM of the quantities to normalize the set.
* **Fastest method:** Use the LCM method to find total CP and SP.
* **Biggest trap:** Dividing the profit/loss by the Selling Price.

---

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **LCM (Least Common Multiple)** — The smallest number divisible by two or more given numbers.
* **Unitary Method** — Finding the value of one unit and then multiplying to find the value of the required number of units.

### Formulas You Must Know First
$$
\text{SP} = \text{CP} \times \left( 1 + \frac{\text{Profit\%}}{100} \right)
$$
**What it means:** Converts Cost Price to Selling Price based on a percentage gain.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **CP (Cost Price)** — The amount paid to acquire an item.
* **SP (Selling Price)** — The amount received after selling an item.
