# Relative Price & Profit

## Overview
These questions involve expressing Cost Price (C.P.) and Selling Price (S.P.) as fractions or percentages of each other rather than using absolute values. The central idea is to assign a variable (usually $x$ or $100$) to one price and express the other in terms of that variable to calculate the percentage change.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "C.P. is $n\%$ of S.P.", "sold at $\frac{a}{b}$ of its price", "ratio of C.P. to S.P.", "profit/loss is a fraction of S.P."
* **Given:** A relationship between C.P. and S.P. (ratio, fraction, or percentage).
* **Asked:** Profit percentage, loss percentage, or the ratio of C.P. to S.P.

---

## Important Formulas

### Profit/Loss Percentage
$$
\text{Profit/Loss \%} = \left| \frac{\text{S.P.} - \text{C.P.}}{\text{C.P.}} \right| \times 100
$$
*Meaning:* The difference between S.P. and C.P. divided by the base C.P.
*Use when:* You have both C.P. and S.P. values or their ratio.

### S.P. in terms of C.P.
$$
\text{S.P.} = \text{C.P.} \times \left(1 \pm \frac{r}{100}\right)
$$
*Meaning:* $r$ is the profit (+) or loss (-) percentage.
*Use when:* You need to find the new S.P. after a percentage change.

---

## Solution Framework
1. **Assume a base:** Assign $100$ or $x$ to the denominator value (e.g., if C.P. is $\frac{4}{3}$ of S.P., let S.P. $= 100$).
2. **Define the other:** Calculate the second price based on the given fraction or percentage.
3. **Find the difference:** Calculate the absolute profit or loss (S.P. $-$ C.P.).
4. **Calculate percentage:** Divide the difference by the **C.P.** and multiply by $100$.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Ratio Shortcut:** If C.P. : S.P. $= a : b$, then Profit $\% = \frac{b-a}{a} \times 100$.
* **Why:** It bypasses the need for variables $x$ by using the ratio directly as the actual values.
* **S.P. Base Trick:** If profit/loss is given as a percentage of **S.P.**, add/subtract the percentage from $100$ to find C.P. (e.g., $10\%$ profit on S.P. means C.P. $= 90\%$ of S.P.).

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Mistake:** Calculating profit/loss percentage on S.P. instead of C.P.
    * *Reason:* Habitual use of the denominator in the fraction provided in the question.
    * *Fix:* Always verify the base is C.P. unless the question explicitly states "profit on S.P."
* **Mistake:** Confusing "sold at $\frac{2}{3}$ of price" with "sold at $\frac{2}{3}$ profit".
    * *Reason:* Misreading the relationship between the price and the multiplier.
    * *Fix:* Read the phrase "of its price" as a direct multiplier for the S.P.

---

## Similar Patterns
* **False Weight Patterns:** These are distinct because they involve a "True Value" vs "False Value" ratio.
* **Comparison:** If the question mentions "weights" or "scales," use the False Weight formulas; otherwise, use the Relative Price framework.

---

## Revision Summary
**Key formula:** $\text{Profit \%} = \frac{\text{S.P.} - \text{C.P.}}{\text{C.P.}} \times 100$.

**Spot it by:** Ratios or fractions relating C.P. and S.P.

**Fastest method:** Assign $100$ to the denominator value and solve for the numerator.

**Biggest trap:** Calculating profit percentage using S.P. as the denominator.
