# Price & Profit Scenarios

## Overview
These questions involve shifting between two or more scenarios where the Cost Price (C.P.) remains constant or changes by a percentage, while the Selling Price (S.P.) and profit/loss percentages vary. The central idea is to always bridge the gap between scenarios using the C.P. as the common link.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "If sold at...", "Had he sold it for...", "Profit/Loss is the same as...", "Reduced/Increased by...".
* **Given:** Two different S.P. values, or one S.P. and a profit/loss percentage, or a change in C.P.
* **Asked:** The C.P. of the article, or the new S.P. required to achieve a specific profit/loss percentage.
* **Scan for:** Phrases like "profit is equal to loss" or "profit is double the loss."

---

## Important Formulas

### [Scenario Linkage]
$$
\frac{S.P._1}{100 \pm \text{Profit/Loss}_1} = \frac{S.P._2}{100 \pm \text{Profit/Loss}_2}
$$
*Meaning:* $S.P._1, S.P._2$ are selling prices; percentages are profit (+) or loss (-).
*Use when:* You need to find a new S.P. without explicitly calculating the C.P.

### [C.P. from Profit/Loss]
$$
C.P. = \frac{S.P. \times 100}{100 \pm \text{Profit/Loss}\%}
$$
*Meaning:* Converts a known S.P. and percentage back to the base C.P.
*Use when:* The question asks for the original C.P. or requires a multi-step calculation.

---

## Solution Framework
1. Identify the known S.P. and its corresponding profit/loss percentage.
2. Calculate the C.P. using the formula $C.P. = \frac{S.P. \times 100}{100 \pm \%}$.
3. If the C.P. changes (e.g., "bought at 20% less"), calculate the new C.P. first.
4. Apply the target profit/loss percentage to the (new) C.P. to find the final S.P.
5. For "profit equals loss" problems, use $S.P._1 - C.P. = C.P. - S.P._2$ to solve for $C.P.$

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Ratio Method:** Use $\frac{S.P._1}{S.P._2} = \frac{100 \pm P_1}{100 \pm P_2}$. This avoids calculating the actual C.P. entirely.
* **Difference Method:** For "If S.P. increased by $X$, profit increases by $Y\%$," simply equate $Y\%$ of $C.P. = X$. This is valid because the difference in S.P. is directly proportional to the difference in profit percentage.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Sign Error:** Treating a loss as a positive percentage in the formula. Always use $(100 - \text{Loss}\%)$.
* **Base Confusion:** Calculating profit/loss on the S.P. instead of the C.P. Always remember profit/loss is reckoned on C.P.
* **New C.P. Neglect:** Forgetting to update the C.P. when the question states "If he had bought it at 20% less." Always calculate the new C.P. before applying the new profit percentage.

---

## Similar Patterns
This pattern is distinct, but can be confused with "False Weight" problems. Remember: Price & Profit scenarios deal with changing money values, while False Weight problems deal with changing quantity/mass.

---

## Revision Summary
**Key formula:** $C.P. = \frac{S.P. \times 100}{100 \pm \%}$.
**Spot it by:** Looking for "If sold at X, then Y; what if sold at Z?"
**Fastest method:** Use the ratio $\frac{S.P._1}{S.P._2} = \frac{100 \pm P_1}{100 \pm P_2}$ for direct S.P. shifts.
**Biggest trap:** Forgetting to calculate the *new* C.P. when the purchase price changes.
