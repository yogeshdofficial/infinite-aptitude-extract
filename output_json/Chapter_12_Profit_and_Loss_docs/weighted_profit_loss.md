# Weighted Profit Loss

## Overview
These questions involve multiple items or parts of a consignment sold at different profit/loss percentages. The central idea is to equate the total profit/loss (or total S.P.) to the sum of the individual parts' profit/loss (or S.P.).

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Two-thirds of a consignment," "sold one at X% gain and another at Y% loss," "ratio of investments," "total gain/loss on the whole."
* **Given:** Individual profit/loss percentages, ratios of quantities/investments, or specific S.P. values.
* **Asked:** Total profit/loss percentage, cost price of a specific item, or the value of the entire consignment.

---

## Important Formulas

### [Total Profit/Loss Equation]
$$
\text{Total Profit/Loss} = \sum (\text{C.P.}_i \times \text{Profit/Loss } \%_i)
$$
*Meaning:* The sum of individual gains/losses equals the net gain/loss.
*Use when:* You are given the total profit/loss amount and need to find the total C.P.

### [Weighted Average S.P.]
$$
\text{Total S.P.} = \sum (\text{C.P.}_i \times (1 \pm \text{Profit/Loss } \%_i))
$$
*Meaning:* Total S.P. is the sum of individual S.P.s calculated from their respective C.P.s.
*Use when:* You need to find the overall profit/loss percentage or the final net amount.

---

## Solution Framework
1. **Assign Variables:** If C.P.s are unknown, use $x$ or the given ratio (e.g., $3x, 5x$).
2. **Calculate Individual Parts:** Express each part's C.P. or S.P. in terms of the variable.
3. **Apply Percentages:** Calculate the S.P. or Profit/Loss for each part using the given percentages.
4. **Summation:** Add the individual values to get the Total C.P. and Total S.P.
5. **Final Calculation:** Use the formula $\text{Profit/Loss } \% = \frac{\text{Net Profit/Loss}}{\text{Total C.P.}} \times 100$.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Ratio Method:** If given a ratio of investments (e.g., $3:5$), assume C.P.s are $300$ and $500$. Calculate profit/loss on these numbers directly to avoid fractions.
* **Why it works:** Percentages are scale-invariant; the result is the same regardless of the multiplier used for the ratio.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Mixing Bases:** Calculating profit/loss percentage on S.P. instead of C.P.
    * *Reason:* Habitual confusion between margin and markup.
    * *Fix:* Always verify the denominator is the Total C.P.
* **Ignoring "Remainder":** Forgetting to calculate the C.P. of the "remaining" part.
    * *Reason:* Misreading the fraction (e.g., using $2/3$ for the whole instead of $1/3$).
    * *Fix:* Ensure the sum of parts equals $1$ (or $100\%$).
* **Sign Errors:** Treating a loss as a positive value in the summation.
    * *Reason:* Forgetting that loss is a negative profit.
    * *Fix:* Always write loss as $-X\%$.

---

## Similar Patterns
This is distinct from "False Weight" problems because it focuses on weighted averages of percentages rather than physical quantity manipulation.

---

## Revision Summary
**Key formula:** $\text{Total Profit/Loss} = \sum (\text{C.P.}_i \times \text{Rate}_i)$.
**Spot it by:** Multiple items/parts sold at different profit/loss rates.
**Fastest method:** Assume values based on the given ratio to eliminate fractions.
**Biggest trap:** Forgetting to subtract the loss percentage from the total.
