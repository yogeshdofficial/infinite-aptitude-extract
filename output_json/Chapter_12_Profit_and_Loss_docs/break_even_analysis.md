# Break-Even Analysis

## Overview

These questions involve a business scenario where you must determine the production volume required to cover fixed costs and reach a specific target profit. The central idea is to equate the total contribution (Selling Price minus Variable Cost per unit) to the sum of Fixed Costs and Target Profit.

---

## Recognition Clues

* **Keywords:** "Overhead expenses," "cost in materials and labour," "sell all he can produce," "make a profit of at least."
* **Given:** Fixed overhead (per week/month), variable cost per unit, selling price per unit, and target profit.
* **Asked:** Number of units ($x$) to be produced/sold.

---

## Important Formulas

### [Break-Even/Target Profit Equation]

$$
x = \frac{\text{Fixed Costs} + \text{Target Profit}}{\text{Selling Price per unit} - \text{Variable Cost per unit}}
$$

*Meaning:* $x$ is the number of units; the denominator is the "Contribution Margin" per unit.
*Use when:* You need to find the exact quantity to hit a specific profit goal.

---

## Solution Framework

1. Identify Fixed Costs (the constant overhead).
2. Calculate the Contribution Margin per unit: $S.P. - \text{Variable Cost}$.
3. Add the Target Profit to the Fixed Costs.
4. Divide the result of Step 3 by the result of Step 2.
5. Round up to the nearest whole number if the result is a fraction (since you cannot sell partial units).

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Mental Math:** Instead of writing the full equation, calculate the "Net Profit per unit" and divide the total overhead by that value.
* **Why it works:** It simplifies the algebra $S.P. \cdot x - V.C. \cdot x = \text{Fixed} + \text{Profit}$ into $x(S.P. - V.C.) = \text{Total Target}$.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Ignoring Fixed Costs:** Forgetting to add the overhead to the target profit.
    * *Reason:* Focusing only on the per-unit margin.
    * *Fix:* Always write down "Total Required = Fixed + Profit" before calculating.
* **Misidentifying Variable Costs:** Treating total overhead as a per-unit cost.
    * *Reason:* Misreading the problem statement.
    * *Fix:* Check units; overhead is usually a lump sum, while material/labour is per unit.
* **Calculation Errors:** Subtracting the wrong values in the denominator.
    * *Reason:* Confusing S.P. and C.P.
    * *Fix:* Ensure the denominator is always $(S.P. - \text{Variable Cost})$.

---

## Similar Patterns

This pattern is distinct from standard Profit/Loss percentage problems because it deals with **absolute values** (fixed overheads) rather than percentage markups or discounts.

---

## Revision Summary

**Key formula:** $x = \frac{\text{Fixed Costs} + \text{Target Profit}}{\text{Contribution Margin}}$.

**Spot it by:** Presence of "overhead expenses" and "cost per unit."

**Fastest method:** Divide (Fixed + Profit) by (S.P. - Variable Cost).

**Biggest trap:** Forgetting to include the fixed overhead in the total target amount.
