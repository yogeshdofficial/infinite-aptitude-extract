# CP SP Ratio

## Overview

These questions involve direct relationships between Cost Price (CP) and Selling Price (SP) expressed as percentages, fractions, or ratios. The central idea is to express both CP and SP in terms of a common variable (usually $x$ or $100$) to calculate profit/loss percentages or missing values.

---

## Recognition Clues

*   **Keywords:** "CP is X% of SP", "Ratio of CP to SP is...", "Profit is X% of SP", "Sold at X% profit".
*   **Given:** A relationship between CP and SP (ratio, percentage, or fraction).
*   **Asked:** Profit/Loss percentage, or the ratio of CP to SP.
*   **Scan for:** Phrases like "profit on selling price" vs "profit on cost price".

---

## Important Formulas

### [Profit/Loss Percentage]
$$
\text{Profit \%} = \left( \frac{\text{SP} - \text{CP}}{\text{CP}} \right) \times 100
$$
*Meaning:* Standard profit calculation relative to Cost Price.
*Use when:* You need the actual profit percentage based on CP.

### [Profit on Selling Price]
$$
\text{Profit \% on SP} = \left( \frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{SP}} \right) \times 100
$$
*Meaning:* Profit calculated as a fraction of the Selling Price.
*Use when:* The question explicitly states "profit is X% of the selling price".

---

## Solution Framework

1.  **Assign a base:** Assume SP = 100 (if percentages are given) or CP = $x$ (if ratios are given).
2.  **Define the other:** Express the unknown price (CP or SP) using the given percentage or ratio relationship.
3.  **Calculate difference:** Find the absolute profit or loss by subtracting CP from SP.
4.  **Apply formula:** Divide the profit/loss by the base specified in the question (CP or SP) and multiply by 100.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

*   **The 100-Base Swap:** If profit is $P\%$ of SP, then CP is $(100 - P)\%$ of SP.
    *   *Why:* Because $SP = CP + Profit$, so $CP = SP - Profit$.
*   **Ratio Conversion:** If $CP:SP = a:b$, then Profit $\% = \frac{b-a}{a} \times 100$.
    *   *Why:* This directly converts the ratio into the standard profit formula.

---

## Common Mistakes

*   **Wrong Base:** Calculating profit percentage on SP when the question implies CP.
    *   *Reason:* Habitual use of CP as the denominator.
    *   *Fix:* Always re-read the question to check if it says "on cost price" or "on selling price".
*   **Inverting the Ratio:** Writing $SP:CP$ when the question asks for $CP:SP$.
    *   *Reason:* Rushing through the question text.
    *   *Fix:* Explicitly write "CP = ..." and "SP = ..." before forming the ratio.
*   **Percentage Confusion:** Treating "Profit is 20% of SP" as "Profit is 20% of CP".
    *   *Reason:* Ignoring the "of SP" qualifier.
    *   *Fix:* Use the "Assume SP = 100" method to avoid algebraic errors.

---

## Similar Patterns

This pattern is distinct from "False Weight" problems (which involve error percentages) and "Successive Discount" problems (which involve multiple SP changes). If the question involves only one item and a relationship between its own CP and SP, it is a CP-SP Ratio problem.

---

## Revision Summary

**Key formula:** $\text{Profit \%} = (\text{Profit} / \text{Base}) \times 100$.

**Spot it by:** Looking for "of SP" or "ratio of CP to SP" in the text.

**Fastest method:** Assume SP = 100 and derive CP immediately.

**Biggest trap:** Calculating profit percentage on CP when the question asks for it on SP.
