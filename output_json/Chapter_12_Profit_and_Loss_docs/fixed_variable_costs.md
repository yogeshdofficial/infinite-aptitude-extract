# Fixed & Variable Costs

## Overview
These questions involve a business scenario where total costs are split into a constant overhead (fixed) and a per-unit production cost (variable). The goal is to find the production volume ($x$) required to achieve a specific target profit.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Overhead expenses," "cost per item," "sell all he can produce," "make a profit of at least."
* **Given:** Fixed weekly/monthly overhead, variable cost per unit, and selling price per unit.
* **Asked:** Number of units ($x$) to be produced/sold to reach a target profit.

---

## Important Formulas

### [Profit Equation]
$$
\text{Profit} = (\text{Selling Price} \times x) - (\text{Variable Cost} \times x + \text{Fixed Overhead})
$$

*Meaning:* $x$ is the number of units; the equation balances total revenue against total costs.
*Use when:* You need to find the break-even point or the volume required for a specific profit target.

---

## Solution Framework
1. Define $x$ as the number of units produced and sold.
2. Express Total Cost as $(\text{Variable Cost} \times x) + \text{Fixed Overhead}$.
3. Express Total Revenue as $\text{Selling Price} \times x$.
4. Set up the equation: $\text{Revenue} - \text{Total Cost} = \text{Target Profit}$.
5. Solve the linear equation for $x$.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Contribution Margin Method:** Calculate the profit per unit (Selling Price - Variable Cost) and divide the (Fixed Overhead + Target Profit) by this value.
* **Why it works:** It isolates the "contribution" each unit makes toward covering the fixed costs and the profit goal, bypassing the full algebraic expansion.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Forgetting the Overhead:** Students often calculate profit as $(\text{SP} - \text{VC}) \times x$ and ignore the fixed overhead.
    * *Reason:* Focusing only on the per-unit margin.
    * *Fix:* Always subtract the fixed overhead from the total margin before equating to profit.
* **Sign Errors:** Subtracting the entire cost expression without distributing the negative sign.
    * *Reason:* Treating the cost as $(VC \times x + \text{Fixed})$ instead of $-(VC \times x + \text{Fixed})$.
    * *Fix:* Always place the entire cost expression in parentheses when subtracting from revenue.

---

## Similar Patterns
This pattern is distinct from standard Profit & Loss because it separates costs into two components. Do not confuse it with simple C.P./S.P. problems where the cost is a single flat rate per item.

---

## Revision Summary
**Key formula:** $\text{Profit} = (\text{SP} - \text{VC})x - \text{Fixed Cost}$.
**Spot it by:** Presence of "overhead expenses" and "cost per item."
**Fastest method:** Divide (Fixed Cost + Target Profit) by (SP - VC).
**Biggest trap:** Forgetting to subtract the fixed overhead from the total revenue.
