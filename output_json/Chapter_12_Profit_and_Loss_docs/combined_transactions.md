# Combined Transactions

## Overview
These questions involve multiple items or parts of a consignment sold under different profit/loss conditions. The central idea is to calculate the **Total Cost Price (C.P.)** and **Total Selling Price (S.P.)** separately to find the net result.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Consignment," "part sold at X% profit/loss," "bought a [Item A] and [Item B]," "total profit/loss."
* **Given:** Individual costs or ratios of costs, and specific profit/loss percentages for each part.
* **Asked:** Total profit/loss amount, total profit/loss percentage, or the cost price of a specific item.

---

## Important Formulas

### [Total Profit/Loss]
$$
\text{Net Profit/Loss} = \sum (\text{S.P.}_i) - \sum (\text{C.P.}_i)
$$
*Meaning:* Sum of all individual selling prices minus sum of all individual cost prices.
*Use when:* You have multiple items with different profit/loss percentages.

### [Weighted Average Profit/Loss]
$$
\text{Total Profit} = (\%P_1 \times \text{C.P.}_1) + (\%P_2 \times \text{C.P.}_2)
$$
*Meaning:* Profit is the sum of the profit/loss values of each individual component.
*Use when:* You need to find the total profit amount given the percentage gain/loss on parts of a total value.

---

## Solution Framework
1. **Define Variables:** Assign $x$ to the unknown C.P. if the total C.P. is given.
2. **Calculate Individual C.P.:** Split the total C.P. based on the given ratios or fractions.
3. **Calculate Individual S.P.:** Apply the respective profit/loss percentage to each part.
4. **Summation:** Add all individual S.P.s and all individual C.P.s.
5. **Final Result:** Subtract Total C.P. from Total S.P. to find the net gain or loss.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Ratio Method:** If the ratio of C.P.s is $a:b$, use the weighted average: $\text{Net } \% = \frac{a(\%P_1) + b(\%P_2)}{a+b}$.
* **Why:** This avoids calculating absolute currency values until the final step, saving time on large numbers.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Ignoring Overhead:** Forgetting to add transportation, repairs, or octroi to the C.P.
    * *Reason:* Treating "purchase price" as the final C.P.
    * *Fix:* Always calculate "Total Investment" before applying profit percentages.
* **Mixing Percentages:** Applying a profit percentage to the S.P. instead of the C.P.
    * *Reason:* Misreading the base of the percentage.
    * *Fix:* Always write $S.P. = C.P. \times (1 \pm \frac{\%}{100})$.
* **Fractional Errors:** Miscalculating parts of a consignment (e.g., $\frac{2}{3}$ vs $\frac{1}{3}$).
    * *Reason:* Rushing the arithmetic.
    * *Fix:* Verify that the sum of parts equals the whole (e.g., $\frac{2}{3} + \frac{1}{3} = 1$).

---

## Similar Patterns
This pattern is distinct from "False Weight" problems because it focuses on **aggregation of multiple transactions** rather than manipulation of units.

---

## Revision Summary
**Key formula:** $\text{Net Profit} = \sum (\text{S.P.}) - \sum (\text{C.P.})$
**Spot it by:** Multiple items or parts sold at different rates.
**Fastest method:** Use the ratio of C.P.s to find the weighted average percentage.
**Biggest trap:** Forgetting to include overhead costs (repairs/transport) in the total C.P.
