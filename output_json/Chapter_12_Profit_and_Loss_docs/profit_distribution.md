# Profit Distribution

## Overview

This pattern involves splitting a total profit between partners based on their relative investment ratios. The central idea is that profit is always distributed in the exact same ratio as the capital invested by each party.

---

## Recognition Clues

* **Keywords:** "Purchased... sold", "paid... times as much as", "receive out of profit".
* **Given:** Total purchase price, total selling price, and the ratio (or relative proportion) of investments.
* **Asked:** The specific share of profit for one of the partners.

---

## Important Formulas

### [Profit Calculation]

$$
\text{Profit} = \text{S.P.} - \text{C.P.}
$$

*Meaning:* S.P. is Selling Price; C.P. is Cost Price.
*Use when:* You need to determine the total amount to be distributed.

### [Share Allocation]

$$
\text{Partner's Share} = \left( \frac{\text{Partner's Ratio}}{\text{Sum of Ratios}} \right) \times \text{Total Profit}
$$

*Meaning:* The ratio is derived from the investment proportions.
*Use when:* You need to calculate the individual payout from the total profit.

---

## Solution Framework

1. Calculate the total profit by subtracting C.P. from S.P.
2. Convert the investment relationship (e.g., "$1\frac{1}{2}$ times") into a simple integer ratio.
3. Sum the parts of the ratio.
4. Multiply the total profit by the fraction representing the specific partner's share.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Ratio Simplification:** If A pays $k$ times B, the ratio is $k:1$. Multiply both sides by the denominator to get integers.
* **Why:** This avoids working with fractions during the final multiplication, reducing calculation errors.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Calculating the share based on the total S.P. instead of the total profit.
    * *Reason:* Confusing revenue with gain.
    * *Fix:* Always subtract C.P. from S.P. first.
* **Mistake:** Misinterpreting "$1\frac{1}{2}$ times as much as" as a ratio of $1:1.5$.
    * *Reason:* Forgetting to convert mixed fractions to improper fractions ($\frac{3}{2}$) before setting the ratio.
    * *Fix:* Write as $\frac{3}{2} : 1$, which simplifies to $3:2$.

---

## Similar Patterns

This pattern is distinct from general Profit & Loss because it focuses on **distribution** rather than percentage gain. Do not confuse it with "False Weight" problems, which involve physical quantity manipulation rather than financial splitting.

---

## Revision Summary

**Key formula:** $\text{Share} = (\text{Ratio Fraction}) \times (\text{S.P.} - \text{C.P.})$.

**Spot it by:** Multiple investors and a "paid X times as much" clause.

**Fastest method:** Convert investment proportions to a simple integer ratio immediately.

**Biggest trap:** Calculating the share from the total S.P. instead of the profit.
