# CP = SP of Other Item

## Overview
This pattern involves selling two items where the Cost Price (CP) of the first equals the Selling Price (SP) of the second. The central idea is to equate the algebraic expressions for the CP and SP of both items to determine the net profit or loss.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Cost price of the first is equal to the selling price of the second."
* **Given:** Total selling price of both items and individual profit/loss percentages.
* **Asked:** Total gain or loss in rupees.
* **Scan for:** A cross-link between the CP of one item and the SP of another.

---

## Important Formulas

### [SP in terms of CP]
$$
\text{SP} = \text{CP} \times \left(1 \pm \frac{\text{Profit/Loss \%}}{100}\right)
$$
*Meaning:* Converts CP to SP based on the percentage change.
*Use when:* Expressing the SP of an item in terms of its unknown CP.

### [CP in terms of SP]
$$
\text{CP} = \text{SP} \times \left(\frac{100}{100 \pm \text{Profit/Loss \%}}\right)
$$
*Meaning:* Reverses the SP to find the original CP.
*Use when:* You have the SP and need to find the CP to equate it to the other item.

---

## Solution Framework
1. Assign a variable (e.g., $x$) to the SP of the first item.
2. Express the SP of the second item as $(\text{Total SP} - x)$.
3. Set the CP of the first item equal to the SP of the second item $(\text{CP}_1 = \text{SP}_2)$.
4. Use the profit/loss percentage of the first item to express its CP in terms of $x$.
5. Equate the expressions and solve for $x$.
6. Calculate the total CP and compare it with the total SP to find the final result.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
No specific shortcut exists for this pattern; the algebraic balancing of the two items is the most reliable method.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Mistake:** Assuming the total profit/loss is simply the average of the two percentages.
    * *Reason:* The base (CP) for each percentage is different.
    * *Fix:* Always calculate the absolute rupee value of CP and SP for each item separately.
* **Mistake:** Confusing CP and SP variables during substitution.
    * *Reason:* The cross-link $(\text{CP}_1 = \text{SP}_2)$ is easy to flip.
    * *Fix:* Write down the variables clearly: $\text{CP}_1 = \text{SP}_2$ and $\text{CP}_2 = \text{SP}_1$ (if applicable).

---

## Similar Patterns
This is distinct from "Two items sold at same SP" (where $SP_1 = SP_2$), which usually involves the $x^2/100$ loss formula. Always check if the problem links CP to SP or SP to SP.

---

## Revision Summary
**Key formula:** $\text{CP} = \text{SP} \times \frac{100}{100 \pm \%}$.
**Spot it by:** The phrase "CP of first = SP of second."
**Fastest method:** Algebraic substitution using a single variable $x$.
**Biggest trap:** Assuming the net percentage gain/loss is the simple sum of the two percentages.
