# Profit/Loss in Units

## Overview

These questions involve selling a specific quantity of items where the profit or loss is expressed in terms of the Selling Price (S.P.) or Cost Price (C.P.) of a certain number of units. The central idea is to equate the total gain or loss to the difference between the S.P. and C.P. of the total quantity sold to find the missing unit price or percentage.

---

## Recognition Clues

*   **Keywords:** "Gain/Loss of the S.P./C.P. of $x$ items," "Selling $n$ items."
*   **Given:** Total quantity sold, total amount (sometimes), and the gain/loss expressed as a quantity of items.
*   **Asked:** Gain/Loss percentage or the unit C.P./S.P. of a single item.
*   **Scan for:** Phrases like "gains the selling price of $x$ metres" or "loss equal to the cost price of $y$ balls."

---

## Important Formulas

### [Basic Gain/Loss Equation]

$$
\text{Gain} = (\text{S.P. of } n \text{ items}) - (\text{C.P. of } n \text{ items})
$$
$$
\text{Loss} = (\text{C.P. of } n \text{ items}) - (\text{S.P. of } n \text{ items})
$$

*Meaning:* $n$ is the total number of items sold.
*Use when:* Setting up the fundamental balance equation to find the relationship between C.P. and S.P.

---

## Solution Framework

1.  Write the equation: $\text{Gain/Loss} = \text{Difference between S.P. and C.P. of total items}$.
2.  Substitute the given gain/loss (e.g., "S.P. of 11m") into the equation.
3.  Rearrange the terms to group all S.P.s on one side and all C.P.s on the other.
4.  If finding percentage: Assume C.P. of 1 unit = ₹1, then calculate $\frac{\text{Gain/Loss}}{\text{C.P.}} \times 100$.
5.  If finding unit price: Equate the resulting S.P. or C.P. total to the given monetary value and divide.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

*   **The "Unit Substitution" Trick:** If gain is S.P. of $x$ items for $n$ items sold, then $\text{C.P. of } n = \text{S.P. of } (n-x)$.
*   **Why it works:** It bypasses algebraic steps by directly converting the total quantity into the equivalent units of the other price type.

---

## Common Mistakes

*   **Mistake:** Confusing "Gain of S.P." with "Gain of C.P."
    *   *Reason:* Misreading the problem statement.
    *   *Fix:* Always write the full equation $(\text{S.P. of } n) - (\text{C.P. of } n) = \text{Given Value}$ before solving.
*   **Mistake:** Calculating percentage on S.P. instead of C.P.
    *   *Reason:* Habitually using the denominator from the equation.
    *   *Fix:* Always ensure the denominator is the C.P. of the items sold.
*   **Mistake:** Forgetting to adjust the quantity when moving terms.
    *   *Reason:* Algebraic error during transposition.
    *   *Fix:* Keep the number of items attached to the S.P./C.P. labels at every step.

---

## Similar Patterns

This pattern is distinct from standard Profit/Loss because the "quantity" is the variable. It is most often confused with "False Weight" problems; distinguish them by checking if the problem mentions "weights" (False Weight) or "units sold" (Profit/Loss in Units).

---

## Revision Summary

**Key formula:** $\text{S.P. of } n - \text{C.P. of } n = \text{Gain/Loss}$.
**Spot it by:** "Gain/Loss of S.P./C.P. of $x$ items."
**Fastest method:** Equate S.P. and C.P. sides to find the ratio of unit prices.
**Biggest trap:** Using S.P. as the base for percentage calculations instead of C.P.
