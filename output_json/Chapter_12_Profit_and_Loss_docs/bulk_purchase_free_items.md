# Bulk Purchase Free Items

## Overview

This pattern involves transactions where the quantity purchased differs from the quantity sold due to a "bonus" or "free" count. The central idea is to equate the total Cost Price (C.P.) of the quantity bought to the total Selling Price (S.P.) of the quantity sold to find the net profit.

---

## Recognition Clues

*   **Keywords:** "allows X copies to be counted as Y", "buy X get Y free", "retailer sells each of the Z copies".
*   **Given:** C.P. per unit, S.P. per unit, and the discrepancy between quantity bought vs. quantity sold.
*   **Asked:** Profit percentage.
*   **Visual Scan:** Look for two different quantities (e.g., 24 vs 25) and two different prices (e.g., ₹5 vs ₹6).

---

## Important Formulas

### [Profit Percentage]

$$
\text{Profit \%} = \left( \frac{\text{Total S.P.} - \text{Total C.P.}}{\text{Total C.P.}} \right) \times 100
$$

*Meaning:* The ratio of net gain to the total investment, expressed as a percentage.
*Use when:* You have calculated the total money spent and total money received for the specific quantities involved.

---

## Solution Framework

1.  Calculate the total C.P. for the quantity the dealer actually paid for.
2.  Calculate the total S.P. for the quantity the dealer actually sold.
3.  Find the absolute profit by subtracting Total C.P. from Total S.P.
4.  Divide the profit by the Total C.P. and multiply by 100 to get the percentage.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

*   **Ratio Method:** Calculate the ratio of (Total S.P. / Total C.P.) and subtract 1.
*   **Why:** This bypasses calculating absolute currency values, working directly with the multiplier of the investment.

---

## Common Mistakes

*   **Mistake:** Calculating profit based on the quantity bought instead of the quantity sold.
    *   *Reason:* Confusing the "cost quantity" with the "revenue quantity."
    *   *Fix:* Always ensure the S.P. is calculated on the total units actually handed over to the customer.
*   **Mistake:** Using the wrong C.P. per unit for the S.P. calculation.
    *   *Reason:* Forgetting that the dealer's profit is the difference between what they paid for the batch and what they collected from the batch.
    *   *Fix:* Clearly label "Total C.P. for X units" and "Total S.P. for Y units" before subtracting.

---

## Similar Patterns

This is distinct from "False Weight" problems because here the quantity difference is a business incentive/deal, whereas "False Weight" involves physical deception in measurement.

---

## Revision Summary

**Key formula:** $\text{Profit \%} = \frac{\text{Total S.P.} - \text{Total C.P.}}{\text{Total C.P.}} \times 100$.

**Spot it by:** Different quantities mentioned for buying vs. selling.

**Fastest method:** Calculate total investment vs. total revenue for the specific batch.

**Biggest trap:** Using the wrong quantity count when calculating the total S.P.
