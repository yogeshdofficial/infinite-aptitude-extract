# Pattern: Solving for Variables in Proportional Equations

## 1. Pattern Overview
This pattern provides a systematic method for finding an unknown value in a proportional relationship by equating the product of the means to the product of the extremes. It is essential for solving problems involving scaling, missing quantities, and comparative ratios.

## 2. Key Formulas
The fundamental relationship for any proportion $a : b :: c : d$ is:

**Fundamental Property of Proportions:**
$$b \times c = a \times d$$

**Fourth Proportional ($d$):**
$$d = \frac{b \times c}{a}$$

**Third Proportional ($c$):**
$$c = \frac{b^2}{a}$$

**Mean Proportional ($x$):**
$$x = \sqrt{a \times b}$$

**Componendo and Dividendo:**
$$\frac{a+b}{a-b} = \frac{c+d}{c-d}$$

## 3. When to Use This Pattern
*   **Keywords:** "Find the fourth proportional," "Find the mean proportional," "Ratio of X to Y is the same as A to B," "In proportion."
*   **Recognition:** When you are given three values and asked to find a fourth, or when two ratios are explicitly stated to be equal.
*   **Given:** Three known quantities ($a, b, c$) or a relationship between two ratios.
*   **To Find:** The missing variable ($x$) that completes the equality.

## 4. Core Concept & Theory
A proportion is simply an equality of two ratios. Because a ratio is a fraction, $a:b = c:d$ is equivalent to $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}$. By cross-multiplying, we derive the rule that the **Product of Means** ($b \times c$) must equal the **Product of Extremes** ($a \times d$). This algebraic transformation allows us to isolate any single variable, making it the most efficient way to solve for unknowns in scaling problems.

## 5. Step-by-Step Solution Method
1.  **Identify the terms:** Assign the given values to $a, b, c,$ and $d$ based on the problem statement.
2.  **Set up the equation:** Write the proportion as $a : b :: c : x$ (where $x$ is the unknown).
3.  **Apply the Product Rule:** Write the equation $a \times x = b \times c$.
4.  **Isolate the variable:** Divide the product of the known terms by the coefficient of the unknown variable.
5.  **Simplify:** Reduce the resulting fraction to its simplest form.

## 6. Worked Examples

**Example 1: Finding the Fourth Proportional**
*Given:* Find the fourth proportional to 4, 9, and 12.
*Solution:*
1. Set up: $4 : 9 :: 12 : x$
2. Apply rule: $4 \times x = 9 \times 12$
3. Solve: $4x = 108$
4. $x = \frac{108}{4} = 27$
*Reasoning:* We treat the sequence as $a, b, c$ and solve for $d$ using the product of means and extremes.

**Example 2: Finding the Mean Proportional**
*Given:* Find the mean proportional between 9 and 16.
*Solution:*
1. Formula: $x = \sqrt{a \times b}$
2. Substitute: $x = \sqrt{9 \times 16}$
3. Calculate: $x = \sqrt{144} = 12$
*Reasoning:* The mean proportional $x$ satisfies $9:x :: x:16$, leading to $x^2 = 144$.

## 7. Recognition Clues & Keywords
*   **"Find the fourth proportional":** Direct instruction to use $d = \frac{bc}{a}$.
*   **"Ratio of A to B is the same as C to D":** Signals an equality of ratios.
*   **"Mean proportional":** Signals the square root method.
*   **Avoid confusion:** Do not confuse "Third Proportional" (where $b$ is repeated) with "Fourth Proportional" (where all three numbers are distinct).

## 8. Common Mistakes
1.  **Incorrect Ordering:** Placing the numbers in the wrong positions (e.g., swapping $a$ and $b$). Always map the numbers to the order they appear in the problem.
2.  **Forgetting the Square Root:** In mean proportional problems, students often calculate $a \times b$ but forget to take the square root.
3.  **Cross-Multiplication Errors:** Failing to multiply the correct pairs (means vs. extremes). Remember: the "inner" numbers are the means, the "outer" numbers are the extremes.
4.  **Units Mismatch:** Attempting to solve a proportion when the units are different (e.g., comparing meters to centimeters). Always convert to the same units first.

## 9. Practice Tips
*   **Master the Cross-Multiply:** Practice writing out $a/b = c/d$ and cross-multiplying until it becomes muscle memory.
*   **Check your work:** Plug your answer back into the ratio. If $a/b$ equals $c/d$, your answer is correct.
*   **Related Patterns:** Study "Variation" (Direct and Inverse) as these are advanced applications of proportional equations.
