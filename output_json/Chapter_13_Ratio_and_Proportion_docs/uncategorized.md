# Ratio and Proportion Fundamentals

## Overview
These questions involve manipulating ratios to find missing parts, percentages, or total values. The central idea is to represent ratio terms as multiples of a common variable ($x$ or $k$) to bridge the gap between parts and actual values.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "ratio of X to Y", "antecedent", "consequent", "in proportion", "value of a+b+c".
* **Given:** A ratio (e.g., $16:9$) and either one specific value or a sum of terms.
* **Asked:** A percentage, a missing term in a proportion, or an algebraic expression involving the ratio variables.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Proportion Property]
$$
\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d} \implies ad = bc
$$
**Variables:** $a, c$ (antecedents), $b, d$ (consequents).
**When to use:** When you have three known values in a proportion and need to find the fourth.
**Worked example:** Given $7:8 = 35:x$. Here $a=7, b=8, c=35$. $7x = 8 \times 35 \implies x = 40$.

### [Sum of Ratio Parts]
$$
\text{Total} = (\text{Sum of ratio terms}) \times x
$$
**Variables:** $\text{Total}$ (given sum), $x$ (common multiplier).
**When to use:** When you are given the total sum of quantities and their ratio.
**Worked example:** Ratio $16:9$, total $25$ students. $16x + 9x = 25x$. If total is $25$, then $x=1$.

---

## Solution Framework
1. **Assign Variables:** Represent the ratio terms as $ax, bx, cx...$ using a common multiplier $x$.
2. **Form Equation:** Use the given information (sum, specific value, or proportion) to create an algebraic equation.
3. **Solve for $x$:** Isolate the common multiplier $x$.
4. **Substitute:** Plug $x$ back into the required expression to find the final answer.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Trick:** For percentage of a part, use $\frac{\text{Part}}{\text{Sum of all parts}} \times 100$.
* **Why it works:** It directly converts the ratio share into a fraction of the whole.
* **When to use:** When asked "What percentage of the class/total is X?".
* **Example:** Ratio $16:9$. Female percentage = $\frac{16}{16+9} \times 100 = 64\%$.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Mistake:** Dividing by only one ratio term instead of the sum.
    * **Fix:** Always sum all parts of the ratio to represent the "whole".
* **Mistake:** Forgetting the factor of $2$ in $a+b:b+c:c+a$ problems.
    * **Fix:** Remember that adding these three ratios results in $2(a+b+c)$.
* **Mistake:** Confusing antecedent (first term) and consequent (second term).
    * **Fix:** Remember $a:b \rightarrow \frac{a}{b}$ (Antecedent/Consequent).

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** If $a + b : b + c : c + a = 6 : 7 : 8$ and $a + b + c = 14$, then the value of $c$ is?

**Solution:**
1. **Assign Variables:** Let $a+b=6k, b+c=7k, c+a=8k$.
2. **Form Equation:** Add them: $(a+b) + (b+c) + (c+a) = 6k+7k+8k \implies 2(a+b+c) = 21k$.
3. **Solve for $k$:** Substitute $a+b+c=14$: $2(14) = 21k \implies 28 = 21k \implies k = \frac{4}{3}$.
4. **Substitute:** Find $a+b = 6k = 6(\frac{4}{3}) = 8$.
5. **Final Step:** Since $a+b+c=14$ and $a+b=8$, then $c = 14 - 8 = 6$.

**Answer:** $6$

## Similar Patterns
* **Partnership Problems:** These involve time factors ($Investment \times Time$). Distinguish by checking if "time" or "duration" is mentioned; if not, it is a simple ratio problem.

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d} \implies ad=bc$ and $\text{Sum} = \text{Ratio Sum} \times x$.
* **Spot it by:** Ratios given with a total or a specific value.
* **First move:** Assign $x$ to ratio terms (e.g., $10x, 6x, 15x$).
* **Fastest method:** Use the sum of parts to find $x$ immediately.
* **Biggest trap:** Forgetting to sum all parts or missing the factor of $2$ in cyclic sums.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Ratio** — A comparison of two quantities by division, written as $a:b$.
* **Proportion** — An equation stating that two ratios are equal, $a:b = c:d$.

### Formulas You Must Know First
$$
\text{Percentage} = \frac{\text{Part}}{\text{Whole}} \times 100
$$
**What it means:** Expresses a portion relative to the total as a value out of $100$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Antecedent** — The first term in a ratio.
* **Consequent** — The second term in a ratio.
