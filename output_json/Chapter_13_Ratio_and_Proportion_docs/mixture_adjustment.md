# Mixture Adjustment

## Overview
This pattern involves changing the composition of a mixture by adding or removing specific components. The central idea is to express the initial quantities using a common variable ($x$), apply the changes, and equate the new quantities to the given final ratio.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "added to," "mixture," "ratio becomes," "some quantity of... added."
* **Given:** Initial total volume or weight, initial ratio, and the amount of one or more components added.
* **Asked:** The amount of a component added or the final quantity of a component.
* **Visual:** Look for a "Before" ratio and an "After" ratio separated by an addition/subtraction event.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Component Quantity]
$$
\text{Quantity} = \text{Total} \times \frac{\text{Ratio Part}}{\text{Sum of Ratio Parts}}
$$

**Variables:**
- $\text{Total}$ = Total volume/weight of the mixture.
- $\text{Ratio Part}$ = The specific part of the ratio for the component.
- $\text{Sum of Ratio Parts}$ = The sum of all parts in the ratio.

**When to use:** To find the starting amount of each ingredient before any changes are made.

**Worked example:** For 64 litres in ratio 5:3, Milk = $64 \times \frac{5}{5+3} = 40$ litres.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Step 1: Define initial quantities** — Assign $x$ to the ratio parts (e.g., $5x$ and $3x$) to represent the actual amounts.
2. **Step 2: Apply changes** — Add or subtract the given values to the specific components (e.g., $3x + 5$).
3. **Step 3: Set up the ratio equation** — Equate the new expressions to the final given ratio (e.g., $\frac{4x+5}{5x+10} = \frac{5}{7}$).
4. **Step 4: Solve for $x$** — Use cross-multiplication to find the value of the variable.
5. **Step 5: Calculate final values** — Substitute $x$ back into the expressions to find the required quantity.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
**No shortcut faster than the standard framework** — The algebraic setup is the most reliable method for these problems. Always prioritize setting up the ratio equation correctly.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Mistake:** Adding the new quantity to the total mixture instead of the specific component.
    * **Fix:** Only add the change to the specific component mentioned in the question.
* **Mistake:** Forgetting to include the initial quantity when calculating the final amount.
    * **Fix:** Always calculate the final amount as $(\text{Initial} + \text{Added})$.
* **Mistake:** Miscalculating the ratio sum.
    * **Fix:** Double-check the sum of ratio parts before calculating initial quantities.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** The ratio of milk and water in 64 litres of a mixture is 5 : 3. What amount of water is added to make the ratio 3 : 5?

**Solution:**
1. **Initial Quantities:** Total parts = $5+3=8$. Milk = $64 \times \frac{5}{8} = 40$ L. Water = $64 \times \frac{3}{8} = 24$ L.
2. **Apply Changes:** Let $x$ be the water added. New water = $24+x$. Milk remains 40 L.
3. **Ratio Equation:** $\frac{40}{24+x} = \frac{3}{5}$.
4. **Solve for $x$:** $40 \times 5 = 3(24+x) \Rightarrow 200 = 72 + 3x \Rightarrow 128 = 3x$.
5. **Final Value:** $x = \frac{128}{3} = 42\frac{2}{3}$ litres.

**Answer:** $42\frac{2}{3}$ litres.

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\text{Quantity} = \text{Total} \times \frac{\text{Part}}{\text{Sum}}$.
* **Spot it by:** Ratios changing after adding/removing components.
* **First move:** Convert ratio parts to $x$ (e.g., $5x, 3x$).
* **Fastest method:** Algebraic cross-multiplication.
* **Biggest trap:** Adding the change to the wrong component.

---

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Ratio:** A comparison of two quantities by division, written as $a:b$.
* **Proportion:** An equation stating that two ratios are equal, $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}$.
* **Cross-Multiplication:** Solving $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}$ as $ad = bc$.

### Formulas You Must Know First
* **Weighted Average:** $\frac{w_1x_1 + w_2x_2}{w_1 + w_2}$. Used for mixture price problems.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Antecedent:** The first term in a ratio.
* **Consequent:** The second term in a ratio.
* **C.P. (Cost Price):** The price at which an item is bought.
* **S.P. (Selling Price):** The price at which an item is sold.
