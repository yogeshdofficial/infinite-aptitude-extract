# Variation

## Overview
This pattern involves variables that change in relation to one another, described as "directly proportional" or "inversely proportional." The central idea is to convert these verbal relationships into an equation using a constant $k$ and solve for $k$ using the provided data.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "varies directly as," "inversely proportional to," "square of," "ratio of."
* **Given:** Ratios of variables (e.g., weights, radii) or specific values for two related quantities.
* **Asked:** A missing value (e.g., length, price, consumption) based on the established relationship.
* **Scan for:** Phrases like "varies as the square of" or "inversely proportional to the square of."

---

## Key Formulas

### Direct Variation
$$
X = kY
$$
**Variables:** $X, Y$ = related quantities; $k$ = constant of proportionality.
**When to use:** When one quantity increases as the other increases (e.g., Price $\propto$ Weight$^2$).

### Inverse Variation
$$
XY = k \quad \text{or} \quad X = \frac{k}{Y}
$$
**Variables:** $X, Y$ = related quantities; $k$ = constant.
**When to use:** When one quantity increases as the other decreases (e.g., Price $\times$ Consumption = Constant).

### Combined Variation
$$
R = \frac{kl}{r^2}
$$
**Variables:** $R$ = Resistance, $l$ = length, $r$ = radius, $k$ = constant.
**When to use:** When a variable depends on multiple factors simultaneously.

---

## Solution Framework
1. **Define the relationship:** Write the proportionality as an equation using $k$ (e.g., $P = kW^2$).
2. **Assign variables:** Use a multiplier (like $x$) for ratios given in the problem (e.g., weights $1x, 2x, 3x$).
3. **Express in terms of $k$:** Substitute the variables into the equation to find the value of each component.
4. **Set up the equation:** Use the "given" difference or equality to solve for the value of $k$ (or $kx^2$).
5. **Calculate the target:** Substitute the known value back into the expression for the required quantity.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
* **Trick:** For inverse variation ($X_1Y_1 = X_2Y_2$), ignore $k$ and equate the products directly.
* **Why it works:** Since $k$ is constant, $X_1Y_1 = k$ and $X_2Y_2 = k$, therefore $X_1Y_1 = X_2Y_2$.
* **When to use:** When comparing two states of the same system (e.g., Q195).
* **Example:** $1.5 \times 60 = 6 \times x \Rightarrow 90 = 6x \Rightarrow x = 15$.

---

## Common Mistakes
* **Mistake:** Forgetting to square the ratio when the problem says "square of."
* **Why it happens:** Rushing through the reading of the problem.
* **Fix:** Circle the word "square" or "cube" in the question immediately.
* **Mistake:** Assuming direct proportion for inverse relationships (like Price/Consumption).
* **Why it happens:** Confusing "proportional" with "directly proportional."
* **Fix:** Check if the product of the two variables remains constant.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** The resistance of a wire is directly proportional to its length and inversely proportional to the square of its radius. Two wires have the same resistance and radii in ratio 9 : 8. If the length of the first is 162 cm, find the length of the second.

**Solution:**
1. **Define:** $R = \frac{kl}{r^2}$. Since $R$ is constant for both, $\frac{l_1}{r_1^2} = \frac{l_2}{r_2^2}$.
2. **Assign:** $r_1 = 9x, r_2 = 8x$.
3. **Substitute:** $\frac{162}{(9x)^2} = \frac{l_2}{(8x)^2}$.
4. **Solve:** $\frac{162}{81x^2} = \frac{l_2}{64x^2} \Rightarrow 2 = \frac{l_2}{64}$.
5. **Result:** $l_2 = 128$ cm.

**Answer:** 128 cm.

## Similar Patterns
* **Partnership Problems:** These involve ratios but are based on $Investment \times Time = Profit$, not proportionality constants. Look for "time" and "investment" keywords to distinguish.

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $X = kY$ (Direct) or $XY = k$ (Inverse).
* **Spot it by:** Keywords "varies as," "proportional to."
* **First move:** Write the equation with $k$ immediately.
* **Fastest method:** Use $X_1Y_1 = X_2Y_2$ for inverse problems.
* **Biggest trap:** Forgetting to square the terms when the problem specifies "square of."

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Ratio:** A comparison of two quantities $a:b$ expressed as $\frac{a}{b}$.
* **Proportionality Constant ($k$):** A fixed value that relates two variables.

### Formulas You Must Know First
* **Algebraic Expansion:** $(ax)^2 = a^2x^2$.
* **Cross-Multiplication:** If $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}$, then $ad = bc$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Directly Proportional:** As one variable increases, the other increases at the same rate.
* **Inversely Proportional:** As one variable increases, the other decreases.
