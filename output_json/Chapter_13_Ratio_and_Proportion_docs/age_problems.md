# Age Problems

## Overview
These questions involve finding the ages of individuals based on ratios provided at different points in time (past, present, or future). The central idea is to represent ages using a common variable ($x$) and equate the ratio of these expressions to the given numerical ratio.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "ratio of ages," "years ago," "years hence," "after X years," "proportion."
* **Given:** Ratios of ages at two different time points (e.g., "5 years ago" and "present").
* **Asked:** Present age of one or more individuals.
* **Variation:** Average age given instead of a direct ratio (e.g., "average age of 3 boys is 25").

---

## Key Formulas

### [Age Ratio Equation]
$$
\frac{\text{Age}_1 \pm t}{\text{Age}_2 \pm t} = \frac{a}{b}
$$

**Variables:**
- $\text{Age}_1, \text{Age}_2$ = Initial ages (e.g., $5x, 2x$)
- $t$ = Time difference in years
- $a:b$ = Ratio after $t$ years

**When to use:** When given a ratio at one time and a new ratio after a specific time gap.

**Worked example:** Chinmay and Maulik are $5x$ and $2x$. After 7 years, ratio is $4:3$.
$\frac{5x+7}{2x+7} = \frac{4}{3} \Rightarrow 15x + 21 = 8x + 28 \Rightarrow 7x = 7 \Rightarrow x = 1$. Chinmay = $5(1) = 5$.

---

## Solution Framework

**Step 1: Define variables** — Assign $x$ to the ratio parts (e.g., $5x, 2x$) for the earliest time point mentioned.
**Step 2: Adjust for time** — Add or subtract the time gap from these variables to reach the second time point.
**Step 3: Set up equation** — Create a fraction using the adjusted ages and equate it to the second given ratio.
**Step 4: Solve for $x$** — Use cross-multiplication to find the value of the common multiplier.
**Step 5: Calculate target age** — Substitute $x$ back into the expression for the specific person requested.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** If the time gap is the same for both people, the difference in ratio parts must be proportional to the time gap.
* **Why it works:** The difference between ages remains constant over time.
* **When to use:** When the ratio parts increase by the same amount (e.g., $5:2$ to $7:4$ is a $+2$ increase for both).
* **Example:** If ages are $5:2$ and after 7 years they become $7:4$, the increase is $2$ parts. $2 \text{ parts} = 7 \text{ years}$. $1 \text{ part} = 3.5 \text{ years}$.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Adding the time gap to only one person's age.
    * **Why it happens:** Forgetting that time passes equally for everyone.
    * **Fix:** Always add/subtract $t$ to both terms in the ratio.
* **Mistake:** Calculating the wrong person's age.
    * **Why it happens:** Rushing to the final step without checking which variable represents whom.
    * **Fix:** Label variables clearly (e.g., $C=5x, M=2x$) at the start.
* **Mistake:** Using the average as a ratio part.
    * **Why it happens:** Confusing "sum of ages" with "ratio of ages."
    * **Fix:** Multiply average by the number of people to get the total sum first.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** The ages of Chinmay and Maulik are in the ratio of $5:2$. After 7 years, the ratio will be $4:3$. What is the age of Chinmay?

**Solution:**
1. **Define:** Let Chinmay = $5x$, Maulik = $2x$.
2. **Adjust:** After 7 years, Chinmay = $5x+7$, Maulik = $2x+7$.
3. **Equation:** $\frac{5x+7}{2x+7} = \frac{4}{3}$.
4. **Solve:** $3(5x+7) = 4(2x+7) \Rightarrow 15x + 21 = 8x + 28 \Rightarrow 7x = 7 \Rightarrow x = 1$.
5. **Target:** Chinmay = $5x = 5(1) = 5$.

**Answer:** 5 years.

## Similar Patterns
**Partnership Problems:** Distinguished by "investment" and "time period" rather than "age" and "time gap."

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\frac{\text{Age}_1 \pm t}{\text{Age}_2 \pm t} = \frac{a}{b}$
* **Spot it by:** Ratios given at two different time points.
* **First move:** Assign $x$ to the ratio parts at the earliest time.
* **Fastest method:** Cross-multiplication or checking the difference in ratio parts.
* **Biggest trap:** Forgetting to add the time gap to both ages.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Ratio:** A comparison of two quantities $a:b$ expressed as $\frac{a}{b}$.
* **Algebraic Equations:** Solving $ax + b = cx + d$ by isolating $x$.

### Formulas You Must Know First
* **Sum of Ages:** $\text{Sum} = \text{Average} \times \text{Number of people}$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Hence:** Future time (e.g., "7 years hence" means $+7$).
* **Ago:** Past time (e.g., "1 year ago" means $-1$).
