# Pattern: Distributing Quantities by Ratio

## 1. Pattern Overview
This pattern provides a systematic method for partitioning a total quantity into smaller parts based on a specified ratio between those parts. It is essential for solving problems involving the fair division of resources, money, or mixtures among multiple parties.

## 2. Key Formulas
To distribute a total quantity $T$ into parts $a$ and $b$ in the ratio $a:b$:

*   **Sum of Ratio Terms:**
    $$S = a + b$$
*   **Value of First Part:**
    $$\text{Part}_1 = \left( \frac{a}{a+b} \right) \times T$$
*   **Value of Second Part:**
    $$\text{Part}_2 = \left( \frac{b}{a+b} \right) \times T$$
*   **General Formula for $n$ parts ($a:b:c...$):**
    $$\text{Part}_i = \left( \frac{\text{Term}_i}{\sum \text{All Terms}} \right) \times \text{Total Quantity}$$

## 3. When to Use This Pattern
*   **Keywords:** "Divide," "Distribute," "Share," "Split," "In the ratio of."
*   **Recognition Clues:** You are given a total amount and a relationship (ratio) between the recipients.
*   **Given:** The total quantity and the ratio of distribution.
*   **To Find:** The specific numerical value assigned to each individual or category.

## 4. Core Concept & Theory
The fundamental idea is that a ratio represents "parts of a whole." If a quantity is divided in the ratio $a:b$, it means for every $a$ units one person gets, the other gets $b$ units. By adding the terms ($a+b$), we find the total number of "equal shares" the quantity is divided into. We then determine the value of one single share and multiply it by the respective ratio terms.

## 5. Step-by-Step Solution Method
1.  **Identify the Ratio:** Write down the ratio terms (e.g., $a:b$).
2.  **Calculate the Sum of Terms:** Add the ratio terms together ($S = a + b$). This represents the "total parts."
3.  **Find the Value of One Part:** Divide the total quantity by the sum of the ratio terms: $\text{Value of 1 part} = \frac{T}{S}$.
4.  **Calculate Individual Shares:** Multiply the "Value of 1 part" by each individual ratio term.
5.  **Verification:** Add the calculated parts together; they must equal the original total quantity $T$.

## 6. Worked Examples

**Example 1: Dividing Money**
*Given:* Divide $1,200 between A and B in the ratio 3:5.
*Solution:*
1. Sum of ratio terms: $3 + 5 = 8$.
2. Value of one part: $1,200 / 8 = 150$.
3. A's share: $3 \times 150 = 450$.
4. B's share: $5 \times 150 = 750$.
*Check:* $450 + 750 = 1,200$. Correct.

**Example 2: Mixture Distribution**
*Given:* A 40-liter mixture contains milk and water in the ratio 7:3. Find the quantity of each.
*Solution:*
1. Sum of ratio terms: $7 + 3 = 10$.
2. Value of one part: $40 / 10 = 4$ liters.
3. Milk: $7 \times 4 = 28$ liters.
4. Water: $3 \times 4 = 12$ liters.
*Check:* $28 + 12 = 40$. Correct.

## 7. Recognition Clues & Keywords
*   **Look for:** "Divide $X$ in the ratio $a:b$."
*   **Distinguish from:** Do not confuse this with **Proportion** ($a:b::c:d$), where you are finding an unknown fourth value rather than splitting a known total.
*   **Context:** If the problem mentions "total," "sum," or "all together," it is almost certainly a distribution problem.

## 8. Common Mistakes
1.  **Dividing by the wrong number:** Students often divide the total by only one of the ratio terms instead of the sum. Always sum the terms first.
2.  **Forgetting to multiply:** After finding the value of "one part," students sometimes forget to multiply by the specific ratio term for the other person.
3.  **Unit Mismatch:** Ensure the total quantity and the ratio refer to the same units (e.g., don't mix liters and milliliters without converting first).
4.  **Order Confusion:** Ensure the first term of the ratio corresponds to the first person/item mentioned in the problem.

## 9. Practice Tips
*   **Mastery:** Practice by creating your own "Total" and "Ratio" and solving for the parts.
