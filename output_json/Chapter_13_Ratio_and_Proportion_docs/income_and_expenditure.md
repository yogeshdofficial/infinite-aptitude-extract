# Income and Expenditure

## Overview
These questions involve calculating unknown incomes, expenditures, or savings based on given ratios and constraints. The central idea is the fundamental equation: $\text{Income} - \text{Expenditure} = \text{Savings}$.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Ratio of incomes," "ratio of expenditures," "saves," "salary," "percentage of income spent."
* **Given:** Ratios of two or more people's incomes/expenditures and a specific value (e.g., "each saves ₹300" or "total salary is ₹33300").
* **Asked:** Individual income, individual expenditure, or the ratio of savings.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Basic Income Equation]
$$
\text{Income} - \text{Expenditure} = \text{Savings}
$$
**Variables:**
- $\text{Income}$: Total money earned.
- $\text{Expenditure}$: Total money spent.
- $\text{Savings}$: Money remaining.

**When to use:** Every question in this pattern.

**Worked example:** If Income ratio is $7:3$ and Expenditure ratio is $5:2$, and each saves ₹300:
$7x - 5y = 300$ and $3x - 2y = 300$.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Define Variables:** Assign separate variables for income ratios (e.g., $7x, 3x$) and expenditure ratios (e.g., $5y, 2y$) to avoid mixing ratios.
2. **Set up Equations:** Write the equation $\text{Income} - \text{Expenditure} = \text{Savings}$ for each person.
3. **Solve for Variables:** Use substitution or elimination to find the value of the ratio multiplier ($x$ or $y$).
4. **Calculate Target:** Plug the multiplier back into the expression for the requested value (e.g., $7x$ for income).
5. **Convert Units (if needed):** If the question asks for monthly income but gives annual savings, divide the final result by 12.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** If savings are equal, the difference between income and expenditure ratios must be proportional to the savings.
* **Why it works:** If $I_1 - E_1 = S$ and $I_2 - E_2 = S$, then $I_1 - E_1 = I_2 - E_2$.
* **When to use:** When the question states "each saves ₹X."
* **Example:** In Q28, Income $7:3$, Exp $5:2$. Difference in income is $7-3=4$, difference in exp is $5-2=3$. Set $4x = 3y$ to find the relationship between $x$ and $y$ instantly.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Using the same variable for income and expenditure ratios (e.g., $7x$ and $5x$).
    * **Why it happens:** Assuming the ratio multiplier is the same for both.
    * **Fix:** Always use $x$ for income and $y$ for expenditure.
* **Mistake:** Using spending percentage instead of savings percentage.
    * **Why it happens:** Misreading "spends 80%" as "saves 80%."
    * **Fix:** Always calculate $\text{Savings \%} = 100\% - \text{Spending \%}$.
* **Mistake:** Forgetting to convert annual figures to monthly.
    * **Why it happens:** Rushing the final step.
    * **Fix:** Check the units of the final answer against the question requirements.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** Weekly incomes of two persons are in the ratio of $7:3$ and their weekly expenses are in the ratio of $5:2$. If each of them saves ₹300 per week, find their weekly incomes.

**Solution:**
1. **Define:** Income of A and B = $7x, 3x$. Expenditure of A and B = $5y, 2y$.
2. **Equations:** $7x - 5y = 300$ and $3x - 2y = 300$.
3. **Solve:** Since both equal 300, $7x - 5y = 3x - 2y \Rightarrow 4x = 3y \Rightarrow y = \frac{4}{3}x$.
4. **Substitute:** $7x - 5(\frac{4}{3}x) = 300 \Rightarrow 7x - \frac{20}{3}x = 300 \Rightarrow \frac{x}{3} = 300 \Rightarrow x = 900$.
5. **Calculate:** Income A = $7 \times 900 = 6300$; Income B = $3 \times 900 = 2700$.

**Answer:** ₹6300 and ₹2700.

## Similar Patterns
**Partnership Problems:** These involve investment and time, whereas Income/Expenditure involves income, expenditure, and savings. Distinguish by checking if the question mentions "spending" or "savings."

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\text{Income} - \text{Expenditure} = \text{Savings}$.
* **Spot it by:** Ratios of income/expenditure and a fixed savings amount.
* **First move:** Assign $x$ to income ratios and $y$ to expenditure ratios.
* **Fastest method:** Equate the savings expressions if the savings amount is identical.
* **Biggest trap:** Using the same variable for both income and expenditure ratios.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Ratio:** A comparison of two quantities by division, written as $a:b$.
* **Linear Equations:** Solving $ax + by = c$ for variables.
* **Percentages:** Converting percentages to fractions (e.g., $80\% = \frac{4}{5}$).

### Formulas You Must Know First
* **Combined Ratio:** If $A:B = a:b$ and $B:C = c:d$, then $A:B:C = ac:bc:bd$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Antecedent:** The first term in a ratio.
* **Consequent:** The second term in a ratio.
