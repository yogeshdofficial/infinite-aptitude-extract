# Basic Ratio

## Overview
This pattern involves finding the ratio between two or more variables when given an equality or a proportional relationship. The central idea is to convert the given statement into an algebraic equation and isolate the ratio (e.g., $x/y$) by rearranging the terms.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "If ... is equal to ...", "find the ratio", "find x : y".
* **Given:** Equations involving percentages, decimals, or fractions of variables (e.g., "10% of x = 20% of y").
* **Asked:** The ratio of the variables (e.g., $x:y$ or $A:B:C$).
* **Format:** Often appears as $a \cdot x = b \cdot y$ or $a(x+y) = b(x-y)$.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Ratio from Equality]
$$
\frac{x}{y} = \frac{b}{a} \quad \text{if } ax = by
$$
**Variables:** $a, b$ are coefficients; $x, y$ are variables.
**When to use:** When you have a simple equality between two terms.
**Worked example:** If $10x = 20y$, then $\frac{x}{y} = \frac{20}{10} = \frac{2}{1}$, so $x:y = 2:1$.

### [Product of Means and Extremes]
$$
\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d} \iff ad = bc
$$
**Variables:** $a, b, c, d$ are terms in a proportion.
**When to use:** When given a proportion like $7:x = 17.5:22.5$.
**Worked example:** $7:x = 17.5:22.5 \implies 17.5x = 7 \times 22.5 \implies x = \frac{157.5}{17.5} = 9$.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Convert to Equation:** Write the given statement as a mathematical equation (e.g., replace "of" with $\times$ and percentages with fractions).
2. **Simplify Coefficients:** Divide both sides by common factors or multiply to clear denominators/decimals.
3. **Group Variables:** Move all terms containing one variable to one side and the other variable to the opposite side.
4. **Isolate Ratio:** Divide to form the fraction $\frac{x}{y}$ (or $\frac{A}{B}$), ensuring the coefficients are on the opposite side.
5. **Final Ratio:** Express the resulting fraction as a colon-separated ratio.

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** For $ax = by = cz$, the ratio $x:y:z$ is $\frac{1}{a} : \frac{1}{b} : \frac{1}{c}$.
* **Why it works:** To keep the products equal, the variables must be inversely proportional to their coefficients.
* **When to use:** When you have three or more variables equated (e.g., $30\%A = 0.25B = \frac{1}{5}C$).
* **Example:** $30\%A = 25\%B = 20\%C \implies \frac{3}{10}A = \frac{1}{4}B = \frac{1}{5}C$. Ratio is $\frac{10}{3} : 4 : 5 \implies 10:12:15$.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Inverting the ratio (writing $1:2$ instead of $2:1$).
    * **Why it happens:** Rushing the final step of $\frac{x}{y} = \frac{2}{1}$.
    * **Fix:** Always write $\frac{x}{y} = \dots$ before writing the colon ratio.
* **Mistake:** Sign errors during expansion (e.g., $2(P-Q) = 2P-Q$).
    * **Why it happens:** Forgetting to distribute the multiplier to the second term inside the bracket.
    * **Fix:** Use parentheses and expand carefully.
* **Mistake:** Forgetting to divide by the common variable in expressions like $(4m+5n):(4m-5n)$.
    * **Why it happens:** Trying to solve for $m$ and $n$ individually instead of the ratio.
    * **Fix:** Divide numerator and denominator by $n$ to get terms of $\frac{m}{n}$.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** If 20% of (P + Q) = 50% of (P – Q), then find P : Q.

**Solution:**
1. **Convert:** $\frac{20}{100}(P+Q) = \frac{50}{100}(P-Q)$.
2. **Simplify:** Divide by $\frac{1}{100}$ and then by $10$: $2(P+Q) = 5(P-Q)$.
3. **Expand:** $2P + 2Q = 5P - 5Q$.
4. **Group:** $2Q + 5Q = 5P - 2P \implies 7Q = 3P$.
5. **Isolate:** $\frac{P}{Q} = \frac{7}{3}$.

**Answer:** $P:Q = 7:3$.

---

## Similar Patterns
**Algebraic Substitution:** If the question asks for the value of an expression (e.g., $4m+5n$) rather than just the ratio, you must substitute the ratio values into the expression.

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $ax = by \implies x/y = b/a$.
* **Spot it by:** Equality signs between percentage/fractional parts of variables.
* **First move:** Convert the sentence into a clean algebraic equation.
* **Fastest method:** Use the reciprocal trick for multi-variable equalities.
* **Biggest trap:** Inverting the final ratio (writing $1:2$ instead of $2:1$).

---

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Percentage to Fraction:** $x\% = \frac{x}{100}$.
* **Cross-Multiplication:** If $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}$, then $ad = bc$.
* **Quadratic Factoring:** Splitting the middle term to solve $ax^2 + bxy + cy^2 = 0$.

### Formulas You Must Know First
* **Distributive Property:** $a(b+c) = ab + ac$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Antecedent:** The first term of a ratio.
* **Consequent:** The second term of a ratio.
* **Proportion:** An equality between two ratios.
