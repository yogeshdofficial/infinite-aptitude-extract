# Proportionality

## Overview
This pattern involves finding an unknown value when given a set of numbers in a specific ratio relationship. The central idea is the property of proportions: for any four numbers $a, b, c, d$ in proportion ($a:b :: c:d$), the product of the extremes equals the product of the means ($ad = bc$).

---

## Recognition Clues
*   **Keywords:** "Fourth proportional to...", "Third proportional to...", "Mean proportional between...".
*   **Given:** A set of 2 or 3 numbers.
*   **Asked:** The missing value that completes the ratio sequence.
*   **Structure:** Look for the colon notation ($a:b :: c:d$) or the word "proportional" in the problem statement.

---

## Key Formulas

### [Fourth Proportional]
$$
x = \frac{bc}{a}
$$
**Variables:** $a, b, c$ are the given numbers in order; $x$ is the fourth proportional.
**When to use:** When given three numbers $a, b, c$ and asked for the fourth.
**Worked example:** For 4, 9, 12: $x = \frac{9 \times 12}{4} = 27$.

### [Third Proportional]
$$
x = \frac{b^2}{a}
$$
**Variables:** $a, b$ are the given numbers; $x$ is the third proportional.
**When to use:** When given two numbers $a, b$ and asked for the third (implies $a:b :: b:x$).
**Worked example:** For 16 and 36: $x = \frac{36^2}{16} = \frac{1296}{16} = 81$.

### [Mean Proportional]
$$
x = \sqrt{ab}
$$
**Variables:** $a, b$ are the given numbers; $x$ is the mean proportional.
**When to use:** When asked for the mean proportional between two numbers.
**Worked example:** For 0.08 and 0.18: $x = \sqrt{0.08 \times 0.18} = \sqrt{0.0144} = 0.12$.

---

## Solution Framework
1.  **Identify the type:** Determine if you need the 4th, 3rd, or mean proportional.
2.  **Set up the equation:** Write the ratio (e.g., $a:b :: c:x$ for 4th, $a:b :: b:x$ for 3rd).
3.  **Apply the product rule:** Set the product of extremes equal to the product of means ($ad = bc$).
4.  **Solve for $x$:** Isolate $x$ using basic algebra.
5.  **Simplify:** Reduce fractions or simplify square roots to reach the final answer.

---

## Shortcut Tricks
*   **Trick:** For mean proportional, convert decimals to fractions first.
*   **Why it works:** It avoids errors in decimal placement and makes square root extraction obvious.
*   **When to use:** When numbers are decimals like 0.08 and 0.18.
*   **Example:** $\sqrt{0.08 \times 0.18} = \sqrt{\frac{8}{100} \times \frac{18}{100}} = \sqrt{\frac{144}{10000}} = \frac{12}{100} = 0.12$.

---

## Common Mistakes
*   **Mistake:** Confusing 3rd and 4th proportional formulas.
    *   **Fix:** Remember 3rd proportional uses the second number twice ($a:b :: b:x$).
*   **Mistake:** Incorrectly setting up the ratio order.
    *   **Fix:** Always write the numbers in the exact order given in the question.
*   **Mistake:** Arithmetic errors in squaring or square roots.
    *   **Fix:** Factorize large numbers into squares before taking the root.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** Find the third proportional to 16 and 36.

**Solution:**
1.  **Identify:** We need the third proportional to 16 and 36.
2.  **Set up:** $16:36 :: 36:x$.
3.  **Product rule:** $16 \times x = 36 \times 36$.
4.  **Solve:** $x = \frac{36 \times 36}{16}$.
5.  **Simplify:** $x = \frac{1296}{16} = 81$.

**Answer:** 81

---

## Similar Patterns
*   **Variation Problems:** These involve $x \propto y$ or $x \propto \frac{1}{y}$. Distinguish by looking for the words "directly proportional" or "inversely proportional" instead of just "proportional to".

---

## Revision Summary
*   **Key formula:** $ad=bc$ for proportions; $\sqrt{ab}$ for mean.
*   **Spot it by:** Looking for "proportional" and the count of numbers provided.
*   **First move:** Write the ratio $a:b :: c:d$ or $a:b :: b:x$.
*   **Fastest method:** Use the product of means = product of extremes.
*   **Biggest trap:** Swapping the order of numbers or using the wrong formula for 3rd vs 4th.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
*   **Ratio** — A comparison of two quantities by division, written as $a:b$ or $\frac{a}{b}$.
*   **Proportion** — The equality of two ratios, written as $a:b :: c:d$.
*   **Square Root** — A value that, when multiplied by itself, gives the original number.

### Formulas You Must Know First
*   **Product of Means/Extremes:** $a:b :: c:d \implies ad = bc$.
    *   **What it means:** The outer terms multiplied equal the inner terms multiplied.
    *   **Example:** $2:4 :: 3:6 \implies 2 \times 6 = 4 \times 3 \implies 12 = 12$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
*   **Extremes** — The first and last terms ($a$ and $d$) in a proportion $a:b :: c:d$.
*   **Means** — The middle terms ($b$ and $c$) in a proportion $a:b :: c:d$.
