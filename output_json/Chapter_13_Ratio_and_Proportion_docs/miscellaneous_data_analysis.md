# Miscellaneous Data Analysis

## Overview
This pattern involves solving word problems that require translating descriptive text into a system of linear equations or ratios. You will recognize these by the presence of multiple variables (like items and costs) or linked ratios that must be unified to find a specific share or quantity.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Total cost," "divided between," "ratio of," "times that of," "sum of."
* **Given:** A total value and relationships between quantities (e.g., $A$ is $1.5$ times $B$).
* **Asked:** A specific share, a ratio of two items, or a value expressed in terms of another variable.
* **Structure:** Look for "Total = (Price $\times$ Quantity) + (Price $\times$ Quantity)" or "Chain ratios" (A:B and B:C).

---

## Key Formulas

### [Total Expenditure]
$$
\text{Total Cost} = (P_1 \times Q_1) + (P_2 \times Q_2)
$$
**Variables:** $P$ = Price per unit, $Q$ = Quantity of items.
**When to use:** When you have a total budget and different unit costs for two types of items.
**Worked example:** $50(2) + 40(10) = 100 + 400 = 500$.

### [Share Allocation]
$$
\text{Share of } X = \frac{\text{Ratio}_X}{\sum \text{Ratio}} \times \text{Total Amount}
$$
**Variables:** $\text{Ratio}_X$ = The part of the ratio belonging to $X$, $\sum \text{Ratio}$ = Sum of all parts.
**When to use:** When dividing a total sum among multiple people based on a ratio.
**Worked example:** For $A:B:C = 21:14:8$, C's share = $\frac{8}{43} \times 1290 = 240$.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Define Variables:** Assign $x$ to the unknown quantity or use the given ratios to express all items in terms of one variable.
2. **Translate to Equation:** Convert the word problem into a mathematical equation (e.g., sum of costs = total).
3. **Solve for Variable:** Perform algebraic operations to isolate the variable.
4. **Calculate Required Value:** Substitute the variable back to find the specific quantity or share requested.
5. **Verify Units:** Ensure the final answer matches the requested format (e.g., ratio vs. currency).

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** Use the "Chain Ratio" method for $A:B$ and $B:C$.
* **Why it works:** It eliminates the middle variable ($B$) by creating a common denominator.
* **When to use:** When given $A = mB$ and $B = nC$.
* **Example:** If $A = \frac{3}{2}B$ and $B = \frac{7}{4}C$, then $A = \frac{3}{2} \times \frac{7}{4}C = \frac{21}{8}C$. Ratio $A:B:C = \frac{21}{8} : \frac{7}{4} : 1 \Rightarrow 21:14:8$.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Mistake:** Inverting the ratio (e.g., writing tables:chairs instead of chairs:tables).
    * **Fix:** Always re-read the final sentence of the question to confirm the order.
* **Mistake:** Arithmetic errors in mixed fraction conversion.
    * **Fix:** Convert $a\frac{b}{c}$ to $\frac{ac+b}{c}$ before starting calculations.
* **Mistake:** Forgetting to sum the ratio parts.
    * **Fix:** Always add all parts of the ratio before calculating the individual share.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** A sum of ₹ 1290 is divided between A, B and C such that A’s share is $1\frac{1}{2}$ times that of B and B’s share is $1\frac{3}{4}$ times that of C. What is C’s share?

**Solution:**
1. **Define Variables:** $A = \frac{3}{2}B$ and $B = \frac{7}{4}C$.
2. **Translate to Equation:** Express $A$ in terms of $C$: $A = \frac{3}{2}(\frac{7}{4}C) = \frac{21}{8}C$.
3. **Solve for Ratio:** $A:B:C = \frac{21}{8} : \frac{7}{4} : 1$. Multiply by 8: $21:14:8$.
4. **Calculate Share:** Sum of parts = $21+14+8 = 43$. C's share = $\frac{8}{43} \times 1290$.
5. **Final Result:** $8 \times 30 = 240$.

**Answer:** ₹ 240.

## Similar Patterns
**Partnership Problems:** These are similar but involve time as a third variable ($Investment \times Time$). Distinguish by checking if "time" is mentioned as a factor for profit distribution.

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $\text{Share} = (\text{Ratio Part} / \text{Total Ratio}) \times \text{Total Amount}$.
* **Spot it by:** Looking for "times that of" or "total cost" phrases.
* **First move:** Convert all relationships into a single common ratio or variable.
* **Fastest method:** Use the chain ratio method to link variables.
* **Biggest trap:** Reversing the order of the requested ratio.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Algebraic Substitution** — Replacing a variable with an equivalent expression.
* **Ratio Unification** — Combining two ratios $A:B$ and $B:C$ into $A:B:C$.
* **Fraction Arithmetic** — Converting mixed fractions to improper fractions.

### Formulas You Must Know First
$$
\text{Total} = \text{Part}_1 + \text{Part}_2 + \dots + \text{Part}_n
$$
**What it means:** The sum of individual shares equals the total amount.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Antecedent** — The first term in a ratio.
* **Consequent** — The second term in a ratio.
* **Proportion** — The equality of two ratios.
