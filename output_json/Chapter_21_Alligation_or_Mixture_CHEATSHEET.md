# Alligation Or Mixture — Exam Cheat Sheet

## How To Solve Any Question In This Chapter
1. **Identify the Goal:** Determine if you are finding a ratio, a missing price, a final quantity after replacement, or a profit-adjusted cost.
2. **Standardize Units:** Ensure all values are in the same unit (e.g., all in ₹/kg, all in % concentration, all in fraction of milk).
3. **Adjust for Profit:** If selling price and profit % are given, calculate $CP = \frac{100}{100+P} \times SP$ before starting alligation.
4. **Apply Alligation:** Place the two component values at the top corners and the mean value in the center. Subtract diagonally to find the ratio.
5. **Handle Replacements:** Use the power formula $Remaining = Initial \times (1 - \frac{removed}{total})^n$ for repeated operations.
6. **Sanity Check:** Ensure the mean value lies between the two component values; if not, the setup is inverted.

## Formula Bank

**[Alligation Basics]**
$$ \text{Ratio} = (\text{Price}_2 - \text{Mean}) : (\text{Mean} - \text{Price}_1) $$
→ *produces: ratio of first to second*
→ *use when: two prices and mean given*

**[Missing Component Price]**
$$ \text{Mean Price} = \frac{\sum (Q_i \times P_i)}{\sum Q_i} $$
→ *produces: weighted average price*
→ *use when: finding unknown component price*

**[Mixture Replacement]**
$$ \text{Remaining} = \text{Initial} \times \left( 1 - \frac{x}{V} \right)^n $$
→ *produces: quantity of original liquid left*
→ *use when: repeated replacement occurs*

**[Profit-Based Alligation]**
$$ \text{C.P. of mixture} = \frac{100}{100 + \text{Gain}\%} \times \text{S.P.} $$
→ *produces: true cost price*
→ *use when: selling price is provided*

**[Quantity Adjustment]**
$$ \text{Part} = \frac{\text{Ratio Part}}{\text{Total Ratio}} \times \text{Total Quantity} $$
→ *produces: specific component amount*
→ *use when: splitting total into ratios*

**[Vessel Mixing]**
$$ \text{Ratio} = |C_2 - C_{mean}| : |C_{mean} - C_1| $$
→ *produces: ratio of vessel contents*
→ *use when: mixing two different mixtures*

## Step Sequences

**Alligation Basics:** Identify prices → Subtract diagonally → Simplify ratio → Result.
**Missing Component Price:** Weight prices → Form equation → Solve for $x$ → Result.
**Mixture Replacement:** Calculate fraction remaining → Apply power $n$ → Multiply initial → Result.
**Profit-Based Alligation:** Convert SP to CP → Apply alligation → Simplify ratio → Result.
**Quantity Adjustment:** Identify total parts → Calculate unit value → Multiply by ratio → Result.
**Vessel Mixing:** Convert to single fraction → Apply alligation → Simplify ratio → Result.

---

## Fastest Tricks

* **Basics:** Convert all values to paise to eliminate decimal errors instantly.
* **Profit-Based:** If selling at CP, ratio of water to milk = $\frac{\text{Gain}\%}{100}$.
* **Replacement:** If $n$ operations, use the ratio of remaining liquid to total.
* **Vessel Mixing:** Use the fraction of the *cheaper* or *first* component only.

---

## Trap Watch

* **Basics:** Subtracting in wrong order → Check $P_1 < Mean < P_2$.
* **Replacement:** Confusing removed amount with remaining → Use $(1 - \frac{x}{V})$.
* **Profit-Based:** Using SP instead of CP → Always convert to CP first.
* **Vessel Mixing:** Using water fraction for one and milk for other → Standardize all to milk.

---

## Last-Minute Reminders

* The mean price must always be between the two individual prices.
* When replacing liquid, the total volume remains constant throughout the process.
* Always simplify the final ratio to its lowest terms to match options.
* If the question mentions "gain %", the cost price is always lower than the selling price.
* In vessel mixing, ensure all fractions represent the same component (e.g., all milk).
