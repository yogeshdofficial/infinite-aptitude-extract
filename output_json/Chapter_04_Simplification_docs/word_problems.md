# Word Problems (Linear & Quadratic Systems)

## Overview
These questions present a narrative involving multiple unknown quantities (like costs or counts) and their relationships. The central idea is to translate the narrative into a system of algebraic equations and solve for the variables using substitution or elimination.

---

## Recognition Clues
* **Keywords:** "Cost of...", "Total of...", "Greater than... by...", "Two varieties of...".
* **Given:** Two or more relationships between variables (e.g., $2x + 3y = 34$).
* **Asked:** The value of a specific variable ($x$) or a combination of variables ($5y + 7x$).
* **Structure:** Look for a set of items with different prices or counts that sum to a total value.

---

## Key Formulas

### Linear System Elimination
$$
a_1x + b_1y = c_1 \quad \text{and} \quad a_2x + b_2y = c_2
$$
**Variables:** $x, y$ = unknowns; $a, b, c$ = given coefficients/totals.
**When to use:** When you have two variables and two distinct linear relationships.
**Worked example:** For $4x+3y=555$ and $3x+4y=460$, multiply the first by 4 and second by 3 to align $y$ coefficients, then subtract to isolate $x$.

### Quadratic Factoring
$$
x^2 + bx + c = 0 \implies (x - p)(x - q) = 0
$$
**Variables:** $p, q$ = two numbers that multiply to $c$ and add to $b$.
**When to use:** When the equation contains an $x^2$ term.
**Worked example:** For $x^2-7x+12=0$, find two numbers that multiply to $12$ and add to $-7$ (which are $-3$ and $-4$), giving $(x-3)(x-4)=0$.

---

## Solution Framework

1. **Define Variables:** Assign $x, y, z$ to the unknown quantities mentioned in the narrative.
2. **Translate to Equations:** Convert each sentence of the narrative into a mathematical equation.
3. **Simplify:** Cross-multiply fractions or move all terms to one side to reach standard form.
4. **Eliminate/Substitute:** Use elimination (adding/subtracting equations) or substitution to reduce the number of variables.
5. **Solve:** Find the value of one variable, then back-substitute to find the others.
6. **Final Calculation:** Plug the found values into the specific expression requested (e.g., $5y+7x$).

---

## Shortcut Tricks

* **Trick:** If coefficients are "swapped" (e.g., $4x+3y$ and $3x+4y$), add and subtract the equations to get $(x+y)$ and $(x-y)$ immediately.
* **Why it works:** $(4x+3y) + (3x+4y) = 7x+7y = 7(x+y)$.
* **When to use:** When the coefficients of $x$ and $y$ in the first equation are the coefficients of $y$ and $x$ in the second.
* **Example:** $4x+3y=555$ and $3x+4y=460 \implies 7(x+y)=1015 \implies x+y=145$.

---

## Common Mistakes

* **Sign Errors:** Forgetting to change signs when moving a term across the equals sign (e.g., $x^2-7x=-12$ becomes $x^2-7x+12=0$).
* **Incomplete Substitution:** Finding $x$ but forgetting to solve for $y$ or the final requested expression.
* **Fractional Arithmetic:** Errors in finding the Least Common Multiple (LCM) when subtracting fractions.

---

## Worked Example (Step-by-Step)

**Question:** The cost of 4 bags and 3 boxes is ₹ 555 and the cost of 3 bags and 4 boxes is ₹ 460. What is the cost of one bag?

**Solution:**
1. **Define:** Let bag cost = $x$, box cost = $y$.
2. **Equations:** $4x + 3y = 555$ (i); $3x + 4y = 460$ (ii).
3. **Eliminate:** Multiply (i) by 4 $\to 16x + 12y = 2220$. Multiply (ii) by 3 $\to 9x + 12y = 1380$.
4. **Subtract:** $(16x - 9x) + (12y - 12y) = 2220 - 1380 \implies 7x = 840$.
5. **Solve:** $x = 120$.

**Answer:** The cost of one bag is ₹ 120.

## Similar Patterns
* **Ratio/Proportion Problems:** These involve relationships like $x:y = a:b$ rather than fixed sums. Distinguish by looking for "ratio" or "parts" keywords.

---

## Revision Summary
* **Key formula:** $a_1x + b_1y = c_1$ (Linear system).
* **Spot it by:** Narrative describing costs or counts of multiple items.
* **First move:** Assign variables and write the equations.
* **Fastest method:** Use the "swapped coefficient" trick (add/subtract equations).
* **Biggest trap:** Forgetting to change signs when moving terms across the equals sign.

## Appendix: Prerequisites

### Concepts You Must Know
* **Linear Equation** — An equation where the highest power of the variable is 1.
* **Quadratic Equation** — An equation of the form $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$.
* **Substitution** — Replacing a variable with an equivalent expression derived from another equation.

### Formulas You Must Know First
* **Distributive Property:** $a(b+c) = ab + ac$.
* **Fractional Addition:** $\frac{a}{b} + \frac{c}{d} = \frac{ad+bc}{bd}$.

### Terms Used In This Pattern
* **Variable:** A letter representing an unknown number.
* **Coefficient:** The number multiplying a variable (e.g., 4 in $4x$).
* **Elimination:** A method to remove a variable by adding or subtracting equations.
