# Chapter 12: Profit and Loss

## 1. Chapter Introduction
This chapter introduces the fundamental financial mathematics used to evaluate business transactions. Understanding Profit and Loss is essential for calculating the financial outcome of buying and selling goods. Whether you are analyzing simple retail transactions or complex scenarios involving dishonest weights and multi-step price adjustments, these concepts provide the tools to determine the efficiency and profitability of any trade.

## 2. Core Ideas
At its heart, this chapter revolves around two primary values:
*   **Cost Price (C.P.):** The amount spent to purchase an article.
*   **Selling Price (S.P.):** The amount received when an article is sold.

Profit occurs when the S.P. exceeds the C.P., while a loss occurs when the S.P. is lower than the C.P. All percentage gains or losses are calculated relative to the Cost Price. The chapter also explores advanced scenarios, such as how traders use false weights to manipulate profit margins and how to adjust prices when production costs change.

## 3. Important Formulas
Use these formulas to calculate the financial outcome of a transaction:

*   **Gain/Loss Amount:**
    $$\text{Gain} = \text{S.P.} - \text{C.P.}$$
    $$\text{Loss} = \text{C.P.} - \text{S.P.}$$

*   **Percentage Calculation:**
    $$\text{Gain \%} = \left( \frac{\text{Gain}}{\text{C.P.}} \times 100 \right)$$
    $$\text{Loss \%} = \left( \frac{\text{Loss}}{\text{C.P.}} \times 100 \right)$$

*   **Finding S.P. from C.P.:**
    $$\text{S.P.} = \text{C.P.} \times \frac{(100 + \text{Gain \%})}{100}$$
    $$\text{S.P.} = \text{C.P.} \times \frac{(100 - \text{Loss \%})}{100}$$

*   **Finding C.P. from S.P.:**
    $$\text{C.P.} = \text{S.P.} \times \frac{100}{(100 + \text{Gain \%})}$$
    $$\text{C.P.} = \text{S.P.} \times \frac{100}{(100 - \text{Loss \%})}$$

## 4. Important Facts and Shortcuts
*   **Percentage Shortcut:** If an item is sold at a 35% gain, the S.P. is simply 135% of the C.P. If sold at a 35% loss, the S.P. is 65% of the C.P.
*   **Two-Item Rule:** When selling two similar items where one results in an $x\%$ gain and the other in an $x\%$ loss, the seller always incurs a loss calculated as:
    $$\text{Loss \%} = \left( \frac{x}{10} \right)^2$$
*   **False Weights:** When a trader uses false weights, the gain percentage is calculated based on the error relative to the true value minus the error.

## 5. How to Recognize the Problem Type
*   **Basic Calculation:** Look for explicit C.P. and S.P. values.
*   **Finding Price:** If the question asks for the "Cost Price" or "New Selling Price" given a percentage, use the conversion formulas.
*   **Dishonest Dealer:** Look for keywords like "false weights," "professes to sell at cost price," or "weight is $y\%$ less than actual."
*   **Multi-Step:** If a question mentions a change in production cost or a secondary profit goal, solve in two distinct stages (find the initial C.P. first, then apply the new conditions).

## 6. Common Mistakes
1.  **Calculating on S.P.:** Always remember that gain or loss is reckoned on the **C.P.**, not the S.P.
2.  **Mixing up Formulas:** Confusing the multiplier for gain (100 + %) versus loss (100 - %).
3.  **Ignoring the Base:** In multi-step problems, failing to calculate the correct C.P. before moving to the second stage of the transaction.
4.  **Misinterpreting False Weights:** Incorrectly applying the error percentage in the denominator of the dishonest dealer formula.

## 7. Chapter Pattern Map
This chapter covers a wide range of scenarios, including:
*   **Standard Percentage Calculations:** Basic profit/loss identification.
*   **Price Adjustments:** Finding C.P. or S.P. from percentage changes.
*   **Dishonest Dealer Scenarios:** Calculating gains when weights are manipulated.
*   **Multi-Step Adjustments:** Handling changes in production costs or multiple profit targets.
*   **Quantity-Based Equivalence:** Managing profit/loss across multiple items.
