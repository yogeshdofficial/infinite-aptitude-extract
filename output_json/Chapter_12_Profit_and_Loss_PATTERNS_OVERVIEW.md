# Profit And Loss

## What This Chapter Is About

This chapter deals with the financial mechanics of buying and selling goods, focusing on the relationship between the cost incurred and the revenue generated. All problems revolve around the Cost Price (CP) and Selling Price (SP) and the resulting percentage change in value.

---

## Key Terms

**Cost Price (CP)** — The total amount spent to acquire an article.

**Selling Price (SP)** — The amount received after selling an article.

**Profit (Gain)** — The surplus earned when `SP > CP`, calculated as `SP - CP`.

**Loss** — The deficit incurred when `SP < CP`, calculated as `CP - SP`.

**Marked Price (MP)** — The price labeled on an article, from which discounts are often subtracted to reach the SP.

---

## Core Formulas

### [Profit/Loss Percentage]

$$
\text{Profit \%} = \left( \frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{CP}} \right) \times 100, \quad \text{Loss \%} = \left( \frac{\text{Loss}}{\text{CP}} \right) \times 100
$$

*When to use:* To determine the relative gain or loss regardless of the absolute currency value.

*Variables:* CP = Cost Price; Profit/Loss = Absolute difference between SP and CP.

### [Calculating SP from CP]

$$
\text{SP} = \text{CP} \times \left( \frac{100 \pm \text{Profit/Loss \%}}{100} \right)
$$

*When to use:* When you know the cost price and the percentage change, and need to find the final selling price.

*Variables:* CP = Cost Price; Profit/Loss % = The percentage rate of gain or loss.

### [Calculating CP from SP]

$$
\text{CP} = \text{SP} \times \left( \frac{100}{100 \pm \text{Profit/Loss \%}} \right)
$$

*When to use:* When you know the final selling price and the percentage change, and need to find the original cost.

*Variables:* SP = Selling Price; Profit/Loss % = The percentage rate of gain or loss.

## How Patterns Connect

**Break-Even Analysis** — Questions where the goal is to find the point where there is no profit and no loss.

**CP SP Ratio** — Problems where the relationship between cost and selling price is expressed as a ratio.

**Cost Structure Changes** — Scenarios where the production or purchase cost changes, requiring a new SP calculation.

**Data Interpretation Profit** — Problems involving the analysis of profit/loss data presented in tables or charts.

**Depreciation Calculation** — Questions involving the reduction in value of an asset over time.

**Equivalent CP SP** — Problems where different items are equated based on their cost or selling prices.

**False Weights** — Situations where a trader uses dishonest scales to manipulate profit margins.

**Find CP** — Direct calculation of the original cost price given the SP and profit/loss percentage.

**Find SP** — Direct calculation of the final selling price given the CP and profit/loss percentage.

**Marked Price Discount** — Problems involving the transition from Marked Price to Selling Price via discounts.

**Mixture Problems** — Questions involving the blending of two or more items with different costs to achieve a target price.

**Multiple Item Transactions** — Problems involving the cumulative profit or loss of several different items.

**Price Change Impact** — Scenarios where a change in price affects the overall profit or loss percentage.

**Profit Distribution** — Problems involving the division of total profit among partners or items.

**Profit Loss Percent** — Basic calculation of the percentage gain or loss from absolute values.

**Rate-Based Transactions** — Problems where items are bought or sold in quantities (e.g., "x items for y rupees").

**Relative Price Calculation** — Problems where prices are compared relative to one another rather than absolute values.

**Same SP Different %** — Scenarios where two items are sold at the same price but result in different profit/loss percentages.

**Successive Discounts** — Problems involving multiple percentage reductions applied one after another on the Marked Price.

**Successive Sales** — Problems where an item is sold multiple times through a chain of sellers.

**Two-Stage Price Change** — Problems where a price is adjusted twice before reaching the final selling price.

**Uncategorized** — Miscellaneous problems that do not fit into standard categories.

**Weighted Profit Loss** — Problems where different quantities of items are sold at different profit/loss rates.

## Common Traps (Chapter-Wide)

* **Base Confusion:** Always calculate profit or loss percentage on the **Cost Price (CP)**, not the Selling Price, unless explicitly stated otherwise.
* **Percentage vs. Absolute Value:** Confusing a percentage change with an absolute currency value; always convert percentages to decimals or fractions before applying them to the base price.
* **False Weight Logic:** In false weight problems, the "gain" is calculated on the weight actually sold (the false weight), not the weight the customer expects to receive.
